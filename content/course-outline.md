## Curriculum Structure: Hybrid (Practice Priority × Logical Dependency)

*Version 1.0 — Unit Level*

---

## How This Outline Works

- **Modules** = Major learning areas (think "courses" in Duolingo)
- **Units** = Focused learning chunks within a module (think "skills" in Duolingo)
- **Each unit** will eventually contain 10-20 concept cards, each generating 3-5 questions across multiple exercise types
- **Prerequisites** are enforced — a module/unit cannot be started until its dependencies are completed
- **Three tiers per unit** — Know It → Understand It → Apply It (unlocked sequentially)
- **Estimated lesson count** assumes 5 questions per lesson, 3-5 lessons per tier per unit

## Module 0: Orientation — What Just Changed and Why

> *Purpose: Before learning any specific provision, the CA must understand the big picture — what this Act is, why it exists, and how to navigate it. This is the "onboarding" module that every user completes first.*

> *Prerequisites: None*

> *Priority: CRITICAL — must complete before any other module*

### Unit 0.1: Why a New Act?
- The problem with the 1961 Act (complexity, amendments, bloat)
- The simplification mandate (Budget 2024 announcement)
- What this Act is NOT (no new taxes, no rate changes, no policy overhaul)
- The Select Committee process (285 recommendations, 84 substantive)
- Timeline: Bill → Committee → Withdrawal → Revised bill → Assent → Effective date

### Unit 0.2: The New Structure at a Glance
- 819 sections → 536 sections
- 5.12 lakh words → 2.6 lakh words
- 47 chapters → 23 chapters
- 14 schedules → 16 schedules
- ~1,200 provisos eliminated
- ~900 explanations converted to sub-sections
- 39 new tables introduced
- How the new numbering system works (sequential, no alphabets like 80CCC)

### Unit 0.3: How to Navigate the New Act
- The table-based format (TDS tables, perquisite tables, presumptive tax tables)
- Section 10's transformation: 140 clauses → Schedules II-VII
- Cross-referencing between sections and schedules
- The parallel reading utility on incometax.gov.in
- Practical tip: building your own quick-reference mapping

### Unit 0.4: What's Gone
- Redundant sections removed (list of key ones and why they were redundant)
- Omitted provisions
- Sections that existed only on paper (e.g., Section 10A — redundant since FY 2012-13)
- Concepts that no longer exist in their old form

## Module 1: The Foundation — Tax Year & Residential Status

> *Purpose: These two concepts underpin everything else. Tax Year replaces the most fundamental terminology CAs have used for decades. Residential Status determines the entire scope of taxable income. Nothing else makes sense without these.*

> *Prerequisites: Module 0*

> *Priority: CRITICAL — unlock all subsequent modules*

### Unit 1.1: Tax Year (Replaces Previous Year + Assessment Year)
- Definition of "Tax Year" (Sec 3)
- How it maps to the old "Previous Year"
- Elimination of "Assessment Year" concept
- What CAs now say/write instead of "AY 2027-28"
- Tax Year for newly set up businesses (mid-year commencement)
- Transition: FY 2025-26 under old Act → Tax Year 2026-27 under new Act
- No "missing year" — clean handover
- Section 536(3): reference to tax year = reference to corresponding previous year

### Unit 1.2: Residential Status
- Sec 6 → New Sec 6 mapping
- Resident, Non-Resident, RNOR conditions
- The 182-day and 60-day rules
- Key language change: "for the purpose of employment outside India" → "for employment outside India" — and what this means practically
- Deemed residence provisions
- Impact on NRIs, returning Indians, crew members
- How residential status determines scope of total income (Sec 5)

### Unit 1.3: Scope of Total Income
- Sec 5 → New Sec 5 mapping
- Income received/deemed to be received in India
- Income accruing/arising/deemed to accrue or arise in India
- Business connection changes: "reasonably attributable" → "attributable"
- Significant economic presence (export exclusion change)
- Income deemed to accrue in India (Sec 9 changes)

## Module 2: The Transition Bridge

> *Purpose: For the next 1-2 years, CAs must operate under BOTH Acts simultaneously. This module covers the practical reality of transition — what runs under old law, what runs under new, and what happens to things in progress.*

> *Prerequisites: Module 1 (Units 1.1 and 1.2)*

> *Priority: CRITICAL — every CA faces this on Day 1 (April 1, 2026)*

### Unit 2.1: The Parallel Operation
- Old Act governs: all income up to March 31, 2026
- New Act governs: all income from April 1, 2026
- Both Acts operating simultaneously during transition
- Which Act applies to which return filing
- E-filing portal handling both regimes

### Unit 2.2: Pending Proceedings
- Assessments in progress → continue under old Act
- Appeals pending → old Act
- Reassessments → old Act
- Prosecutions → old Act
- Key principle: the new Act does not go back in time

### Unit 2.3: Carry-Forward & Continuity
- Carry-forward of losses under old Act → honored under new Act
- MAT credit carry-forward
- Depreciation carry-forward
- Deductions claimed under old Act (existing investments under 80C etc.)
- Registrations and approvals (Sec 12A, 80G approvals) — continuity provisions

### Unit 2.4: TDS During Transition
- TDS on payments before April 1, 2026 → old sections
- TDS on payments from April 1, 2026 → new sections
- What changes on TDS certificates
- Form number changes
- Compliance calendar during transition year

### Unit 2.5: Return Filing During Transition
- Return for AY 2026-27 (income of FY 2025-26) → filed under OLD Act in July 2026
- Return for Tax Year 2026-27 (income from April 2026) → filed under NEW Act in 2027
- No double filing required
- Updated return concept (Sec 267)
- Self-assessment changes
