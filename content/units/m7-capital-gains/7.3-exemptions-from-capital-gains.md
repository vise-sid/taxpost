# Unit 7.3: Exemptions from Capital Gains (Sections 82-91)

## Overview

The capital gains exemption framework — rollover relief for residential house, agricultural land, bonds, and industrial/SEZ relocation — moves from Sections 54-55A to Sections 82-91. The genuine 2025 Act changes here: **two exemptions dropped** (Sections 54EE and 54GB — both sunset-expired, not re-enacted), **CGAS accepts electronic deposits**, and a **new claw-back provision** under Section 536(2)(h) for post-April 2026 violations of lock-in periods on exemptions claimed under the old Act.

| 54 | **82** | Exemption — LTCG from residential house, reinvested in residential house | Renumbered only |
| 54B | **83** | Exemption — capital gains from agricultural land, reinvested in agricultural land | Renumbered only |
| 54D | **84** | Exemption — compulsory acquisition of industrial land/building, reinvested | Renumbered only |
| 54EC | **85** | Exemption — LTCG from land/building, invested in specified bonds | Renumbered only |

## Exemptions Dropped — Sections 54EE and 54GB

These are genuine 2025 Act changes — not because the policy changed, but because the 2025 Act chose not to re-enact dead provisions.

| Old Section | Subject | Sunset Date | Why Not Re-Enacted |
|---|---|---|---|
| **54EE** | LTCG invested in specified fund (notified fund investing in startups) — max Rs 50 lakh, 3-year lock-in | **31 March 2021** | The fund was never notified. Dead letter provision from inception. |
| **54GB** | LTCG on residential property, invested in equity of eligible startup — max Rs 50 lakh | **31 March 2025** | Sunset expired. With the 2025 Act effective 1 April 2026, no transition gap. |

> **No action required** for ongoing assessments — these exemptions were already unavailable for transfers after their respective sunset dates under the 1961 Act.

**Bottom line: Remove 54EE and 54GB from your exemption checklists. They were dead before the 2025 Act — now they are formally gone.**

---

## Sections 82-89: The Renumbered Suite

All exemption sections are renumbered without substantive change. The conditions, time limits, caps, and lock-in periods are identical to the 1961 Act position as it stood. A quick reference:

| New Section | Old Section | Asset Transferred | Reinvestment In | Time Limit | Key Condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **82** | 54 | Residential house (LTCG) | Residential house | 1 yr before / 2 yrs after purchase; 3 yrs construct | Individuals/HUFs only. Two-house option if LTCG ≤ Rs 2 crore (once in lifetime). |
| **83** | 54B | Agricultural land | Agricultural land | 2 yrs after transfer | Land used for agriculture for 2 years preceding transfer. |
| **84** | 54D | Industrial land/building (compulsory acquisition) | Land/building for shifting/re-establishing undertaking | 3 yrs after acquisition | — |
| **85** | 54EC | Land/building (LTCG) | Specified bonds (NHAI/REC/PFC/IRFC) | 6 months after transfer | Max Rs 50 lakh per FY. 5-year lock-in. Any assessee. |
| **86** | 54F | Any LTCA other than residential house | Residential house (net consideration) | 1 yr before / 2 yrs after purchase; 3 yrs construct | Individuals/HUFs only. Must not own >1 other house on date of transfer. |
| **87** | 54G | Assets of industrial undertaking in urban area | Similar assets in non-urban area | 1 yr before / 3 yrs after | — |
| **88** | 54GA | Assets of industrial undertaking in urban area | Similar assets in SEZ | 1 yr before / 3 yrs after | — |
| **89** | 54H | Foreign asset (NRI) | New asset in India | Extended time as specified | — |

> The Rs 10 crore cap on Sections 82 and 86 was introduced by Finance Act 2023 (effective AY 2024-25). It is now part of the base statute — not a 2025 Act change.

**Bottom line: Same exemptions, new numbers. Update your section references in opinions and computation sheets. 54→82, 54B→83, 54D→84, 54EC→85, 54F→86, 54G→87, 54GA→88, 54H→89.**

## Section 90: Cost of Acquisition and Cost of Improvement (Old Section 55)

Definitions of cost of acquisition and cost of improvement for various asset categories. Key rules unchanged:

- Goodwill: purchase price (self-generated = nil for acquisitions after 1 April 2020)
- Assets acquired before 1 April 2001: FMV as on 1 April 2001 may be substituted
- Cost of improvement: only expenditure on or after 1 April 2001 qualifies

**Renumbered only.**

---

## Section 91: Reference to Valuation Officer (Old Section 55A)

Circumstances permitting a reference to the Valuation Officer for capital asset valuation are unchanged.

**Renumbered only.**

---

## Capital Gains Account Scheme — Electronic Deposits

The CGAS continues under the 2025 Act. The scheme allows deposit of unutilised capital gains before the return-filing due date to secure exemption under Sections 82-88.

**What is new:** The scheme now accepts **electronic deposits**. Assessees can transfer funds electronically to the Capital Gains Account without physical deposit receipts. Account types (Type A — savings, Type B — term deposit) and designated banks remain the same.

**Bottom line: CGAS goes digital. Inform clients they can now make electronic transfers to their Capital Gains Account.**

---

## Section 536(2)(h): Claw-Back for Post-April 2026 Lock-In Violations

This is a genuine 2025 Act transition provision. It addresses a specific scenario: what happens when an assessee claimed an exemption under the 1961 Act (old Sections 54/54B/54D/54EC/54F/54G/54GA) and violates the lock-in period **after 1 April 2026** (when the 2025 Act takes effect)?

**The rule:** If the exempt asset is sold within the lock-in period and the sale occurs on or after 1 April 2026, the original exemption is withdrawn and the capital gains become taxable — computed under the **2025 Act's** provisions (new section numbers, new rates as applicable).

**Why this matters:** A client who claimed Section 54 exemption in AY 2025-26 (transfer in FY 2024-25) and purchased a house with a 3-year lock-in ending in FY 2027-28 — if they sell the new house in FY 2026-27 or FY 2027-28 (post-April 2026), the claw-back is governed by the 2025 Act, not the 1961 Act.

> **Watch out:** The rates and computation rules applicable to the claw-back are those of the 2025 Act, which may differ from what the client expected when claiming the original exemption. Always flag this for clients who are within lock-in periods straddling 1 April 2026.

**Bottom line: Lock-in violations after 1 April 2026 are governed by the 2025 Act, even if the original exemption was claimed under the 1961 Act. Review all clients currently in lock-in windows.**

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 54-55A → Sections 82-91.** Ten sections renumbered. Core exemption framework preserved.
- **Sections 54EE and 54GB dropped.** Both sunset-expired — 54EE was never operative (fund never notified), 54GB expired 31 March 2025.
- **CGAS now accepts electronic deposits.** Same account types, same banks, new digital channel.
- **Section 536(2)(h):** Claw-back provision for lock-in violations occurring on or after 1 April 2026 — taxable under 2025 Act provisions, even if the original exemption was claimed under the 1961 Act.
- **All exemption conditions, time limits, caps, and lock-in periods are unchanged** by the 2025 Act. The Rs 10 crore cap (Sections 82 and 86) was Finance Act 2023.
