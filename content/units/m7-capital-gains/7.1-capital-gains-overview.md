# Unit 7.1: Capital Gains Overview (Sections 67-72)

## Overview

The capital gains charging, definitions, transfer exclusions, and computation framework moves from Sections 45-48 to Sections 67-72. The core logic is preserved. The genuine 2025 Act changes here are narrow: two obsolete transfer exemptions dropped from Section 70 (BIFR and demutualisation — both functionally dead), and four definition clauses remapped under Section 2. Everything else is renumbering.

> Rates, indexation provisions, and grandfathering rules as amended by the Finance (No.2) Act 2024 are carried forward into the 2025 Act — no further change by the 2025 Act.

---

## Old → New Section Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 45 | **67** | Charging section — capital gains taxable in the year of transfer | Renumbered only |
| 46 | **68** | Capital gains on distribution of assets by companies in liquidation | Renumbered only |
| 46A | **69** | Capital gains on insurance compensation for destruction of capital asset | Renumbered only |
| 47 | **70** | Transactions not regarded as transfer | **2 obsolete exemptions dropped** |
| 47A | **71** | Conditions for non-recognition of transfer in certain cases | Renumbered only |
| 48 | **72** | Mode of computation — full value of consideration minus cost of acquisition/improvement | Renumbered only |

---

## Key Definitions — Old → New

The 2025 Act reorganises definitions under Section 2. Four definition clauses central to capital gains have new clause numbers:

| Term | Old Clause | New Clause | Substantive Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capital asset | 2(14) | **2(22)** | None — same definition, same exclusions |
| Transfer | 2(47) | **2(109)** | None — definition unchanged |
| Short-term capital asset | — | **2(101)** | New dedicated clause — previously embedded in Section 2(42A) |
| Long-term capital asset | — | **2(67)** | New dedicated clause — previously embedded in Section 2(29A) |

The creation of standalone clauses 2(101) and 2(67) is a structural improvement — short-term and long-term capital asset now have their own numbered definitions instead of being tucked inside cross-referenced sub-clauses. The holding period thresholds themselves are unchanged.

**Bottom line: Update your definition cross-references. 2(14)→2(22), 2(47)→2(109). Note the new 2(101) and 2(67) for short-term and long-term.**

---

## Section 67: Charging Section (Old Section 45)

Section 67 charges capital gains to tax in the previous year in which the transfer takes place. Every charging trigger from old Section 45 is preserved:

- Transfer of a capital asset
- Conversion of a capital asset into stock-in-trade (gains taxable in the year of ultimate sale, computed as at the date of conversion)
- Transfer under a specified agreement (joint development agreements — taxable in the year of receipt of certificate of completion)
- Receipt of compensation on compulsory acquisition — taxable in the year of receipt

**Renumbered only. No substantive change.**

---

## Section 70: Transactions Not Regarded as Transfer (Old Section 47)

This is where the 2025 Act makes a genuine change. Old Section 47 listed transactions excluded from the definition of "transfer." Section 70 carries forward most of these but **does not re-enact two**:

### Exemptions Not Carried Forward

| Omitted Exemption | Old Provision | Why Dropped |
|---|---|---|
| Transfer by an **industrially sick company** managed by BIFR/NCLT rehabilitating agency | Section 47(xiiia) | BIFR was dissolved. NCLT handles insolvency under IBC. Provision was functionally dead. |
| Transfer of membership right in a **recognised stock exchange** on demutualisation | Section 47(xiiib) | Demutualisation of Indian stock exchanges was completed years ago. Provision was functionally dead. |

These are not policy changes — they are housekeeping. The 2025 Act chose not to re-enact provisions that could never apply again.

### Exemptions Retained (Illustrative)

All other categories of non-transfer continue under Section 70:

- Gift or irrevocable trust (not to a non-resident)
- Holding company ↔ 100% Indian subsidiary
- Amalgamation, demerger, business reorganisation
- Shareholder receiving shares in amalgamation
- Conversion of bonds/debentures into shares
- Partner ↔ firm transfers on dissolution
- Sole proprietor → company conversion
- Private/unlisted company → LLP conversion (conditions under Section 71)

> **Watch out:** If you have any pending assessments where clients relied on the BIFR or demutualisation exemptions, those exemptions are no longer available for transfers under the 2025 Act. For transfers that occurred under the 1961 Act, the saving clauses preserve the old position.

**Bottom line: Remove BIFR and demutualisation from your standard transfer-exclusion checklists. Everything else in old Section 47 carries forward into Section 70.**

---

## Section 72: Mode of Computation (Old Section 48)

The computation formula is unchanged in structure:

```
Capital Gains = Full Value of Consideration
              − Expenditure on Transfer
              − Cost of Acquisition
              − Cost of Improvement
```

> Indexation was abolished by Finance (No.2) Act 2024 for all transfers on or after 23 July 2024. The 2025 Act carries this forward — no further change. Grandfathering for land/building acquired before 23 July 2024 by resident individuals/HUFs also continues as enacted by FA 2024.

**Renumbered only. The 2025 Act does not modify the computation mechanism beyond what FA 2024 already did.**

## What You Must Update

1. **Section references in opinions and returns:** 45→67, 46→68, 46A→69, 47→70, 47A→71, 48→72.
2. **Definition cross-references:** 2(14)→2(22), 2(47)→2(109). Note new 2(101) for short-term, 2(67) for long-term.
3. **Transfer exclusion checklist:** Remove industrially sick company (BIFR) and stock exchange demutualisation exemptions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 45-48 → Sections 67-72.** Core capital gains framework renumbered. Logic preserved.
- **Definition clauses remapped:** Capital asset 2(14)→2(22), transfer 2(47)→2(109), plus new standalone clauses 2(101) short-term and 2(67) long-term.
- **Section 70 (old 47):** Two obsolete exemptions not carried forward — BIFR transfers and stock exchange demutualisation. Both were functionally dead.
- **Section 72 (old 48):** Renumbered. Indexation and rate changes were enacted by Finance (No.2) Act 2024 and are carried forward — no further modification by the 2025 Act.
- **Holding periods unchanged.** 12 months for listed equity/MFs, 24 months for unlisted/immovable/other — no change since Finance Act 2017.
