# Unit 7.2: Special Computations (Sections 73-81)

## Overview

The special computation rules for depreciable assets, bonus shares, slump sales, stamp-duty deeming, and related provisions move from Sections 49-51 to Sections 73-81. Nine sections renumbered, one genuine codification: **Section 76** elevates the debt MF/MLD always-STCG treatment (originally inserted into old Section 50AA by Finance Act 2023) into a standalone section in the base statute. Everything else is renumbering.

> Rates and indexation provisions as amended by Finance (No.2) Act 2024 are carried forward — no change by the 2025 Act.

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 49 | **73** | Cost with reference to cost to previous owner (gift, inheritance, partition, etc.) | Renumbered only |
| 50 | **74** | Depreciable assets — cost = WDV, always STCG | Renumbered only |
| 50A | **75** | Cost of acquisition of bonus shares/debentures | Renumbered only |

| 51 | **81** | Advance money received and forfeited | Renumbered only |

## Section 76: Debt MFs and MLDs — The Codification (Old Section 50AA)

This is the only section in this batch where the 2025 Act does more than renumber.

Old Section 50AA was inserted by the Finance Act 2023 as an amendment overlay — it modified the treatment of specified mutual funds and market-linked debentures by deeming them always short-term. The 2025 Act codifies this into the base statute as a standalone Section 76, giving it structural permanence rather than leaving it as an amendment insertion.

### What Section 76 Covers

| Asset Type | Treatment | Tax Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Specified mutual funds** — where ≤65% of total proceeds is invested in equity shares of domestic companies (debt MFs) | Always short-term, regardless of holding period | Slab rates |
| **Market-linked debentures (MLDs)** — listed or unlisted, where return is linked to market performance | Always short-term, regardless of holding period | Slab rates |

### What Changed vs. Old 50AA

Nothing substantive. The policy — preventing debt fund investors from claiming LTCG treatment on what is economically a fixed-income return — is preserved verbatim. The change is structural: what was an amendment insertion is now a first-class section in the base statute.

> **Practitioner note:** Section 76 applies to units of specified mutual funds acquired on or after 1 April 2023 and to MLDs whenever acquired. For MF units acquired before 1 April 2023, the old regime continues under transition provisions.

**Bottom line: Same rule, now a standalone section. Update your section reference from 50AA to 76.**

## Sections 73-75, 77-81: Renumbered Only

These eight sections are pure renumbering. A brief note on each:

### Section 73: Cost to Previous Owner (Old Section 49)

When a capital asset is acquired by gift, will, inheritance, succession, or HUF partition, the cost of acquisition is the cost at which the **previous owner** acquired the asset. Holding period of the previous owner is included. Chain traced back to the first owner who acquired by a mode involving consideration. FMV substitution for pre-1 April 2001 assets continues.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 74: Depreciable Assets (Old Section 50)

Depreciable assets in a block: full value of consideration deducted from WDV. If block ceases to exist — always STCG (or STCL). If block continues — WDV adjustment only.

**Renumbered only.** Indexation was never relevant to WDV-based computation.

### Section 75: Bonus Shares and Debentures (Old Section 50A)

Bonus shares allotted on or after 1 April 2001: cost = nil. Before 1 April 2001: cost = FMV as on 1 April 2001.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 77: Slump Sale (Old Section 50B)

Transfer of an undertaking as a going concern for lump-sum consideration. Capital gains = consideration minus net worth. LTCG if held >36 months; STCG if ≤36 months. The 36-month threshold for slump sales is an exception to the general 24-month rule for other assets — deliberately retained.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 78: Stamp Duty Value as Deemed Consideration (Old Section 50C)

Where immovable property is transferred for less than stamp duty value (beyond the 10% tolerance band), stamp duty value is deemed as full value of consideration.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 79: Unquoted Shares — FMV (Old Section 50CA)

Where unquoted shares are transferred for less than FMV (beyond 10% tolerance), FMV is deemed as full value of consideration.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 80: Consideration Not Ascertainable (Old Section 50D)

Where full value of consideration cannot be determined, FMV of the asset on the date of transfer is deemed as full value.

**Renumbered only.**

### Section 81: Advance Money Forfeited (Old Section 51)

Forfeited advance money is deducted from cost of acquisition on eventual transfer. If asset is never transferred, forfeited amount is taxable as income from other sources.

**Renumbered only.**

## What You Must Update

1. **Section references:** 49→73, 50→74, 50A→75, 50AA→76, 50B→77, 50C→78, 50CA→79, 50D→80, 51→81.
2. **Computation templates for debt MF/MLD gains:** Update the section citation from 50AA to 76. Treatment is identical.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 49-51 → Sections 73-81.** Nine sections renumbered.
- **Section 76 (old 50AA):** The only codification — debt MFs and MLDs always treated as STCG at slab rates. Originally a Finance Act 2023 insertion, now a standalone section in the base statute. No policy change.
- **All other sections (73-75, 77-81):** Pure renumbering. Depreciable assets, bonus shares, slump sales, stamp duty deeming, FMV provisions, advance forfeiture — all unchanged.
- **No new special computation categories added by the 2025 Act.**
