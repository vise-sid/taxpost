# Unit 3.1: The New TDS Architecture

## Overview

The 1961 Act scattered TDS across 30+ sections (192 through 194T), each covering a specific payment type with its own threshold, rate, and compliance rules. The 2025 Act consolidates the entire TDS framework into three sections: **392** (salary), **393** (all other TDS, organized in six tables), and **394** (TCS). Supporting provisions cover certificates (395), compliance (397), and default consequences (398). The substance is almost entirely unchanged — this is a reorganization exercise, not a reform.

---

## The Consolidation at a Glance

| Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Sections 192, 192A | **Section 392** | TDS on salary and RPF/EPF withdrawals |
| Sections 193, 194, 194A–194T | **Section 393** | All non-salary TDS — six tables |
| Section 206C | **Section 394** | TCS (Tax Collected at Source) |
| Section 197 | **Section 395** | Lower/nil withholding certificates |
| Section 203, 203A | **Section 397** | TDS compliance — returns, certificates, statements |
| Section 201 | **Section 398** | Consequences of default in deduction/deposit |

**Bottom line: 30+ sections become 3. The section numbers change; the obligations do not.**

---

## Section 393: The Table-Based Architecture

This is the structural innovation of the new Act. Instead of separate sections for each payment type, Section 393 organizes all non-salary TDS into **six tables**, grouped by payee type and purpose.

### The Six Tables

| Table | Subject | Entries | Payee Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Table 1** | TDS on payments to residents | 26 entries (8 serial numbers, multiple sub-entries) | Resident |
| **Table 2** | TDS on payments to non-residents | 19 entries | Non-resident |
| **Table 3** | TDS on payments to any person | 7 entries | Any person (resident + non-resident) |
| **Table 4** | Exemptions from TDS | Specified exempt categories | — |
| **Table 5** | Perquisite statements | Statement requirements for specified perquisites | — |
| **Table 6** | Self-declarations / Form 121 | Declarations by payee to avoid/reduce TDS | — |

### How to Read a Table Reference

The citation format under the new Act is:

> **Section 393, Table [X], Sl. No. [Y]**

For example, contractor payments (old 194C) are now cited as **Section 393, Table 1, Sl. No. 6(i)**. Every TDS certificate, challan, and return from April 2026 must use this format.

### Table 1: Resident Payments (26 Entries)

Covers all TDS on payments made to resident payees. Eight serial numbers, most with sub-entries:

| Sl. No. | Payment Category | Old Section(s) |
|---|---|---|
| 1(i)–(ii) | Insurance commission, commission/brokerage | 194D, 194H |
| 2(i)–(ii) | Rent (general, P&M, land/building) | 194I |
| 3(i)–(iii) | Immovable property, JDA, compulsory acquisition | 194IA, 194IC, 194LA |
| 4(i)–(iv) | MF/UTI income, business trust, AIF, securitisation | 194K, 194LBA, 194LBB, 194LBC |
| 5(i)–(iii) | Interest (securities, bank/PO, other) | 193, 194A |
| 6(i)–(iii) | Contractor, contractor (Ind/HUF), professional/technical/director/royalty | 194C, 194M, 194J |
| 7 | Dividend | 194 |
| 8(i)–(vi) | Life insurance, goods purchase, senior citizen lottery, perquisite benefits, e-commerce, VDA | 194DA, 194Q, 194B/BB/BA, 194BA, 194O, 194S |

## What Was Removed: Sections 206AB and 206CCA

The old Act imposed **higher TDS/TCS rates** on non-filers of income tax returns through Sections 206AB (TDS) and 206CCA (TCS). These required deductors to verify whether the payee had filed returns for the preceding two years and, if not, apply TDS at twice the applicable rate or 5%, whichever was higher.

**Sections 206AB and 206CCA were first removed from the 1961 Act by Finance Act 2025 (effective 1 April 2025). The 2025 Act does not re-enact them.**

This means:
- No separate higher-rate mechanism for non-filers under TDS
- No separate higher-rate mechanism for non-filers under TCS
- Deductors no longer need to run the "specified person" check on the compliance portal before each deduction

### What Continues: Higher Rates for Invalid/No PAN

The penalty for invalid PAN or failure to furnish PAN continues under **Section 397**. If the deductee does not provide a valid PAN, TDS is deducted at 20% (or the applicable rate, whichever is higher). This is unchanged from old Section 206AA.

> **Watch out:** The removal of 206AB/206CCA does not mean non-filers escape consequences entirely. The government may introduce alternative compliance mechanisms. But as of the Act text, the specific higher-rate TDS/TCS for non-filers is gone. Monitor CBDT notifications on this.

**Bottom line: Two compliance burdens removed — no more "specified person" verification for non-filers. But PAN-based higher rates under Section 397 remain.**

## Supporting Provisions

### Section 395: Lower/Nil Withholding Certificates

| Old Section | New Section | Function |
|---|---|---|
| 197 | **395(1)** | Application for lower/nil TDS certificate |
| 197A | **395** | Self-declaration for nil TDS (Form 121 for residents below threshold) |

New certificate applications from April 2026 go through **Form 128** under Section 395(1). Old Section 197 certificates valid for TY 2026-27 income continue to apply (covered in Unit 2.4).

### Section 397: Compliance

Consolidates old Sections 200, 200A, 203, 203A, and 206AA into a single compliance section:
- TDS deposit timelines
- TDS return filing requirements
- TDS certificate issuance
- PAN-based higher rate (old 206AA)
- Statement processing and computation of tax

### Section 398: Default Consequences

Replaces old Section 201. Covers:
- Deemed assessee-in-default provisions
- Interest on late deposit of TDS
- Consequences when TDS is not deducted or not deposited

## What Did NOT Change

This is worth stating explicitly because the consolidation can create a false impression of reform:

- **No TDS rate changes** — every rate in Section 393 reproduces the rate from the corresponding old section
- **No threshold changes by the 2025 Act itself** — the 2025 Act does not alter thresholds compared to the 1961 Act as amended. However, Finance Act 2025 had already changed certain thresholds in the old Act (e.g., senior citizen interest from Rs 50,000 to Rs 1,00,000, dividend from Rs 5,000 to Rs 10,000), and the 2025 Act adopts these amended figures.
- **No change in who must deduct** — payer categories (specified persons, any person, etc.) remain the same
- **No change in deposit timelines** — 7th of the following month remains the standard
- **No change in quarterly return frequency** — returns are still quarterly, with new form numbers (covered in Unit 2.4)

The consolidation is **presentational, not substantive**. The Act reorganized 30+ sections into a table format for readability and reduced cross-referencing. It did not change what gets deducted, at what rate, or by whom.

**Bottom line: If a client asks "did TDS rates change under the new Act?" — the answer is no. Only the section numbers and organizational structure changed.**

## Practical Impact

### What You Must Do Differently

1. **Update all section citations** — every TDS certificate, challan, and return from April 2026 uses Section 393 table references, not old section numbers (194C, 194H, 194J, etc.)
2. **Learn the table structure** — know which table (1, 2, or 3) applies to each payment type, because that determines the citation format
3. **Stop running 206AB checks** — the non-filer verification step is no longer required
4. **Update your software** — verify that your TDS software maps old sections to new Section 393 serial numbers before the first April 2026 deduction

### What Stays the Same

1. Rates and thresholds — identical
2. Deposit timelines — 7th of the following month
3. Quarterly return filing — same frequency, new form numbers
4. PAN-based higher rate — continues under Section 397
5. Lower withholding certificate mechanism — continues under Section 395

---

## Key Takeaways

- **30+ old TDS sections consolidate into 3**: Section 392 (salary), Section 393 (non-salary, six tables), Section 394 (TCS)
- **Section 393 uses six tables**: Table 1 (residents, 26 entries), Table 2 (non-residents, 19 entries), Table 3 (any person, 7 entries), Table 4 (exemptions), Table 5 (perquisite statements), Table 6 (self-declarations/Form 121)
- **Citation format changes**: old "Section 194C" becomes "Section 393, Table 1, Sl. No. 6(i)" — every certificate and return must use the new format
- **Sections 206AB and 206CCA were removed by Finance Act 2025 and not re-enacted in the 2025 Act**: no more higher TDS/TCS for non-filers; the "specified person" check is eliminated
- **PAN-based higher rates continue** under Section 397 (old 206AA)
- **No substantive rate or threshold changes by the 2025 Act itself**: the consolidation is purely presentational, though the 2025 Act adopts threshold changes already made by Finance Act 2025 (e.g., senior citizen interest threshold increased to Rs 1,00,000)
- **Supporting provisions**: Section 395 (certificates), 397 (compliance), 398 (default consequences) replace scattered old sections
