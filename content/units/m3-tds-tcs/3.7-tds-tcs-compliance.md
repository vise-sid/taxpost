# Unit 3.7: TDS/TCS Compliance Framework

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 overhauls the compliance infrastructure surrounding TDS and TCS — not the rates or thresholds, but the **forms, returns, certificates, penalties, and procedural mechanisms** that practitioners interact with daily. Every form number has changed. The return filing structure has been remapped. Certificates carry new designations. The penalty framework under Section 398 replaces old Section 201 with a more structured approach. This unit provides the complete compliance mapping that every deductor, collector, and practitioner needs as a working reference from April 1, 2026.

---

## TDS/TCS Return Forms

The quarterly return forms have been entirely renumbered. The old 24Q/26Q/27Q/27EQ designations are replaced by numbered forms under the Income Tax Rules, 2026.

| Old Form | Coverage | New Form | New Act Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| 24Q | Salary TDS quarterly return | **Form 138** | Section 392 |
| 26Q | Non-salary TDS (residents) quarterly return | **Form 140** | Section 393 (Tables 1 & 3) |
| 27Q | TDS on non-residents quarterly return | **Form 144** | Section 393 (Table 2) |
| 27EQ | TCS quarterly return | **Form 143** | Section 394 |

### Filing Deadlines

The quarterly filing structure continues. Due dates remain on the same cycle:

| Quarter | Period | Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | April – June | 31 July |
| Q2 | July – September | 31 October |
| Q3 | October – December | 31 January |
| Q4 | January – March | 31 May |

Government deductors depositing TDS without a challan (book entry) follow the same quarterly schedule.

## TDS/TCS Certificates

### Certificate Forms

| Old Form | Description | New Form | New Act Reference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form 16 | Salary TDS certificate (annual) | **Form 130** | Section 392 |
| Form 16A | Non-salary TDS certificate (quarterly) | **Form 131** | Section 393 |
| Form 16B | TDS on property sale | **Form 132** | Section 393 |
| Form 16C | TDS on rent (individuals/HUFs) | **Form 133** | Section 393 |
| Form 16D | TDS on contractor/professional (individuals/HUFs) | **Form 133** | Section 393 |
| Form 16E | TDS on virtual digital asset transfer | **Form 133** | Section 393 |
| Form 27D | TCS certificate | **Form 133** | Section 394 |

### Form 130 — New Three-Part Structure

The salary TDS certificate (old Form 16, new Form 130) has been restructured from **two parts to three parts**:

| Part | Old Form 16 | New Form 130 |
|---|---|---|
| Part A | TDS details (auto-generated from TRACES) | TDS details (system-generated from quarterly Form 138) |
| Part B | Salary computation and tax calculation | Salary breakup and computation |
| Part C | **Did not exist** | **Deduction details under the Act** |

**Key change:** Form 130 separates the deduction details (standard deduction, Chapter VI-A deductions, exemptions under Section 19) into a distinct Part C, making the certificate more granular. Part A continues to be system-generated from the quarterly returns (now Form 138 instead of 24Q).

### Form 133 — Consolidated Certificate

Old Forms 16C, 16D, 16E, and 27D have been merged into a single **Form 133**. This consolidation mirrors the challan-cum-statement merger (Form 141) — one form serving multiple transaction types that previously required separate certificates.

## Challan-cum-TDS/TCS Statements

The old Act required four separate challan-cum-statement forms for different transaction types. The new Act consolidates them into one.

| Old Form | Transaction Type | New Form |
|---|---|---|
| 26QB | Sale of immovable property | **Form 141** |
| 26QC | Rent by individuals/HUFs | **Form 141** |
| 26QD | Payment to resident contractor/professional by individuals/HUFs | **Form 141** |
| 26QE | Virtual digital asset transfer | **Form 141** |

**Form 141** is a single unified challan-cum-statement that handles all four transaction categories. The form includes a transaction-type field to distinguish between property sale, rent, contractor payment, and VDA transfer.

## Lower Withholding Certificate

| Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|
| Section 197 | **Section 395(1)** |
| Form 13 (application for lower/nil deduction) | **Form 128** |

A taxpayer expecting lower income than the threshold, or with carry-forward losses, may apply to the Assessing Officer for a certificate authorizing TDS at a lower rate or nil rate. The application is now filed via **Form 128** under Section 395(1).

**Validity:** The certificate is issued for a specified period and for specified payers. The deductor relies on the certificate to deduct at the reduced rate.

**Transition note:** Certificates issued under old Section 197 that cover income receivable during Tax Year 2026-27 remain valid under the new Act (see Unit 2.4).

## Self-Declaration for Non-Deduction

### Form 121 — Merged 15G/15H

| Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|
| Section 197A | **Section 393(6)** |
| Form 15G (below 60 years, non-company) | **Form 121** |
| Form 15H (senior citizens, 60+ years) | **Form 121** |

The distinction between Form 15G (for individuals below 60) and Form 15H (for senior citizens) has been **eliminated**. A single **Form 121** now serves all declarants — regardless of age — who declare that their total income is below the taxable limit and therefore no TDS should be deducted.

**Key simplification:** Under the old Act, the conditions for 15G and 15H were different (15G required tax on estimated income to be nil; 15H required only that the person be 60+ with estimated tax being nil). Form 121 unifies these under Section 393(6).

**Who can file:** Any person (not being a company or firm) whose estimated total income for the tax year results in nil tax liability.

## Foreign Remittance Forms

| Old Form | Description | New Form |
|---|---|---|
| Form 15CA | Information to be furnished for remittance to non-resident | **Form 145** |
| Form 15CB | Certificate from CA for remittance exceeding threshold | **Form 146** |

The procedural requirement remains the same: before any remittance to a non-resident, the remitter must furnish information via Form 145 (old 15CA). If the remittance exceeds the specified threshold, a Chartered Accountant's certificate in Form 146 (old 15CB) must accompany the filing.

## Interest on Defaults — Section 398

Section 398 of the 2025 Act replaces old Section 201 and provides a structured penalty framework for TDS/TCS defaults.

### Interest Rates

| Default | Interest Rate | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Failure to deduct tax at source | **1% per month** (or part of month) | Section 398(3)(a)(i) |
| Failure to deposit tax after deduction | **1.5% per month** (or part of month) | Section 398(3)(a)(ii) |

### Interest Computation

- **1% per month** runs from the date on which tax was deductible to the date on which tax is actually deducted.
- **1.5% per month** runs from the date of deduction to the date of actual deposit with the government.

Both rates are computed on a simple interest basis. "Part of a month" is treated as a full month for computation purposes.

### Assessee-in-Default — Section 398

Section 398 (replacing old Section 201) declares the deductor as an **assessee-in-default** for failure to deduct or deposit TDS. The key provisions:

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Default declaration** | Deductor treated as assessee-in-default for failure to deduct or failure to deposit |
| **Exception** | No default if the deductee has **filed a return of income**, **included the relevant income**, and **paid the tax due** on such income |
| **Time limit** | Order under Section 398 cannot be passed after **6 years** from the end of the tax year in which the deduction was required |
| **Charge on assets** | Tax due from assessee-in-default constitutes a **charge on the assets** of the defaulting deductor |

**The deductee-has-paid exception** is practically significant. If a contractor from whom TDS was not deducted has already included the income in their return and paid tax, the deductor is not treated as in default for the principal TDS amount — though interest under Section 398(3) may still apply for the period of delay.

## Key Takeaways

- **Return forms completely renumbered**: 24Q → Form 138, 26Q → Form 140, 27Q → Form 144, 27EQ → Form 143. Quarterly filing cycle and deadlines unchanged.
- **Certificate forms remapped**: Form 16 → Form 130 (now 3 parts instead of 2), Form 16A → Form 131, Form 16B → Form 132, Forms 16C/16D/16E/27D → consolidated Form 133.
- **Form 130 has three parts** — the new Part C separates deduction details, making the salary certificate more structured than old Form 16.
- **Four challan-cum-statement forms (26QB/QC/QD/QE) become one Form 141.**
- **15G and 15H merged into Form 121** — age-based distinction eliminated; single declaration form under Section 393(6).
- **Lower withholding**: Section 197 → Section 395(1), Form 13 → Form 128.
- **Foreign remittance**: 15CA → Form 145, 15CB → Form 146.
- **Interest rates**: 1% per month for failure to deduct; 1.5% per month for failure to deposit after deduction (Section 398(3)).
- **Assessee-in-default (Section 398)**: 6-year time limit, charge on assets, exception where deductee has filed and paid.
