# Unit 3.2: TDS on Salary (Section 392)

## Overview

Section 392 replaces old Sections 192 and 192A. It governs TDS on salary at the average rate, employer-paid perquisite tax, startup ESOP deferrals, adjustments for other income and losses, perquisite statements, RPF/EPF withdrawal TDS, and foreign currency salary. The substantive position is almost entirely retained — the same computation methodology, the same adjustments, the same employer obligations. The most significant practical change is not in Section 392 itself but in the default tax regime: Section 202 (new regime) applies unless the employee opts out, which directly affects how the employer computes TDS.

---

## Old --> New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 192(1) | **392(1)** | TDS on salary at average rate | Renumbered only |
| 192(1A) | **392(2)** | Employer pays tax on perquisites | Renumbered only |
| 192(1B) | **392(3)** | Startup ESOP — deferred TDS on share perquisites | Renumbered only |
| 192(2B) | **392(4)** | Adjustments: other income, HP loss, relief u/s 89 | Renumbered + reference update |
| 192(2C) | **392(5)** | Perquisite statement by employer | Renumbered only |
| 192A | **392(6)–(7)** | RPF/EPF premature withdrawal TDS | Renumbered only |
| 192(3) | **392(8)** | Foreign currency salary | Renumbered only |

---

## Section 392(1): TDS at Average Rate

The employer deducts TDS on salary at the **average rate of income tax** computed on the estimated total income of the employee for the tax year. This is identical to old Section 192(1).

The computation:
1. Estimate total salary income for the tax year
2. Allow deductions the employee is entitled to claim
3. Compute tax at applicable slab rates
4. Divide total tax by 12 (or number of months remaining) to get monthly TDS

### Default Regime: Section 202

Here is the change that affects every salary TDS computation from April 2026. **Section 202 (new tax regime) is the default.** The employer computes TDS under the new regime slab rates unless the employee furnishes a declaration opting for the old regime.

This is not new policy — Section 115BAC already made the new regime default from AY 2024-25. But under the 2025 Act, the reference shifts to **Section 202**, and the opt-out mechanism references the corresponding new Act provisions.

> **Watch out:** If an employee does not submit any regime declaration, the employer must compute TDS under Section 202 (new regime). Applying old regime rates without a written opt-in from the employee is incorrect under the new Act. Ensure your payroll system defaults to Section 202.

**Bottom line: Same rule as before — new regime is default. The section reference changes from 115BAC to 202.**

---

## Section 392(2): Employer-Paid Perquisite Tax

Where the employer opts to pay tax on non-monetary perquisites on behalf of the employee, the tax paid is not treated as income of the employee. This replicates old Section 192(1A) without change.

Practical use case: employer-provided accommodation, car, club membership — the employer can bear the tax on these perquisites. The employee's gross salary is not increased by the tax amount.

**Bottom line: Same provision, new number. No change in treatment.**

---

## Section 392(3): Startup ESOP Deferral

For eligible startups, TDS on the perquisite value of ESOPs/shares allotted to employees is deferred. The tax is deducted at the earliest of:
- 60 days from the date of allotment (this was 48 months in the old Act — **verify current rules as the period has been subject to amendments**)
- The date the employee sells the shares
- The date the employee ceases employment

This carries forward old Section 192(1B). The eligible startup definition and conditions remain governed by CBDT notification criteria.

**Bottom line: Startup ESOP deferral continues. Section reference changes from 192(1B) to 392(3).**

---

## Section 392(4): Adjustments

The employer may factor in other income and deductions while computing salary TDS, provided the employee furnishes the relevant information. This replaces old Section 192(2B).

### What the Employer Can Adjust

| Adjustment | Old Reference | New Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Other source income (interest, etc.) reported by employee | 192(2B) | 392(4) |
| House property loss (set-off against salary) | 192(2B), loss u/s 24 | 392(4), loss u/s 22 |
| Relief for salary arrears/advance | 192(2B), relief u/s 89 | 392(4), relief u/s **157** |

### Key Reference Updates

- Old Section 89 (relief when salary paid in arrears) --> **Section 157**
- Old Section 24 (deduction from HP income) --> **Section 22**
- Loss from house property capped at Rs 2,00,000 for set-off against salary — this limit is unchanged

> **Watch out:** The section references for adjustments change — relief under Section 89 becomes Section 157, HP loss under Section 24 becomes Section 22. If your Form 12B or investment declaration templates reference old section numbers, update them before April 2026.

**Bottom line: Same adjustments permitted, same limits. Update section references in employee declaration forms.**

---

## Section 392(5): Perquisite Statement

The employer must furnish a statement of perquisites provided to employees. This replicates old Section 192(2C). The statement format and filing requirements are governed by the Income Tax Rules, 2026.

**Bottom line: Same obligation, new section number.**

---

## Section 392(6)–(7): RPF/EPF Withdrawal TDS

Premature withdrawal from a Recognised Provident Fund or EPF is subject to TDS at **10%** if the amount exceeds **Rs 50,000**. This replaces old Section 192A.

| Parameter | Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|---|
| Section | 192A | 392(6)–(7) |
| Rate | 10% | 10% |
| Threshold | Rs 50,000 | Rs 50,000 |
| Applicable to | RPF/EPF premature withdrawal | RPF/EPF premature withdrawal |

No PAN → TDS at 20% (under Section 397, old 206AA).

**Bottom line: Identical provision. Update section reference from 192A to 392(6)–(7).**

---

## Section 392(8): Foreign Currency Salary

For salary paid in foreign currency, TDS is computed by converting the salary to Indian rupees at the prescribed exchange rate. This replicates old Section 192(3).

The exchange rate is the telegraphic transfer buying rate (TTBR) of SBI on the date of deduction, as prescribed in the Rules.

**Bottom line: No change. Same conversion methodology, new section number.**

---

## Perquisite Valuation: Still in the Rules

A critical point for practitioners: perquisite valuation methodology is **not in the Act**. It remains in the **Income Tax Rules** (old Rules 3, 3A; new IT Rules, 2026). The Act defines what a perquisite is (Section 17); the Rules prescribe how to value it.

This means:
- Motor car valuation tables → Rules
- Accommodation valuation (metro/non-metro, population-based) → Rules
- Interest-free loan computation → Rules
- Specified employee thresholds → Rules

The 2025 Act introduces **delegated threshold-setting** for perquisites (covered in Unit 4.2), allowing CBDT to update monetary limits via Rules notification without amending the Act. This is a structural improvement for future flexibility but does not change current valuation methodology.

---

## What Stayed the Same — The Full List

- TDS at average rate methodology
- Employer-paid perquisite tax treatment
- Startup ESOP deferral mechanism
- Adjustments for other income, HP loss, and salary relief
- Perquisite statement obligation
- RPF/EPF withdrawal TDS rate (10%) and threshold (Rs 50,000)
- Foreign currency salary conversion rules
- Quarterly deposit timelines (7th of following month)

The legislative intent is clear: Section 392 is a renumbering and reorganization of Section 192/192A. The computation, compliance, and obligations are carried over intact.

**Bottom line: If you know how to compute salary TDS under Section 192, you already know Section 392. Update your section references and declaration forms — the math is the same.**

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 192 --> 392**: eight subsections mapped one-to-one, no substantive changes
- **Section 192A --> 392(6)–(7)**: RPF/EPF withdrawal TDS continues at 10% above Rs 50,000
- **Default tax regime is Section 202** (new regime) — employer must use it unless employee opts out in writing
- **Relief u/s 89 --> Section 157**, **HP loss u/s 24 --> Section 22**: update all adjustment references
- **Perquisite valuation remains in the Rules**, not the Act — no change in valuation methodology
- **Investment declaration forms and Form 12B templates** need updated section references before April 2026
- **Substantive position unchanged**: "Almost same provisions retained" — this is renumbering, not reform
