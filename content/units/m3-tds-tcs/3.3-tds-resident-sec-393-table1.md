# Unit 3.3: TDS -- Resident Payments (Section 393, Table 1)

## Overview

Section 393, Table 1 consolidates all TDS provisions for payments made to **resident** payees into a single table with 26 entries organized across 8 serial numbers. Every old section from 193, 194, 194A through 194S that applied to resident payments is now located here. Rates and thresholds are carried over as-is — the change is purely organizational. This unit provides the complete table with old-to-new mappings, rates, thresholds, and change types for every entry.

---

## Complete Table 1: TDS on Payments to Residents

### Sl. No. 1: Commission

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Payer | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1(i)** | Insurance commission | 194D | 2% | Rs 20,000 | Any person paying insurance commission | Renumbered only |
| **1(ii)** | Commission or brokerage (non-insurance) | 194H | 2% | Rs 20,000 | Specified person | Renumbered only |

Old Section 194D covered insurance commission with TDS at "rates in force" — for residents, this was effectively 2% (after the Finance Act rate specification). Old Section 194H covered all other commission/brokerage at 2%.

**Bottom line: Same rates, same Rs 20,000 threshold. Two sections become two sub-entries in one serial number.**

---

### Sl. No. 2: Rent

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **2(i)** | Rent — general (individuals/HUFs not covered by tax audit) | 194-IB (old) | 2% | Rs 50,000/month | Renumbered only |
| **2(ii)(a)** | Rent — plant and machinery | 194I(a) | 2% | Rs 50,000/month | Renumbered only |
| **2(ii)(b)** | Rent — land, building, furniture, fittings | 194I(b) | 10% | Rs 50,000/month | Renumbered only |

The rent provisions were split across old Sections 194I (specified persons) and 194-IB (individuals/HUFs not subject to tax audit). Table 1 consolidates them under a single serial number with sub-entries.

> **Watch out:** The rate differential between plant/machinery rent (2%) and land/building rent (10%) continues. This is not a consolidation of rates — only of section numbers. Misclassifying a land/building rental as plant/machinery results in short deduction.

**Bottom line: Three rent scenarios, same rates as before. Cite Section 393, Table 1, Sl. No. 2(i), 2(ii)(a), or 2(ii)(b) as applicable.**

---

### Sl. No. 3: Immovable Property Transactions

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3(i)** | Purchase of immovable property | 194IA | 1% | Rs 50,00,000 (Rs 50 lakh) | Renumbered only |
| **3(ii)** | Joint development agreement (JDA) | 194IC | 10% | Nil | Renumbered only |
| **3(iii)** | Compulsory acquisition of immovable property | 194LA | 10% | Rs 5,00,000 (Rs 5 lakh) | Renumbered only |

Property purchase TDS at 1% above Rs 50 lakh applies to any transferee (not just specified persons). The JDA provision at 10% with no threshold also carries over unchanged. Compulsory acquisition compensation continues at 10% above Rs 5 lakh.

**Bottom line: All three immovable property TDS provisions are renumbered only. Rates, thresholds, and payer obligations identical.**

---

### Sl. No. 4: Investment Income Distributions

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **4(i)** | Income from units of mutual fund/UTI | 194K | 10% | Rs 10,000 | Renumbered only |
| **4(ii)** | Business trust distributions (interest/dividend/rental) | 194LBA | 10% | Nil | Renumbered only |
| **4(iii)** | Investment fund (AIF) distributions | 194LBB | 10% | Nil | Renumbered only |
| **4(iv)** | Securitisation trust distributions | 194LBC | 10% | Nil | Renumbered only |

All four entries carry a uniform 10% rate. The Rs 10,000 threshold applies only to MF/UTI income; business trust, AIF, and securitisation trust distributions have no threshold.

**Bottom line: Investment income distributions — same four provisions, same 10% rate. New table reference replaces four separate section numbers.**

---

### Sl. No. 5: Interest

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Payer | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **5(i)** | Interest on securities | 193 | Rates in force (10%) | Rs 10,000 | Any person paying interest on listed debentures/government securities | Renumbered only |
| **5(ii)** | Interest other than interest on securities — bank/post office | 194A | Rates in force (10%) | Rs 50,000 (Rs 1,00,000 for senior citizens) | Banking company, co-op society, post office | Renumbered only |
| **5(iii)** | Interest other than interest on securities — others | 194A | Rates in force (10%) | Rs 50,000 | Any other specified person | Renumbered only |

The interest provisions are among the most frequently encountered in practice. The split between securities interest (old 193) and other interest (old 194A) is preserved. Senior citizen higher threshold of Rs 1,00,000 continues for bank/post office interest.

> **Watch out:** The "rates in force" language means the actual rate is specified in the Finance Act / Part II of the First Schedule, not in Section 393 itself. For residents, this has been 10% for years and continues at 10%. But the Act technically says "rates in force," so the rate could change via a future Finance Act without amending Section 393.

**Bottom line: Interest TDS continues at 10%, same thresholds. Senior citizen benefit (Rs 1 lakh threshold for bank/PO interest) is preserved.**

---

### Sl. No. 6: Contractor and Professional Payments

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **6(i)** | Contractor/sub-contractor payments | 194C | 1% (individual/HUF) / 2% (others) | Rs 30,000 per contract or Rs 1,00,000 aggregate p.a. | Renumbered only |
| **6(ii)** | Contractor/professional payments by individual/HUF (non-audit) | 194M | 2% | Rs 50,00,000 (Rs 50 lakh) p.a. | Renumbered only |
| **6(iii)(a)** | Technical services (not being professional) | 194J | 2% | Rs 50,000 | Renumbered only |
| **6(iii)(b)** | Professional services | 194J | 10% | Rs 50,000 | Renumbered only |
| **6(iii)(c)** | Director fees/remuneration (non-salary) | 194J | 10% | Nil | Renumbered only |
| **6(iii)(d)** | Royalty (non-cinema) | 194J | 10% | Rs 50,000 | Renumbered only |

This serial number absorbs the highest volume of TDS transactions for most CA practices. The old 194C and 194J rate structures — including the 2% vs 10% split within 194J — are reproduced exactly.

Key distinction preserved:
- **194J technical services → 2%** (call centre, data processing, etc.)
- **194J professional services → 10%** (legal, medical, engineering, etc.)
- **Director fees → 10%** with no threshold

> **Watch out:** The 194C dual threshold (Rs 30,000 per contract OR Rs 1,00,000 aggregate) continues. This is the provision that trips up most deductors — individual payments below Rs 30,000 still require TDS if aggregate payments to the same contractor exceed Rs 1 lakh in the year.

**Bottom line: The most-used TDS provisions — contractor and professional payments — are renumbered only. The 1%/2%/10% rate structure and all thresholds are identical.**

### Sl. No. 7: Dividend

| Entry | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **7** | Dividend | 194 | 10% | Rs 5,000 | Renumbered only |

TDS on dividend at 10% continues. The Rs 5,000 threshold (below which no TDS applies) is carried over.

**Bottom line: Same rate, same threshold. Section 194 becomes Section 393, Table 1, Sl. No. 7.**

The miscellaneous entries capture provisions that were added to the 1961 Act in recent years (194Q in 2021, 194R in 2022, 194S in 2022). All rates and thresholds are carried over without change.

**Bottom line: Recent additions to the old Act (goods purchase, business perquisites, e-commerce, VDA) all find their place in Table 1 with identical terms.**

## Key Takeaways

- **Table 1 contains 26 entries across 8 serial numbers** — covering every TDS provision applicable to resident payees
- **Every entry is renumbered only** — no rate changes, no threshold changes, no change in payer obligations
- **The most-used provisions** (194C contractors, 194J professionals, 194I rent, 194A interest) all map cleanly to specific serial numbers in Table 1
- **Rate differentials are preserved**: 2% vs 10% within old 194J (now Sl. No. 6(iii)(a) vs (b)/(c)/(d)), 2% vs 10% for rent (now Sl. No. 2(ii)(a) vs (b))
- **Senior citizen higher threshold for bank interest** (Rs 1 lakh vs Rs 50,000) continues at Sl. No. 5(ii)
- **Recent provisions** (194Q, 194R, 194S, 194O) are absorbed into Table 1 at Sl. No. 8
- **Print the quick-reference table above** — you will need it for every TDS certificate and return in April 2026
