# Unit 3.4: TDS -- Non-Resident Payments (Section 393, Table 2)

## Overview

Section 393, Table 2 consolidates all TDS provisions for payments made to **non-resident** payees into 19 entries. The most significant structural change here is the breakup of old Section 195 — which was a catch-all for any payment to a non-resident — into specific serial numbers with designated rates. Under the 1961 Act, a practitioner would cite "Section 195" for NR royalties, NR dividends, NR interest, and virtually everything else, relying on the rate schedule or DTAA for the actual rate. Under the 2025 Act, each payment category has its own serial number in Table 2 with an explicit rate. The substance is unchanged, but the specificity is a meaningful improvement for compliance.

---

## The Section 195 Breakup: The Key Structural Change

Old Section 195 was the Swiss Army knife of NR TDS — one section covering all payments to non-residents (other than salary and those covered by specific sections like 194E, 194LC, 196A-196D). The payer had to look up the applicable rate in the First Schedule or the relevant DTAA.

Under Table 2, the old Section 195 catch-all is split into explicit entries:

| Payment Type | Old Citation | New Citation |
|---|---|---|
| Interest on foreign currency loan | "Section 195, rate as per Schedule/DTAA" | Section 393, Table 2, Sl. No. [specific] — 20% or DTAA |
| Royalty/FTS | "Section 195, rate as per Schedule/DTAA" | Section 393, Table 2, Sl. No. [specific] — 20% or DTAA |
| NR dividend | "Section 195, rate as per Schedule/DTAA" | Section 393, Table 2, Sl. No. [specific] — 20% or DTAA |
| Residuary (all other NR payments) | "Section 195" | Section 393, Table 2, Sl. No. [specific] — 30% or DTAA |

This means the deductor now has a precise serial number to cite on the challan and certificate, rather than the generic "195."

**Bottom line: Section 195 is no longer a single catch-all. Each NR payment type has its own line item in Table 2. The rates are the same — but the citation is now specific.**

## Complete Table 2: TDS on Payments to Non-Residents

### Sports and Entertainment

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | NR sportsman / sports association income | 194E | 20% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |

Old Section 194E covered income paid to non-resident sportsmen (match fees, endorsements, appearance fees) and sports associations. The 20% rate continues. DTAA provisions (particularly Article 17 in most treaties — "Artistes and Sportspersons") may provide a lower rate or exemption.

---

### Interest Payments

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Interest on foreign currency borrowing | 195 | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Restructured — extracted from 195 catch-all |
| — | Interest on infrastructure debt fund bonds | 194LC | 5% | Nil | No (concessional rate is Act-specified) | Renumbered only |
| — | Interest on rupee-denominated bonds (masala bonds) | 194LC | 5% | Nil | No | Renumbered only |
| — | Interest on securities — FPI | 196D | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |

The concessional 5% rate for infrastructure bonds and rupee-denominated bonds (old Section 194LC) continues. These were introduced as incentive provisions and the new Act preserves them at the same rate.

FPI interest (old 196D) moves at 20% or the DTAA rate, whichever is lower. This was the effective rate under the old Act as well.

**Bottom line: Interest rates for NR payments — unchanged. Infrastructure/masala bond concession at 5% preserved.**

---

### Investment Fund and Trust Distributions

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Business trust distributions (interest/dividend/rental) to NR | 194LBA | 5% (interest) / 10% (dividend/rental) | Nil | Yes, for dividend/rental | Renumbered only |
| — | Investment fund (AIF) distributions to NR | 194LBB | 10% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |
| — | Securitisation trust distributions to NR | 194LBC | 10% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |

For business trust distributions to non-residents, the rate depends on the nature of the distributed income — interest component at 5%, dividend/rental at 10%. AIF and securitisation trust distributions continue at 10%.

**Bottom line: Trust/fund distribution rates for NR payees — unchanged. Same split rates for business trusts.**

---

### Mutual Fund and Capital Gains

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Income from MF units (NR) | 196A | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |
| — | Offshore fund — LTCG on units | 196B | 10% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |
| — | Offshore fund — STCG on units | 196B | 12.5% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |
| — | GDR income / LTCG | 196C | 10% | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |
| — | FPI — income from securities (other than interest) | 196D | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Renumbered only |

Section 196B's dual rates — 10% for LTCG and 12.5% for STCG from offshore fund units — continue. GDR income/gains under old Section 196C remain at 10%.

FPI securities income (dividends, capital gains other than interest) under old Section 196D carries forward at 20% or the DTAA rate.

**Bottom line: All MF/capital gains TDS rates for NR payees are carried over. The 10%/12.5% LTCG/STCG split for offshore funds is preserved.**

---

### IFSC Fund Income (New Entry)

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Income from IFSC fund units | 196E (new) | 10% | Nil | Yes | **New provision** |

This is one of the few genuinely new entries in Table 2. Old Section 196E did not exist in the 1961 Act — it is a new provision in the 2025 Act covering TDS on income from units of funds operating in International Financial Services Centres (IFSCs like GIFT City).

The 10% rate aligns with the broader policy of concessional taxation for IFSC operations. This applies to non-resident investors in IFSC-based funds.

**Bottom line: New entry — not a renumbering. NR investors in IFSC funds now have a specific 10% TDS provision at Section 393, Table 2.**

---

### Royalty, FTS, and Residuary

| Sl. No. | Payment Type | Old Section | Rate | Threshold | DTAA Override | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Royalty | 195 | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Restructured — extracted from 195 |
| — | Fees for technical services (FTS) | 195 | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Restructured — extracted from 195 |
| — | Dividend to NR | 195 | 20% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Restructured — extracted from 195 |
| — | Residuary — all other NR payments | 195 | 30% or DTAA rate | Nil | Yes | Restructured — extracted from 195 |

These four categories were all lumped under old Section 195 with the rate determined by the First Schedule or DTAA. Table 2 gives each its own serial number:

- **Royalty and FTS at 20%** — this is the Act rate. Most DTAAs provide 10% or 15% for royalties and 10%-15% for FTS. The lower DTAA rate applies where available.
- **NR dividend at 20%** — again, DTAA rates (typically 10%-15%) will often apply.
- **Residuary at 30%** — this is the highest rate and applies to any NR payment not covered by a specific entry. DTAA rates override where applicable.

> **Watch out:** The residuary rate is 30%, not 20%. Under old Section 195, practitioners sometimes defaulted to 20% for unclassified NR payments. Under Table 2, the residuary rate is explicitly 30%. If the payment fits a specific category (royalty, FTS, dividend, interest), use the specific entry at 20%. Only payments that do not fit any specific category fall under the 30% residuary.

**Bottom line: Old Section 195 is disaggregated into specific entries. The rates are the same as what the First Schedule prescribed. The improvement is in citation specificity — each payment type now has its own line.**

## DTAA Override: How It Works Under the New Act

The DTAA override mechanism is unchanged in substance. Where a Double Tax Avoidance Agreement provides a lower rate than the Act rate, the lower rate applies — provided the payee:
1. Furnishes a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from their home country
2. Provides Form 10F (or equivalent under IT Rules, 2026) with required particulars
3. Does not have a Permanent Establishment (PE) in India that would attribute the income to domestic operations

The new Act reference for DTAA benefit is **Section 159** (old Section 90) and **Section 160** (old Section 91).

> **Watch out:** The TRC + Form 10F requirement continues. Deductors must collect these documents before applying the DTAA rate. Deducting at the DTAA rate without TRC on file exposes the deductor to deemed assessee-in-default proceedings under Section 398 (old 201).

**Bottom line: DTAA override mechanism is identical. Section references change from 90/91 to 159/160. Document collection requirements (TRC, Form 10F) continue.**

## Practical Impact

### What Changes in Daily Practice

1. **Citation specificity for NR payments** — you can no longer just write "Section 195" on every NR payment challan. Each payment type has its own Table 2 serial number. Update your challan templates.
2. **Software mapping** — verify that your TDS software correctly maps old 195-based transactions to the appropriate Table 2 serial number based on payment nature (royalty vs FTS vs dividend vs residuary).
3. **Residuary rate awareness** — the explicit 30% residuary rate in Table 2 makes it harder to default to a lower rate for unclassified payments. Classify correctly or face the 30% rate.
4. **IFSC fund entries** — if you handle NR clients investing in GIFT City funds, note the new 196E provision at 10%.

### What Stays the Same

1. All rates are unchanged from what the First Schedule / specific sections prescribed
2. DTAA override mechanism continues identically
3. TRC and Form 10F documentation requirements are preserved
4. No threshold changes for any NR payment category
5. Form 15CA/15CB requirements for foreign remittances continue (under corresponding new Act rules)

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Table 2 contains 19 entries** covering all TDS on payments to non-resident payees
- **Old Section 195 catch-all is disaggregated** into specific serial numbers for interest, royalty, FTS, dividend, and residuary payments — each with its own rate
- **Residuary rate is explicitly 30%** (not 20%) — misclassification risk is real
- **Section 196E (IFSC fund income at 10%) is a new provision** — not a renumbering from the old Act
- **Concessional rates preserved**: infrastructure bonds and masala bonds at 5%, offshore fund LTCG at 10%
- **DTAA override is unchanged**: lower treaty rate applies with TRC + Form 10F; new references are Section 159/160
- **All other rates and thresholds are carried over as-is** — this is reorganization, not reform
- **Update challan and certificate templates** to cite specific Table 2 serial numbers instead of generic "Section 195"
