# Unit 11.1: Set Off Rules (Sections 108-109)

## Overview

Set off of losses is the first step in the loss utilisation hierarchy — intra-head first, then inter-head. The 2025 Act consolidates the set-off machinery into two compact sections: **Section 108** (intra-head) and **Section 109** (inter-head), replacing the old Sections 70 and 71 respectively. The substantive rules are unchanged. The drafting is tighter, with sub-sections replacing provisos, but no new restriction or relaxation is introduced by the 2025 Act itself.

> HP loss inter-head cap of Rs 2 lakh (Section 109(1)(b)) — carried forward from Finance Act 2017 (effective AY 2018-19) — no further change by the 2025 Act.

---

## Old --> New Section Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 70 | **108** | Set off of loss from one source against income from another source under the same head (intra-head) | Restructured — cleaner sub-section format, same rule |
| 71 | **109** | Set off of loss from one head against income from another head (inter-head) | Restructured — provisos replaced by sub-sections, same restrictions |

---

## What Genuinely Changed (2025 Act)

### No Substantive Change

The 2025 Act does not alter any set-off rule. Sections 108 and 109 are a faithful restatement of old Sections 70 and 71, with two improvements in form:

1. **Section 108** replaces the proviso-based structure of old Section 70 with numbered sub-sections. The intra-head set-off rule and the capital gains segregation rule (LTCL only against LTCG) are now distinct sub-sections rather than a main section with a proviso.

2. **Section 109** consolidates the inter-head restrictions (previously scattered across provisos to Section 71) into a structured sub-section format. The Rs 2 lakh house property loss cap, the prohibition on setting off capital gains losses against other heads, and similar restrictions are now explicitly enumerated sub-sections.

No new restriction is introduced. No existing restriction is removed.

### LTCL Against STCG — The Withdrawn Proposal

The Income Tax Bill 2025, as introduced, proposed allowing long-term capital loss to be set off against short-term capital gains. This would have been a genuine substantive change. However, **this proposal was withdrawn during Parliamentary consideration and does not appear in the final Income Tax Act, 2025 as enacted**. The existing rule — LTCL only against LTCG — continues unchanged under Section 108(2).

---

## What Stayed the Same

The following rules operate identically under the 2025 Act:

### Intra-Head Set Off (Section 108)

| Rule | Section | Position |
|---|---|---|
| Loss from one source set off against income from another source under the same head | 108(1) | Unchanged |
| Capital gains segregation: LTCL set off only against LTCG | 108(2) | Unchanged |
| STCL set off against STCG or LTCG | 108(2) | Unchanged |

### Inter-Head Set Off (Section 109)

| Rule | Section | Position |
|---|---|---|
| Loss under any head (other than capital gains) set off against income under any other head | 109(1) | Unchanged |
| HP loss: inter-head set off capped at Rs 2 lakh per tax year | 109(1)(b) | Unchanged (FA 2017) |
| Capital gains loss: cannot be set off against income under any other head | 109 | Unchanged |
| Speculation business loss: set off only against speculation business income | 109 | Unchanged |
| Specified business loss (Section 36(1)(viii) activities): set off only against specified business income | 109 | Unchanged |
| Race horse maintenance loss: set off only against race horse income | 109 | Unchanged |
| Loss from an activity of owning/maintaining race horses: not set off against any other income | 109 | Unchanged |

### The Inter-Head Prohibition List

The following losses remain ring-fenced — they cannot be set off against income from other heads:

- **Capital gains losses** — only against capital gains
- **Speculation business losses** — only against speculation profits
- **Specified business losses** — only against specified business income
- **Race horse losses** — only against race horse income
- **HP losses exceeding Rs 2 lakh** — excess carried forward, not set off inter-head

---

## Practical Impact

### Nothing Changes in Practice

Set-off computations for AY 2026-27 onward follow the same logic as prior years. The only difference is the section citation:

| Old Citation | New Citation |
|---|---|
| Section 70 | **Section 108** |
| Section 71 | **Section 109** |

### Do Not Assume the LTCL-STCG Relaxation Applies

Several commentaries published during the Bill stage discussed the proposed LTCL-STCG set-off relaxation. Since this was withdrawn in the final Act, any client advisory or return computation assuming this relaxation would be incorrect. Section 108(2) retains the existing restriction: LTCL is set off only against LTCG.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 70 --> Section 108 (intra-head set off); Section 71 --> Section 109 (inter-head set off) — restructured, not changed.**
- **No substantive change in any set-off rule by the 2025 Act.**
- **LTCL-against-STCG proposal was in the Bill but withdrawn in the final Act — the old restriction continues.**
- **HP loss Rs 2 lakh inter-head cap continues under Section 109(1)(b) — this was a Finance Act 2017 change, not a 2025 Act change.**
- **Capital gains, speculation, specified business, and race horse losses remain ring-fenced — same restrictions, new section numbers.**
