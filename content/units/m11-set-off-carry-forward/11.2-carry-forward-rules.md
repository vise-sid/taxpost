# Unit 11.2: Carry Forward Rules (Sections 110-115)

## Overview

Where a loss cannot be fully set off in the year it arises, the carry-forward provisions determine how long it survives and under what conditions it can be utilised in subsequent years. The 2025 Act maps the six carry-forward provisions of the 1961 Act into Sections 110 through 115, preserving every carry-forward period, every restriction, and every condition without change. The transition provisions under Section 536(2)(m) and (n) ensure that pre-2026 losses carry forward seamlessly into the new Act's framework.

> All carry-forward periods are unchanged by the 2025 Act. The restriction on set-off against undisclosed income (Section 120, old 79A) — carried forward from Finance Act 2024 — no further change by the 2025 Act.

## What Genuinely Changed (2025 Act)

### No Substantive Change

The 2025 Act does not alter any carry-forward period, any carry-forward condition, or any carry-forward restriction. Sections 110-115 are a direct renumbering of the corresponding 1961 Act provisions. The internal structure of each section is reorganised — provisos become sub-sections — but the legal effect is identical.

---

## What Stayed the Same

### Carry-Forward Periods

| Loss Type | Period | New Section | Old Section |
|---|---|---|---|
| House property loss | 8 assessment years | 110 | 71B |
| Capital gains loss (LTCL and STCL) | 8 assessment years | 111 | 74 |
| Business loss (other than speculation/specified) | 8 assessment years | 112 | 72 |
| Speculation business loss | 4 assessment years | 113 | 73 |
| Specified business loss (Section 36(1)(viii) activities) | Indefinite (no time limit) | 114 | 73A |
| Race horse activity loss | 4 assessment years | 115 | 74A |

### Carry-Forward Conditions

| Condition | New Section | Position |
|---|---|---|
| Brought-forward business loss set off only against business income (not salary, HP, CG, or other sources) | 112 | Unchanged |
| Brought-forward capital loss: LTCL only against LTCG; STCL against any capital gain | 111 | Unchanged |
| Brought-forward speculation loss: only against speculation income | 113 | Unchanged |
| Brought-forward specified business loss: only against specified business income | 114 | Unchanged |
| Brought-forward race horse loss: only against race horse income | 115 | Unchanged |
| HP loss: set off against any income in subsequent years (no inter-head restriction on brought-forward HP loss) | 110 | Unchanged |

### Timely Filing Requirement (Section 121)

Section 121 (old Section 80) continues to require that a return of income be filed within the due date under Section 263(1) (old Section 139(1)) for carry-forward of business loss, capital loss, speculation loss, and specified business loss. House property loss carry-forward does not require timely filing — this exception also continues unchanged.

### Restriction on Set-Off Against Undisclosed Income (Section 120)

Section 120 (old Section 79A, inserted by Finance Act 2024) restricts set-off of any brought-forward loss or unabsorbed depreciation against undisclosed income detected during search, survey, or requisition proceedings. This provision, operative from AY 2025-26 under the 1961 Act, is carried forward as Section 120 without modification.

---

## Transition: Pre-2026 Losses Under the New Act

### Section 536(2)(m) and (n)

The transition provisions explicitly address the fate of losses that arose under the 1961 Act but remain unabsorbed as of 31 March 2026:

- **Section 536(2)(m)** — any loss that was eligible for carry-forward under the 1961 Act as on 31 March 2026 shall be deemed to be a loss under the corresponding provision of the 2025 Act. The remaining carry-forward period continues as if the loss had arisen under the new Act.

- **Section 536(2)(n)** — unabsorbed depreciation under old Section 32(2) carries forward under the corresponding provision of the 2025 Act.

### Practical Effect

A business loss arising in AY 2022-23 under old Section 72 has 8 years of carry-forward — i.e., it can be set off up to AY 2030-31. Under the transition provisions, this loss continues to be available for set off under new Section 112, and the 8-year window is computed from the original year of loss. The transition does not reset or extend the carry-forward period.

## Practical Impact

### Citation Updates Only

| Old Citation | New Citation |
|---|---|
| Section 71B (HP loss) | **Section 110** |
| Section 74 (CG loss) | **Section 111** |
| Section 72 (business loss) | **Section 112** |
| Section 73 (speculation loss) | **Section 113** |
| Section 73A (specified business loss) | **Section 114** |
| Section 74A (race horse loss) | **Section 115** |
| Section 79A (undisclosed income restriction) | **Section 120** |
| Section 80 (timely filing) | **Section 121** |

### Pre-2026 Loss Records

Firms maintaining loss carry-forward registers should annotate existing entries with the new section references. A loss recorded under "Section 72 — AY 2023-24" becomes "Section 112 (old Section 72) — AY 2023-24" for returns filed under the 2025 Act. The remaining carry-forward window is unchanged.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 71B, 72, 73, 73A, 74, 74A --> Sections 110-115 — all carry-forward periods and conditions unchanged.**
- **No carry-forward period is altered by the 2025 Act: 8 years (HP, CG, business), 4 years (speculation, race horse), indefinite (specified business).**
- **Section 121 (old 80): timely filing requirement for carry-forward continues unchanged.**
- **Section 120 (old 79A): restriction on set-off against undisclosed income — carried forward from Finance Act 2024.**
- **Transition provisions (Section 536(2)(m) and (n)) ensure pre-2026 losses carry forward seamlessly — remaining period runs from original year of loss, not reset.**
