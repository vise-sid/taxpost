# Unit 11.3: Special Situations (Amalgamation, Demerger, Change in Constitution)

## Overview

Certain business reorganisations — amalgamation, demerger, conversion, succession — require special provisions to determine whether accumulated losses of the predecessor entity survive. The 2025 Act carries forward the existing framework in Sections 116 through 119 (old Sections 72A, 72AA, and 79), but introduces two genuine changes: a **new dedicated section for co-operative bank reorganisation** (Section 118) and a **potentially significant rewording of the shareholding continuity test** in Section 119 (old Section 79).

## Old --> New Section Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 72A | **116** | Carry forward of accumulated loss and unabsorbed depreciation in amalgamation and demerger | Restructured |
| 72AA | **117** | Carry forward of accumulated loss in business reorganisation of banking companies | Renumbered only |
| *(No separate section)* | **118** | Carry forward of accumulated loss in business reorganisation of co-operative banks | **Genuinely changed — new section** |
| 79 | **119** | Carry forward and set off of losses in case of change in shareholding | **Genuinely changed — wording altered** |
| 80 | **121** | Loss return to be filed within due date | Renumbered only |

## What Genuinely Changed (2025 Act)

### 1. Section 118 — Co-operative Bank Business Reorganisation (New Provision)

Under the 1961 Act, business reorganisation of co-operative banks was addressed by extending Section 72AA (banking companies) through amendments and interpretive guidance. The 2025 Act carves out a **standalone Section 118** dedicated to co-operative bank reorganisation.

**What this means:** Co-operative bank reorganisations — mergers, amalgamations, and reconstructions directed by the Reserve Bank of India or the relevant Registrar of Co-operative Societies — now have their own dedicated provision for carry forward of accumulated losses and unabsorbed depreciation. The conditions and framework are tailored to the co-operative banking structure rather than being borrowed from the commercial banking provision.

**Why it matters:** Co-operative banks operate under a distinct regulatory and governance framework (state co-operative societies acts, multi-state co-operative societies acts, RBI directions). A dedicated section eliminates the interpretive friction that arose from applying Section 72AA's banking company framework to co-operative bank reorganisations.

### 2. Section 119 — Shareholding Continuity Test (Rewording)

Old Section 79 restricted carry-forward of losses for companies (not being companies in which the public are substantially interested) where a change in shareholding occurred. The test required that shares carrying at least 51% of the voting power on the last day of the year in which the loss was incurred must be beneficially held by the same **"persons"** (plural) on the last day of the year in which the loss is to be set off.

**Section 119 of the 2025 Act changes "persons" to "the person" (singular).**

**Interpretive consequence:** Under old Section 79, a group of shareholders could collectively satisfy the 51% continuity requirement. For example, if three individuals held 20%, 18%, and 15% respectively in both the loss year and the set-off year, they collectively met the 51% threshold. Under the new wording, it is arguable that **a single beneficial owner must hold at least 51%** — not a group collectively. If this interpretation prevails:

- A closely-held company with three equal shareholders (33% each) could lose the ability to carry forward losses even if all three remain unchanged, because no single person holds 51%.
- Loss carry-forward planning for private companies would need to focus on concentration of shareholding, not merely continuity of the group.

**Caveat:** This is a wording change whose full effect will depend on CBDT circulars and judicial interpretation. It is possible that the singular "person" will be read to include the plural under the General Clauses Act, 1897 (Section 13(2)). However, the deliberate change from plural to singular in a provision that has existed since 1961 suggests a conscious legislative choice. Practitioners should flag this in their advisory until clarity emerges.

## What Stayed the Same

### Amalgamation and Demerger (Section 116)

| Rule | Position |
|---|---|
| Accumulated loss and unabsorbed depreciation of amalgamating company carry forward to amalgamated company | Unchanged |
| Conditions: industrial undertaking, revival scheme, continuity of business for minimum period | Unchanged |
| Demerger: losses and depreciation attributable to the demerged undertaking carry to the resulting company | Unchanged |
| Non-attributable losses remain with the demerged company | Unchanged |

> Section 72A(6B) anti-evergreening provision (loss carry-forward limited to remaining period within original 8-year window) — carried forward from Finance Act 2025 — no further change by the 2025 Act.

### Banking Company Reorganisation (Section 117)

Section 117 is a direct renumbering of old Section 72AA. The provision enabling carry forward of accumulated losses on business reorganisation of banking companies (as directed by RBI under the Banking Regulation Act) continues without modification.

### Timely Filing (Section 121)

Section 121 (old Section 80) continues to require filing within the due date for carry-forward eligibility. This applies equally to losses carried forward in reorganisation contexts — the successor entity must file its return within the due date to preserve carry-forward claims inherited from the predecessor.

---

## Practical Impact

### Co-operative Bank Reorganisations (Section 118)

Practitioners advising co-operative banks on RBI-directed mergers or reorganisations should cite Section 118 directly. The pre-2025 practice of relying on Section 72AA by analogy or extension is no longer necessary. Verify that the specific conditions in Section 118 are met — they may differ in detail from those in Section 117 (banking companies).

### Private Company Shareholding Reviews (Section 119)

For closely-held companies with carried-forward losses, the change from "persons" to "the person" warrants immediate review:

1. **Identify companies where no single shareholder holds 51%** — these are at risk if the singular interpretation prevails.
2. **Document the shareholding position** as on the last day of the loss year and the proposed set-off year.
3. **Consider restructuring** to concentrate 51% voting power in a single beneficial owner before the set-off year closes, if commercially feasible.
4. **Monitor CBDT guidance** — a circular or clarification on this point is likely given the interpretive significance.

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 72A --> Section 116 (amalgamation/demerger), Section 72AA --> Section 117 (banking company reorganisation) — restructured, not changed.**
- **GENUINE CHANGE: Section 118 is a new dedicated provision for co-operative bank business reorganisation — previously handled through Section 72AA by extension.**
- **GENUINE CHANGE: Section 119 (old Section 79) changes "persons" (plural) to "the person" (singular) in the 51% shareholding continuity test — could require a single beneficial owner to hold 51%, not a group collectively. Major interpretive consequence for closely-held companies.**
- **Anti-evergreening (old Section 72A(6B)) — carried forward from Finance Act 2025 — remaining period within original 8-year window only.**
- **Section 121 (old 80): timely filing requirement unchanged and applies to successor entities in reorganisations.**
