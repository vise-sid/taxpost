# Unit 19.4: Key Forms

## Overview

The IT Rules, 2026 reduce the total number of prescribed forms from **399 to 190** — a 52% reduction. The reduction is achieved through three mechanisms: merging overlapping forms into single consolidated forms, dropping forms linked to defunct or sunset provisions, and eliminating paper-specific forms where digital filing is now mandatory. For practitioners, this unit provides the critical old-to-new form mapping and highlights the genuinely new consolidations.

### Self-Declaration Forms

| Old Form (1962 Rules) | New Form (2026 Rules) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form 15G + Form 15H | **Form 121** (merged) | Self-declaration for non-deduction of TDS on interest/dividend | **Genuinely changed — merger** |

**Form 121 merger explained:** Under the 1962 Rules, two separate forms existed — Form 15G for persons below 60 years and Form 15H for senior citizens (60+). The IT Rules, 2026 merge these into a single **Form 121** with a unified format. The eligibility conditions (estimated total income below taxable limit for 15G; senior citizen status for 15H) are now captured as fields within the consolidated form rather than requiring separate form selection.

### Tax Audit Reports

| Old Form (1962 Rules) | New Form (2026 Rules) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Form 3CA + Form 3CB + Form 3CD | **Form 26** (consolidated) | Tax audit report and particulars | **Genuinely changed — consolidation** |

**Form 26 consolidation explained:** Under the 1962 Rules, tax audit required:
- **Form 3CA** — audit report where accounts were already audited under another law (e.g., Companies Act)
- **Form 3CB** — audit report where accounts were not otherwise audited
- **Form 3CD** — statement of particulars (the detailed annexure)

The IT Rules, 2026 consolidate all three into a single **Form 26**. The distinction between previously-audited and not-previously-audited accounts is now handled within the form structure rather than requiring separate form selection. Form 3CD's detailed particulars are incorporated as part of Form 26.

## ITR Form Changes

The return of income forms under the 2026 Rules incorporate the following changes:

### ITR-1 (Sahaj)

| Aspect | Old Position | New Position | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Number of house properties | 1 house property only | **Up to 2 house properties** | Genuinely changed |

ITR-1 previously restricted eligibility to taxpayers with income from a single house property. The revised ITR-1 now permits taxpayers with income from **up to 2 house properties** to file using this simplified form. This aligns with the Act's recognition of two self-occupied properties and reduces the number of taxpayers forced to use the more detailed ITR-2.

### ITR-4 (Sugam)

The eligibility criteria for ITR-4 (used by taxpayers opting for presumptive taxation) have been **tightened**. Taxpayers with certain categories of income or deductions that were previously permitted under ITR-4 may now be required to file ITR-3 or ITR-5.

### ITR-U to ITR-UN

| Aspect | Old Position | New Position | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Updated return form name | ITR-U | **ITR-UN** | Renamed |

The updated return form — used for filing returns after the due date under the updated return provisions — has been renamed from ITR-U to **ITR-UN** (Updated — New). The substantive provisions governing updated returns remain as per the Act.

## Genuinely New Features

Three changes in the forms framework represent genuine new introductions under the IT Rules, 2026:

### 1. Form 121 — Merger of 15G and 15H

A single declaration form replaces two separate forms. Recipients of interest, dividend, or similar income who are eligible for non-deduction of TDS file one form regardless of age.

### 2. Form 26 — Consolidation of Tax Audit Forms

Three forms (3CA, 3CB, 3CD) collapse into one. The audit report and statement of particulars are now a single filing. This eliminates the practitioner's preliminary decision of which audit report form to use.

### 3. Digital Filing Mandate

The IT Rules, 2026 mandate electronic filing for all prescribed forms. Paper filing is permitted **only for super senior citizens (age 80 and above)** who are not required to get their accounts audited. This extends the digitisation trend that has been progressing incrementally — the 2026 Rules make it a comprehensive default.

| Filing Mode | Who Can Use It |
|---|---|
| Electronic (mandatory) | All taxpayers, deductors, and collectors |
| Paper (exception) | Super senior citizens (80+) not subject to audit |

## Key Takeaways

- 399 forms reduced to 190 (52% reduction) through mergers, deletions, and rationalisation
- Form 15G and 15H merged into **Form 121** — single self-declaration regardless of age
- Form 3CA, 3CB, and 3CD consolidated into **Form 26** — single tax audit filing
- ITR-1 now allows income from up to 2 house properties (was 1)
- ITR-U renamed to ITR-UN
- Digital filing mandatory for all; paper filing only for super senior citizens (80+) not subject to audit
- All form number references in software, templates, and SOPs require updating from 1 April 2026
