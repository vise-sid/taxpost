# Unit 19.2: Salary Rules — Perquisites & Allowances

## Overview

The Income Tax Rules, 2026 introduce significant monetary revisions to salary-related rules — perquisite valuation limits, HRA city classification, and prescribed allowance thresholds. These are **Rules 2026 changes, not Income Tax Act 2025 changes**. The Act delegates valuation to the Rules via "as may be prescribed" language; the Rules 2026 exercise that delegation with revised numbers.

Three old rules map to three new rules:
- **Rule 3 to Rule 15** — perquisite valuation (revised limits)
- **Rule 2A to Rule 279** — HRA exemption (expanded city list)
- **Rule 2BB to Rule 280** — prescribed allowances (revised limits)

> The architecture of delegation is unchanged. Valuation remains in the Rules, not the Act. Future revisions require only a CBDT notification, not a Finance Bill.

## Old to New Rule Mapping

| Old Rule (1962) | New Rule (2026) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rule 3 | **Rule 15** | Perquisite valuation — monetary limits | Genuinely changed (IT Rules 2026) |
| Rule 2A | **Rule 279** | HRA exemption — city classification | Genuinely changed (IT Rules 2026) |
| Rule 2BB | **Rule 280** | Prescribed allowances — children education, hostel | Genuinely changed (IT Rules 2026) |

## Rule 3 to Rule 15: Perquisite Valuation — Revised Limits

### Accommodation (Employer-Provided)

The percentage of salary treated as taxable perquisite for employer-provided accommodation has been reduced across all city tiers:

| City Classification | Old Rate (Rule 3) | New Rate (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Population > 25 lakh | 15% of salary | **10% of salary** | -5 percentage points |
| Population 10-25 lakh | 10% of salary | **7.5% of salary** | -2.5 percentage points |
| Population < 10 lakh | 7.5% of salary | **5% of salary** | -2.5 percentage points |

The reduction benefits employees housed in employer-provided accommodation by lowering the deemed perquisite value. The population-based tiering structure itself is retained.

### Meals and Food

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 3) | New Limit (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax-free meal value per meal | Rs 50/meal | **Rs 200/meal** | +300% |

Meals valued at or below Rs 200 per meal — whether provided in office, through a canteen, or via non-transferable meal vouchers — are not treated as a perquisite. The Rs 50 limit had remained unchanged since its original notification and was widely regarded as obsolete.

### Education

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 3) | New Limit (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free education in employer-maintained school | Rs 1,000/month/child | **Rs 3,000/month/child** | +200% |

Where the employer maintains an educational institution and provides free or concessional education to the employee's children, no perquisite value arises if the cost does not exceed Rs 3,000 per month per child.

### Gifts

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 3) | New Limit (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual gift exemption threshold | Rs 5,000/year | **Rs 15,000/year** | +200% |

Gifts (in cash or kind) from the employer aggregating up to Rs 15,000 in a tax year are excluded from perquisite valuation. The entire amount becomes taxable once the aggregate exceeds Rs 15,000.

### Medical Loans

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 3) | New Limit (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer medical loan exemption | Not available | **Up to Rs 2,00,000** | New provision |

Loans advanced by the employer for medical treatment of the employee or family members are exempt from interest-free loan perquisite treatment up to Rs 2,00,000. This is a new exemption introduced by the IT Rules, 2026 — no equivalent existed under Rule 3 of the 1962 Rules.

## Rule 2A to Rule 279: HRA — Expanded City List

### The Change

Under old Rule 2A, only **four cities** qualified for the higher HRA exemption computation (50% of salary instead of 40%): Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, and Chennai.

Rule 279 of the IT Rules 2026 expands this list to **eight cities**:

| 50% HRA Cities — Old (Rule 2A) | 50% HRA Cities — New (Rule 279) |
|---|---|
| Mumbai | Mumbai |
| Delhi | Delhi |
| Kolkata | Kolkata |
| Chennai | Chennai |
| — | **Bengaluru** (added) |
| — | **Pune** (added) |
| — | **Hyderabad** (added) |
| — | **Ahmedabad** (added) |

### Practical Impact

Employees residing in Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, or Ahmedabad who receive HRA will now be entitled to claim exemption computed at 50% of salary (instead of 40%). For an employee earning a salary of Rs 12,00,000 per annum, this translates to an additional Rs 1,20,000 in the exemption computation base — a meaningful difference in the final taxable HRA amount.

Payroll systems must update city classification logic to include the four new cities in the higher exemption tier from Tax Year 2026-27.

> The HRA exemption methodology itself (least of three: actual HRA received, rent paid minus 10% of salary, 50%/40% of salary) is unchanged. Only the city classification has been expanded.

## Rule 2BB to Rule 280: Prescribed Allowances — Revised Limits

### Children Education Allowance

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 2BB) | New Limit (Rule 280) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Children education allowance | Rs 100/month/child (max 2 children) | **Rs 3,000/month/child** (max 2 children) | +2,900% |

The Rs 100 per month limit had been unchanged since 1997 and was universally regarded as symbolic rather than functional. The revision to Rs 3,000 per month per child represents a substantive increase that restores practical relevance to this allowance.

### Hostel Expenditure Allowance

| Item | Old Limit (Rule 2BB) | New Limit (Rule 280) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hostel expenditure allowance | Rs 300/month/child (max 2 children) | **Rs 9,000/month/child** (max 2 children) | +2,900% |

The hostel allowance follows the same pattern — a long-frozen limit revised to reflect contemporary costs.

### What Stayed the Same

The two-child limit for both education and hostel allowances is retained. The allowance categories themselves (children education, hostel expenditure, transport) are unchanged — only the monetary ceilings have been revised.

---

## Critical Attribution Note

All changes described in this unit are **IT Rules 2026 changes** — they flow from CBDT's notification of the Income Tax Rules, 2026, not from the Income Tax Act, 2025 itself. The Act provides the enabling framework ("as may be prescribed"); the Rules provide the numbers.

This distinction matters because:
- Rules can be amended by CBDT notification without Parliamentary approval
- Future revisions to these limits will follow the same Rules-amendment route
- In professional communications, the correct citation is "Rule 15/279/280 of the IT Rules, 2026" — not "Section X of the IT Act, 2025"

---

## Key Takeaways

- Perquisite valuation limits comprehensively revised under Rule 15 (replacing Rule 3) — accommodation rates reduced, meals Rs 50 to Rs 200, education Rs 1,000 to Rs 3,000, gifts Rs 5,000 to Rs 15,000
- Medical loan exemption up to Rs 2,00,000 is a new provision under Rule 15
- HRA 50% cities expanded from 4 to 8 under Rule 279 (replacing Rule 2A) — Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad added
- Children education allowance Rs 100 to Rs 3,000 and hostel Rs 300 to Rs 9,000 under Rule 280 (replacing Rule 2BB)
- All changes are IT Rules 2026 changes (CBDT notification), not IT Act 2025 changes
- Payroll systems require configuration updates for accommodation percentages, meal limits, gift thresholds, HRA city classification, and allowance ceilings
