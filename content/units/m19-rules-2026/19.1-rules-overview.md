# Unit 19.1: Rules Overview (511 Rules, 399 Forms to 333 Rules, 190 Forms)

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 rewrites the primary legislation. The **Income Tax Rules, 2026** — notified vide **CBDT Notification No. 22/2026 dated 20 March 2026**, effective 1 April 2026 — complete the overhaul by replacing the Income Tax Rules, 1962. Where the 1962 Rules had accumulated 511 rules and 399 forms over six decades of amendments, the 2026 Rules consolidate these into **333 rules and 190 forms** — a 35% reduction in rules and a 52% reduction in forms.

The consolidation philosophy mirrors the Act itself: same substance, fewer pages, clearer organisation. Multiple rules addressing the same subject have been merged, redundant rules have been dropped, and forms have been rationalised by combining overlapping declarations.

> Navigator documents mapping old rules to new rules are available on incometaxindia.gov.in.

| **Forms** | 399 | 190 | 209 forms fewer (52%) |
| **Notification** | — | CBDT Notification No. 22/2026 | 20 March 2026 |
| **Effective date** | — | 1 April 2026 | Applies from Tax Year 2026-27 |

---

## Consolidation Philosophy

The 2026 Rules adopt four structural principles:

1. **Merger of related rules**: Where the 1962 Rules addressed one topic across multiple rules (e.g., separate rules for different allowances), the 2026 Rules consolidate them into a single rule with sub-rules. This eliminates cross-referencing across scattered provisions.

2. **Elimination of redundancies**: Rules that served no current purpose — those linked to sunset provisions, expired incentives, or defunct schemes — have been dropped entirely rather than carried forward as dead text.

3. **Form rationalisation**: The 52% reduction in forms is achieved primarily through mergers. Declarations that previously required separate forms (e.g., Form 15G and Form 15H) are now served by a single consolidated form. Return forms have been streamlined to reduce repetitive disclosure requirements.

4. **Sequential renumbering**: Like the Act, the Rules adopt clean sequential numbering. There are no alphabetical suffixes (Rule 2A, 2B, 2BA, 2BB, 2BC...) — a single topic receives a single rule number.

## How to Use the Navigator

CBDT has published navigator documents on incometaxindia.gov.in that provide:

1. **Rule-to-Rule mapping**: Old rule number to new rule number, with subject description.
2. **Form-to-Form mapping**: Old form number to new form number, indicating which forms have been merged and which have been dropped.
3. **Reverse lookup**: New rule or form number back to the corresponding old provision.

Practitioners should download and bookmark these navigators. During the transition period (Tax Year 2026-27), references in assessment orders, notices, and appellate proceedings will increasingly use the new numbering, and the ability to cross-reference quickly will be essential.

---

## What This Means for Practice

**For compliance professionals:** Every software system, template, and standard operating procedure that references rule numbers or form numbers requires updating. This includes payroll systems (Rule 3 to Rule 15), TDS return filing utilities (Form 24Q to Form 138), and tax audit report generators (Form 3CA/3CB/3CD to Form 26).

**For advisory professionals:** Client-facing letters, opinions, and memoranda citing specific rules must migrate to the new numbering from 1 April 2026. Internal knowledge bases and precedent databases should be tagged with both old and new rule references during the transition.

**For litigation professionals:** Pending proceedings initiated before 1 April 2026 continue under the old rule references. New proceedings from 1 April 2026 use the new numbering. Managing both simultaneously is unavoidable for the next 2-3 years.
