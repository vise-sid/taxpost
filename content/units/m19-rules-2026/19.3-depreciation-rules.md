# Unit 19.3: Depreciation Rules

## Overview

Rule 5 of the Income Tax Rules, 1962 — the provision governing depreciation rates and computation methodology — has been renumbered to **Rule 25 of the IT Rules, 2026**. The depreciation rate table, computation methodology, and asset classification structure are **carried forward without change**. The rates that practitioners have worked with — 5% for residential buildings, 10% for non-residential, 15% for general plant and machinery, 40% for computers — remain identical.

The one genuine Rules 2026 change in the depreciation framework is the introduction of a **40% depreciation cap** for taxpayers opting for concessional tax regimes under Sections 199(3), 200(5), and 202(2) of the IT Act, 2025.

---

## Old to New Rule Mapping

| Old Rule (1962) | New Rule (2026) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rule 5 | **Rule 25** | Depreciation rates, computation, asset blocks | Renumbered; one genuine change (40% cap) |
| Appendix I to Rule 5 | **Appendix I to Rule 25** | WDV depreciation rate table | Carried forward (no rate changes) |
| Appendix II to Rule 5 | **Appendix II to Rule 25** | SLM rates for power generation undertakings | Carried forward |

## Depreciation Rates: All Carried Forward

The following rate table represents the depreciation rates under Rule 25 (formerly Rule 5). **No rate has been changed by the IT Rules, 2026.** These are the same rates that were applicable under the 1962 Rules as of 31 March 2026.

### Buildings

| Asset | WDV Rate | Change Status |
|---|---|---|
| Residential buildings | 5% | Carried forward — no change |
| Non-residential buildings (offices, factories, godowns) | 10% | Carried forward — no change |
| Temporary structures (wooden, not likely to last > 3 years) | 40% | Carried forward — no change |

### Furniture and Fittings

| Asset | WDV Rate | Change Status |
|---|---|---|
| Furniture and fittings | 10% | Carried forward — no change |

### Plant and Machinery

| Asset | WDV Rate | Change Status |
|---|---|---|
| General plant and machinery | 15% | Carried forward — no change |
| Motor vehicles (other than those used in hire business) | 15% | Carried forward — no change |
| Commercial vehicles used in hire business | 30% | Carried forward — no change |
| Computers and computer software | 40% | Carried forward — no change |
| Energy-saving devices (specified) | 40% | Carried forward — no change |
| Electric vehicles | 40% | Carried forward — no change (reflects clean energy policy, introduced by Finance Act, 2019) |
| Air pollution control equipment | 40% | Carried forward — no change |
| Books (annual publications) | 40% | Carried forward — no change |
| Books (not annual publications) — professional libraries | 40% | Carried forward — no change |

### Intangible Assets

| Asset | WDV Rate | Change Status |
|---|---|---|
| Know-how, patents, copyrights, trademarks, licences, franchises, other commercial rights | 25% | Carried forward — no change |

> Goodwill is excluded from depreciable intangible assets. This exclusion was introduced by Finance Act, 2021 (effective AY 2021-22) — carried forward by the 2025 Act, no further change.

## Genuine Rules 2026 Change: 40% Depreciation Cap for Concessional Tax Regimes

### What Changed

Rule 25 of the IT Rules 2026 introduces a **cap of 40% on depreciation rates** for taxpayers who opt for concessional tax regimes under the following provisions of the IT Act, 2025:

| Act Section | Regime | Who It Applies To |
|---|---|---|
| Section 199(3) | Concessional rate for domestic companies | Domestic companies opting for lower corporate tax rate |
| Section 200(5) | Concessional rate for new manufacturing companies | New manufacturing companies opting for lower rate |
| Section 202(2) | New tax regime for individuals/HUFs | Individuals, HUFs, and other non-corporate taxpayers opting for the new regime |

### How the Cap Works

Under the concessional regimes, no asset class may claim depreciation at a rate exceeding 40%. Since the highest standard rate in the depreciation table is already 40% (computers, electric vehicles, energy-saving devices), this cap has **no immediate practical impact on the current rate table**. However, it serves as a legislative ceiling — if CBDT were to notify higher rates for any asset class in the future (e.g., a promotional 60% rate for a new technology category), taxpayers in concessional regimes would be capped at 40%.

### Why It Matters

The 40% cap is a structural design choice tied to the revenue framework of concessional regimes. These regimes offer lower tax rates in exchange for forgoing certain deductions and exemptions. The depreciation cap ensures that accelerated depreciation cannot be used to erode the concessional regime's tax base beyond a defined limit.

For current compliance purposes, no working paper or computation adjustment is required, as no existing rate exceeds 40%. The cap becomes relevant only if future CBDT notifications introduce higher rates.

## 180-Day Rule: Retained

The half-year rule for assets acquired and put to use for less than 180 days during the tax year is retained under Rule 25. Where an asset is acquired and used for less than 180 days in the year of acquisition, depreciation is restricted to 50% of the applicable rate.

| Aspect | Status |
|---|---|
| 180-day threshold | Retained — no change |
| 50% rate for sub-180-day use | Retained — no change |
| "Put to use" requirement | Retained — no change |

---

## Appendix II: SLM Rates for Power Generation

Appendix II to Rule 25 (formerly Appendix II to Rule 5) prescribes straight-line method (SLM) depreciation rates for power generation undertakings eligible under the relevant Act provisions. The SLM rate table is **carried forward without change**.

## Practical Implications

**For compliance:** No depreciation rate requires revision in tax computation software or working papers. The Rule 5 to Rule 25 renumbering affects citations in audit reports, computation statements, and advisory opinions — not the numbers themselves.

**For concessional regime taxpayers:** The 40% cap is a ceiling that does not currently bind. However, it should be documented in the computation file as an applicable constraint. If CBDT notifies higher rates for any asset class in the future, taxpayers in concessional regimes will need to apply the cap.

**For documentation:** Tax audit reports and depreciation schedules should reference "Rule 25 of the IT Rules, 2026" (not "Rule 5 of the IT Rules, 1962") for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Rule 5 (depreciation) renumbered to Rule 25 — rates and methodology carried forward without change
- All standard depreciation rates (5%, 10%, 15%, 25%, 30%, 40%) are unchanged
- Electric vehicles continue at 40% (introduced by Finance Act, 2019 — carried forward)
- Goodwill remains excluded from depreciable intangibles (Finance Act, 2021 — carried forward)
- **Genuine Rules 2026 change:** 40% depreciation cap for taxpayers in concessional tax regimes (Sections 199(3), 200(5), 202(2)) — currently non-binding as no rate exceeds 40%
- 180-day rule and Appendix II (SLM for power generation) retained
- Citation change only: "Rule 25, IT Rules 2026" replaces "Rule 5, IT Rules 1962"
