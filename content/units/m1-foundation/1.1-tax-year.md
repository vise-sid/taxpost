# Unit 1.1: Tax Year

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 replaces the dual concepts of "Previous Year" and "Assessment Year" with a single unified term — **Tax Year**. This is the most fundamental terminology change in the new Act, affecting every return, notice, form, and communication issued from April 1, 2026 onwards. Section 3 has been redefined, Assessment Year has been eliminated from Section 2, and the cascading impact extends to all forms under the new Income Tax Rules, 2026.

---

## Section 3: Previous Year → Tax Year

### Old → New Mapping

| | Old Section 3 (1961 Act) | New Section 3 (2025 Act) |
|---|---|---|
| **Defined term** | "Previous Year" | "Tax Year" |
| **Definition** | Financial year immediately preceding the assessment year | Twelve-month period of the financial year commencing on 1st April |
| **Nature** | Derivative — defined in relation to Assessment Year | Standalone — self-referencing, no dependency on AY |
| **New businesses (sub-section 2)** | First PY = date of setup to March 31 | First Tax Year = date of setup to March 31 |

### What Changed

- **Terminology**: "Previous Year" is replaced by "Tax Year" throughout the Act.
- **Definition structure**: The old definition was derivative — it existed only in relation to Assessment Year. The new definition is standalone.
- **Substance**: Unchanged. The period remains April 1 to March 31. The provision for newly set up businesses (Section 3(2)) is identical — the first tax year runs from the date of setup to the following March 31.

### What Stayed the Same

The underlying concept is identical. A 12-month financial year, April 1 to March 31, during which income is earned and tax liability arises. Only the label changed.

---

## Assessment Year: Eliminated

**Assessment Year is not merely renamed — it is eliminated.** The term does not appear in Section 2 (Definitions) of the 2025 Act, nor anywhere else as a defined concept.

| Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|
| Section 2(9): "Assessment Year" = 12 months commencing April 1 | **No equivalent definition exists** |
| Returns filed "for AY 2027-28" | Returns filed "for Tax Year 2026-27" |
| Assessment conducted "for AY" | Assessment conducted "for Tax Year" |

Where the old Act referenced "assessment year," the new Act uses:
- **"Succeeding tax year"** or **"subsequent tax year"** — for the year following the earning year
- **"Financial year succeeding the relevant tax year"** — for filing deadlines

The practical consequence: returns are now labeled by the earning year, not a separate assessment year. Income earned in Tax Year 2026-27 → return filed for Tax Year 2026-27.

| Old Terminology | New Terminology |
|---|---|
| AY 2027-28 | **Tax Year 2026-27** |
| PY 2025-26 / AY 2026-27 | Last year under old Act — old terminology applies |
| "Return for AY 2027-28" | "Return for Tax Year 2026-27" |

---

## Section 4: Charge of Income Tax

### Old → New Mapping

| Old Section 4 | New Section 4 |
|---|---|
| 2 sub-sections + 1 proviso | **5 sub-sections**, no provisos |

### What Changed

The structure was simplified. The old Section 4 combined the charge, exceptions for other-period income, and TDS/advance tax provisions into two dense sub-sections with a proviso. The new version separates each into a distinct sub-section:

| Sub-section | Coverage |
|---|---|
| 4(1) | Charge at rates enacted by Central Act for the tax year |
| 4(2) | Charge on total income of the tax year |
| 4(3) | "Income-tax" includes additional income-tax by whatever name |
| 4(4) | Income of a period other than the tax year — charged accordingly |
| 4(5) | TDS, TCS, and advance tax as provided under the Act |

### What Stayed the Same

The charging mechanism is substantively identical. No change in what is charged, how it is charged, or at what rates. This is a structural reorganization only.

---

## Transition: No Missing Year

| Period | Governed By | Terminology |
|---|---|---|
| Income up to March 31, 2026 | Income Tax Act, 1961 | Previous Year / Assessment Year |
| Income from April 1, 2026 | Income Tax Act, 2025 | Tax Year |

There is no gap between the two Acts. The handover is seamless.

### Section 536: Repeal and Savings

**Section 536(1):** The Income-tax Act, 1961 is hereby repealed.

**Section 536(2)** preserves continuity for all in-flight matters:
- Pending assessments, appeals, reassessments, and penalty proceedings continue under the 1961 Act
- Rights, privileges, obligations, and liabilities acquired under the old Act are preserved
- Elections, declarations, and options made under the old Act remain valid
- Loss carry-forwards from earlier years are intact
- Recovery of amounts due under the old Act continues

**Section 536(3)** provides the critical bridge: where the 2025 Act refers to any tax year commencing on or before 1st April, 2025, the reference shall be construed as a reference to the corresponding "previous year" under the repealed Act.

This ensures that lookback calculations (RNOR's 10-year test, loss carry-forwards originating under the old Act) work seamlessly across the transition.

### Which Act Applies When

| Situation | Applicable Act | Terminology |
|---|---|---|
| Assessment for FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26) | 1961 Act | Previous Year / Assessment Year |
| Return for FY 2025-26 (filed July 2026) | 1961 Act | Previous Year / Assessment Year |
| Return for Tax Year 2026-27 (filed July 2027) | 2025 Act | Tax Year |
| Pending appeal from AY 2023-24 | 1961 Act | Assessment Year |
| Scrutiny notice for Tax Year 2026-27 | 2025 Act | Tax Year |

The governing principle: the Act under which the income was earned determines the applicable law and terminology.

## Form Changes Under Income Tax Rules, 2026

The terminology change cascades into all prescribed forms:

| Old Form | New Form | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Form 16 | **Form 130** | Salary TDS certificate |
| Forms 15G / 15H | **Form 121** (merged) | Declaration for no TDS deduction on interest |
| Form 26AS | **Form 168** | Annual tax statement |
| Forms 3CA, 3CB, 3CD | **Form 26** (merged) | Tax audit report — single consolidated form |

### Rules and Forms Simplification

| Metric | Old (IT Rules, 1962) | New (IT Rules, 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Total rules | 511 | **333** |
| Total forms | 399 | **~190** |

All forms under the new Rules reference "Tax Year" in place of "Assessment Year" and "Financial Year."

### Practical Impact

- All TDS certificates (Form 130, replacing Form 16) issued for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards must use the new form numbers and "Tax Year" terminology.
- The merger of Forms 15G and 15H into Form 121 eliminates the distinction between senior citizens and others for the declaration form.
- The consolidation of three tax audit forms (3CA, 3CB, 3CD) into a single Form 26 simplifies the audit reporting process.
- Templates, software, and standard operating procedures referencing old form numbers require updating before April 1, 2026.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **"Previous Year" → "Tax Year"** (Section 3) — same period, standalone definition without dependency on Assessment Year
- **"Assessment Year" → eliminated** — not defined anywhere in the 2025 Act; replaced by "succeeding tax year" where needed
- **Section 4** restructured from 2 sub-sections + proviso to 5 clear sub-sections — same charging mechanism
- **Section 536(3)** bridges lookback calculations across old/new Acts, ensuring seamless continuity
- **No missing year** — clean handover at April 1, 2026
- **Form changes**: Form 16→130, 15G/15H→121 (merged), 26AS→168, 3CA/3CB/3CD→Form 26 (merged)
- **Rules simplified**: 511→333 rules, 399→~190 forms
- **Pending proceedings** under the 1961 Act continue with old terminology per Section 536(2)
