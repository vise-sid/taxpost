# Unit 1.3: Scope of Total Income

## Overview

Section 5 (Scope of Total Income) is substantively unchanged — the same residential-status-based framework applies. However, **Section 9 (Income Deemed to Accrue or Arise in India) contains two genuine substantive changes**: the removal of "reasonably" from the business connection attribution test, and the introduction of an export exclusion for Significant Economic Presence (SEP). Additionally, the sections in this neighborhood (7 through 11) have been significantly reshuffled — most notably, **new Section 10 covers the Portuguese Civil Code provision (old Section 5A), not exemptions.** Old Section 10 (exemptions) is now Section 11.

---

## Section 5: Scope of Total Income — No Substantive Change

### Old → New Mapping

**Section 5 → Section 5** — same section number retained.

| Residential Status | Scope of Taxable Income | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| ROR | All worldwide income | No |
| RNOR | Indian income + foreign business/profession controlled from India | No |
| NR | Only Indian-source income | No |

The only modification is the replacement of "previous year" with "tax year" throughout and the RNOR cross-reference update from old Section 6(6) to Section 6(13).

---

## Section 9: Income Deemed to Accrue or Arise in India

### Structural Change

Old Section 9 contained provisos and explanations in a complex nested structure. The 2025 Act reorganizes it into **12 clean sub-sections** (9(1) through 9(12)), converting all provisos and explanations into independently readable sub-sections.

### Substantive Change #1: Attribution Standard

| Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|
| "only such part of the income as is **reasonably attributable** to the operations carried out in India" | "only so much of the income that is **attributable** to operations carried out in India" |

The word **"reasonably"** has been removed. This is the most significant substantive change in Module 1.

**Impact**: The removal of "reasonably" tightens the attribution standard for business connection income. Under the old Act, "reasonably" introduced a degree of subjectivity in determining what portion of income relates to Indian operations. The new wording makes the test potentially more objective and removes the obligation on the tax authority to demonstrate the "reasonableness" of attribution.

This change affects all non-resident assessees with business connections in India. Where DTAAs are in force, treaty protections continue to apply, but the domestic law attribution standard is now stricter.

### Substantive Change #2: SEP Export Exclusion (New)

The 2025 Act introduces a new proviso to the Significant Economic Presence (SEP) provision:

> "Transactions or activities which are confined to the purchase of goods in India for the purpose of export shall not constitute significant economic presence in India."

Under the old Act, the business connection provision (Explanation 1(b)) already excluded export-oriented purchases from constituting a business connection. However, the SEP provision (Explanation 2A) contained **no such exclusion** — creating an asymmetry. The 2025 Act aligns the two by extending the export exclusion to SEP.

Non-residents purchasing goods in India purely for export now have explicit statutory protection from both business connection and SEP provisions.

### Structural Change: SEP and Business Connection Merger

Under the old Act, business connection and SEP had separate attribution rules, with SEP specifically excluded from the business connection attribution framework. The 2025 Act merges both provisions under a uniform attribution framework.

## Section Reshuffling: 5A Through 10

This is the area most likely to cause citation errors during the transition. The sections between 5 and 11 have been significantly rearranged:

| Old Section | New Section | Topic | Nature of Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | 5 | Scope of total income | Renumbered only |
| **5A** | **10** | Portuguese Civil Code apportionment | Renumbered |
| 7 | 7(1) | Income deemed to be received | Renumbered only |
| 9 | 9 (12 sub-sections) | Income deemed to accrue in India | **Substantively changed** |
| **9A** | **9(12) + Schedule I** | Fund manager regime | Absorbed into Section 9 |
| **9B** | **8** | Income on dissolution/reconstitution | Renumbered |
| **10** | **11** + Schedules II-VIII | Exempt income | Restructured |

### The Section 10 Trap

New Section 10 of the 2025 Act covers the **Portuguese Civil Code apportionment** provision (old Section 5A) — applicable to spouses in Goa, Dadra & Nagar Haveli, and Daman & Diu under the community property system.

**New Section 10 ≠ Old Section 10.** Old Section 10 (exempt income) is now **Section 11** read with Schedules II-VIII.

| Looking For | Incorrect Reference | Correct Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Exempt income (HRA, gratuity, agricultural income) | Section 10 | **Section 11** + Schedules II-VIII |
| Portuguese Civil Code apportionment | Section 5A | **Section 10** |
| Fund manager safe harbour | Section 9A | **Section 9(12)** + Schedule I |
| Dissolution/reconstitution income | Section 9B | **Section 8** |

Any opinion or filing citing "Section 10" for exempt income under the 2025 Act would be citing the Portuguese Civil Code provision — an incorrect reference.

---

## Section 9(12) + Schedule I: Fund Manager Regime

Old Section 9A has been absorbed into **Section 9(12)**, with the detailed conditions moved to **Schedule I** of the 2025 Act.

The provision is unchanged: fund management activities by an eligible fund manager on behalf of an eligible investment fund do not constitute business connection in India, provided Schedule I conditions are satisfied.

**Schedule I conditions** (identical to old Section 9A):
- Fund established solely for pooling funds and investment
- Fund resident in a country with DTAA (Section 159) or notified country
- Fund manager not a "connected person" merely by virtue of fund management
- Minimum remuneration standards (0.10% of AUM for Category-I FPI, or other prescribed rates)
- Temporary non-compliance of up to 90 days does not disqualify

---

## Section 8: Dissolution/Reconstitution (Old Section 9B)

Old Section 9B → **new Section 8**. Covers income arising when a partner or member receives capital asset or stock-in-trade on dissolution or reconstitution of a firm, AOP, or BOI.

The provision is substantively unchanged: the specified entity is deemed to have transferred the asset at fair market value. Income is taxable as capital gains (for capital assets) or business income (for stock-in-trade).

---

## Section 7: Income Deemed to be Received — No Change

Section 7 retains the same section number. Covers employer contributions to recognized provident fund, approved superannuation fund, and NPS exceeding prescribed limits. The ₹7.5 lakh aggregate cap for employer EPF + NPS + superannuation contributions continues unchanged.

## Practical Impact

- **Non-resident advisory**: The "attributable" (replacing "reasonably attributable") standard requires review of all business connection opinions. The attribution analysis framework should be updated to reflect the stricter test.
- **SEP advisory**: Non-resident clients purchasing goods in India for export should be informed of the explicit SEP exclusion — this protection should be documented in advisory letters.
- **Template updates** — the following find-and-replace operations are required:
  - "Section 10" for exemptions → **"Section 11"**
  - "Section 9A" for fund manager → **"Section 9(12) read with Schedule I"**
  - "Section 9B" for dissolution → **"Section 8"**
  - "Section 5A" for Portuguese Civil Code → **"Section 10"**
- **Training**: The Section 10 reassignment is the highest-risk citation error in the transition period.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 5** (Scope): No substantive change — "previous year" → "tax year" only
- **Section 9** contains **two substantive changes**:
  1. "Reasonably attributable" → "attributable" — tighter business connection attribution standard
  2. SEP export exclusion added — purchases in India for export now explicitly excluded from SEP
- **Section 10 trap**: New Section 10 = Portuguese Civil Code (old 5A), not exemptions. Old Section 10 exemptions → Section 11 + Schedules II-VIII
- **Section 9 restructured**: Provisos and explanations converted into 12 clean sub-sections
- **Old 9A → Section 9(12) + Schedule I** (fund manager regime, conditions unchanged)
- **Old 9B → Section 8** (dissolution/reconstitution, rules unchanged)
- **Section 536(3)** bridges "tax year" references to corresponding "previous year" for lookback calculations
