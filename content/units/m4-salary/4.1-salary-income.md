# Unit 4.1: Salary Income

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 consolidates all salary-related provisions into five contiguous sections (15 through 19), replacing the scattered architecture of the 1961 Act where salary income was governed by Sections 15, 16, and 17(1)-(3), with exemptions buried across dozens of Section 10 sub-clauses. The most significant structural change is Section 19 (Deductions from Salaries), which merges old Section 16 deductions with retirement benefit exemptions formerly housed in Section 10(10), 10(10A), 10(10AA), 10(10B), and 10(10C) into a single table of 14 deduction categories. The basis of charge remains unchanged, but the perquisite provisions introduce delegated threshold-setting and a new commuting cost exclusion.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15 | **15** | Basis of charge — Salaries | Unchanged |
| 17(1) | **16** | "Salary" defined | Moved + minor addition |
| 17(2) | **17** | Perquisite defined | Moved + substantive changes |
| 17(3) | **18** | Profits in lieu of salary | Moved + reference updates |
| 16 | **19** | Deductions from salaries | Moved + major restructuring |
| 10(10), 10(10A), 10(10AA), 10(10B), 10(10C) | **19 (Table)** | Retirement benefit exemptions | Merged into Section 19 |

### Structural Observation

Under the 1961 Act, a practitioner computing salary income had to navigate Section 15 (charge), Section 16 (deductions), Section 17(1)-(3) (definitions of salary, perquisite, profits in lieu), and then cross-reference at least five sub-clauses of Section 10 for exemptions on gratuity, pension commutation, leave encashment, retrenchment compensation, and VRS. The 2025 Act places the entire computation — charge, definitions, deductions, and exemptions — within a single block of five consecutive sections.

---

## What Changed

### Section 15: Basis of Charge — No Change

Salary is chargeable on a "due" or "receipt" basis, whichever is earlier. This provision carries over without modification.

### Section 16: Salary Defined (Old Section 17(1))

The definition now lists 12 categories of receipts that constitute "salary." The notable addition:

| Addition | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Agniveer Corpus Fund contributions** | Agniveer Corpus Fund contributions were introduced by Finance Act 2022 (inserting Section 80CCH and Section 10(12C) into the 1961 Act). The 2025 Act relocates this provision into the salary definition under Section 16. |

The remaining 11 categories replicate old Section 17(1) without substantive change.

### Section 17: Perquisite Defined (Old Section 17(2))

Nine categories, (a) through (i). Three significant changes:

| Change | Old Position | New Position |
|---|---|---|
| **Delegated thresholds** | Fixed monetary limits specified in the Act itself | Replaced with "such amount as may be prescribed" — enables CBDT to update thresholds via Rules without amending the Act |
| **Commuting cost exclusion** | No specific exclusion | Section 17(2)(e): employer-paid home-to-office commuting costs are excluded from taxable perquisites |
| **Family definition** | Defined within the section | Now references Schedule III of the Act |

**Retained without change:**
- The Rs 7,50,000 aggregate cap on employer contributions to PF, NPS, and superannuation funds
- Valuation methodology for accommodation, motor car, and other specified perquisites (subject to Rules)

### Section 18: Profits in Lieu of Salary (Old Section 17(3))

Substantively similar to the old provision. The key technical change is that exclusions now reference **Schedule II** and **Schedule III table entries** of the 2025 Act instead of the old Section 10 sub-clauses. No change in scope or quantum of what constitutes profits in lieu of salary.

### Section 19: Deductions from Salaries (Old Section 16 + Section 10 Exemptions) — Most Significant Change

This is the provision where the 2025 Act departs most from the old structure. Section 19 uses a **table format** listing 14 deduction categories, consolidating:
- Old Section 16 deductions (standard deduction, entertainment allowance, professional tax)
- Old Section 10(10) — gratuity exemptions
- Old Section 10(10A) — commuted pension exemptions
- Old Section 10(10AA) — leave encashment exemptions
- Old Section 10(10B) — retrenchment compensation exemptions
- Old Section 10(10C) — VRS/voluntary termination exemptions

#### Section 19 — Deduction Table

| S. No. | Category | Deduction Amount |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Professional tax | Full deduction of amount paid |
| 2 | Standard deduction | Rs 75,000 (new regime / Section 202) or Rs 50,000 (old regime) |
| 3 | Entertainment allowance (government employees) | Lower of: actual allowance, 20% of salary, or Rs 5,000 |
| 4 | Death-cum-retirement gratuity (government employees) | Entire amount exempt |
| 5 | Defence retiring gratuity | Full amount exempt |
| 6 | Gratuity under Payment of Gratuity Act | As per Section 4(2) / 4(3) of the PG Act |
| 7 | Other gratuity (non-PG Act, non-government) | Least of: actual received, notified limit, or half-month salary multiplied by completed years of service |
| 8 | Pension commutation (government employees) | Entire commuted amount exempt |
| 9 | Pension commutation (non-government) | One-third of full pension value (if gratuity received) or one-half (if no gratuity received) |
| 10 | Retrenchment compensation | Least of: actual received, amount calculated under Industrial Disputes Act, or Rs 50,000 |
| 11 | VRS / Voluntary termination | Lower of: actual received or Rs 5,00,000 |
| 12 | Leave encashment (government employees) | Entire amount exempt |
| 13 | Leave encashment (non-government) | Least of: actual received, 10 times average monthly salary, Rs 25,00,000, or cash equivalent of leave standing to credit |
| 14 | Aggregation rules | Rules for computing aggregate limits across categories |

#### Why This Matters

Under the 1961 Act, these deductions and exemptions were scattered across two separate sections (Sections 10 and 16), forcing practitioners to look in multiple places while computing salary income. The 2025 Act places every deduction available against salary income in one location. For return preparation, this means a single reference point instead of cross-referencing between the charging section, the deduction section, and the general exemption section.

---

## What Stayed the Same

- **Basis of charge** (Section 15): "Due" or "receipt" basis, whichever is earlier — identical.
- **Core salary definition**: 11 of 12 categories in Section 16 replicate old Section 17(1) without change.
- **Perquisite valuation framework**: The overall structure of nine categories tracks old Section 17(2). Valuation Rules (to be prescribed) will determine actual computation.
- **Profits in lieu of salary**: Scope unchanged; only cross-references updated.
- **Standard deduction quantum**: Rs 75,000 (new regime) and Rs 50,000 (old regime) carried forward from Finance Act 2024 amendments.
- **Retirement benefit limits**: Gratuity calculation formulae, pension commutation fractions (1/3 and 1/2), retrenchment compensation cap (Rs 50,000), VRS limit (Rs 5,00,000), and leave encashment cap (Rs 25,00,000) — all unchanged.
- **Entertainment allowance**: Available only to government employees, same three-way ceiling.

---

## Practical Impact

### For Practitioners

| Area | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Salary computation** | Single-section reference (Section 19 table) replaces multi-section cross-referencing. Return preparation becomes more linear. |
| **Perquisite valuation** | Watch for CBDT notifications — the shift from fixed statutory thresholds to "prescribed" amounts means Rules will set and revise limits without legislative amendment. |
| **Commuting benefit** | Employer-provided transport or reimbursement for home-to-office travel is now a non-taxable perquisite. Advise employer clients on structuring accordingly. |
| **Section 10 habit** | Stop looking at Section 10 for salary exemptions. Gratuity, pension, leave encashment, retrenchment, and VRS exemptions are now in Section 19, not Section 10. |
| **Schedule references** | Familiarise with Schedule II and Schedule III — they replace the old Section 10 sub-clause references throughout the salary provisions. |

### For Employers

| Area | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Form 16 → Form 130** | TDS certificates for salary must use the new form and section references from Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. |
| **Agniveer scheme** | Employers engaging Agniveers must include Agniveer Corpus Fund contributions in salary computation under Section 16. |
| **Perquisite reporting** | Monitor CBDT Rules for revised thresholds — amounts previously hardcoded in the Act may change via notification. |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **5 contiguous sections** (15-19) replace a scattered framework spread across Sections 10, 15, 16, and 17 of the old Act.
- **Section 19 is the anchor change** — a single table of 14 deduction categories merging old Section 16 deductions with five categories of Section 10 exemptions.
- **Delegated thresholds** in perquisite provisions (Section 17) allow CBDT to update monetary limits via Rules, removing the need for annual Finance Act amendments.
- **Relocated provisions**: Agniveer Corpus Fund contributions (Section 16, originally introduced by Finance Act 2022) and commuting cost exclusion (Section 17).
- **No substantive change** in basis of charge, salary definition (barring Agniveer), retirement benefit limits, or standard deduction amounts.
- **Cross-reference shift**: Schedule II and Schedule III replace old Section 10 sub-clause references.
