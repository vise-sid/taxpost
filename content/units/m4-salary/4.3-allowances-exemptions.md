# Unit 4.3: Allowances & Exemptions

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 fundamentally restructures how salary-related exemptions are handled. Under the 1961 Act, allowances and terminal benefits were exempt under various clauses of Section 10 and excluded from total income. The 2025 Act dismantles this framework entirely. Allowance-related exemptions migrate to Schedule III (exempt income for eligible persons), while terminal benefits — gratuity, leave encashment, commuted pension, retrenchment compensation, and VRS — are reclassified as **deductions from salary** under Section 19. The legal mechanism changes; the net tax effect does not.

---

## The Big Shift: Section 10 Is Gone

Under the 1961 Act, Section 10 housed all exemptions in a single, sprawling section with 50+ clauses. The 2025 Act breaks this apart:

| Old Location | New Location | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Section 10 (general exemptions) | **Schedule II** | Exempt income — general |
| Section 10 (eligible person exemptions) | **Schedule III** | Exempt income — eligible persons |
| Section 10 (salary-related terminal benefits) | **Section 19** | Deductions from salary income |

For salary practitioners, the critical distinction is this: allowances that remain available (HRA, LTC, special allowances) moved to Schedule III, while terminal benefits (gratuity, leave encashment, pension, retrenchment, VRS) moved to Section 19 as deductions within the salary head itself.

## Salary-Related Exemption Mapping: Old to New

| Old Section | Subject | New Location |

| 10(5) | Leave Travel Concession / Assistance | Schedule III, Table S.No. 8 |
| 10(10) | Gratuity | Section 19 (deduction from salary) |
| 10(10A) | Commuted pension | Section 19 (deduction from salary) |
| 10(10AA) | Leave encashment on retirement | Section 19 (deduction from salary) |
| 10(10B) | Retrenchment compensation | Section 19 (deduction from salary) |
| 10(10C) | VRS compensation | Section 19 (deduction from salary) |

---

## Allowances Retained in Schedule III

### HRA — Schedule III, Table S.No. 11 (Old Section 10(13A))

The House Rent Allowance exemption moves to Schedule III but the computation formula is unchanged. The exempt amount remains the **lowest of**:

1. Actual HRA received from the employer
2. Rent paid minus 10% of salary
3. 50% of salary (metro cities) or 40% of salary (non-metro cities)

"Salary" for this purpose continues to mean basic pay plus dearness allowance (to the extent it forms part of retirement benefits).

**New regime impact:** HRA exemption is **not available** under the new tax regime (Section 202). Employees opting for the new regime receive no benefit from HRA, regardless of rent paid.

### LTC/LTA — Schedule III, Table S.No. 8 (Old Section 10(5))

Leave Travel Concession or Assistance retains its existing structure:

- Available for **two journeys in a block of four calendar years**
- Covers travel expenses for the employee and family within India
- Economy class air fare for destinations served by air; AC first class rail fare otherwise

**New regime impact:** LTC/LTA exemption is **not available** under Section 202.

### Special Allowances for Duty — Schedule III, Table S.No. 12 (Old Section 10(14)(i))

Allowances granted to meet expenses wholly, necessarily, and exclusively incurred in the performance of duties continue to be exempt to the extent actually spent. Examples include travel allowance, conveyance allowance, and daily allowance for tour/transfer.

### Other Prescribed Allowances — Schedule III, Table S.No. 13 (Old Section 10(14)(ii))

Allowances prescribed by rule — such as children education allowance (Rs 100 per month per child, max 2 children), hostel expenditure allowance (Rs 300 per month per child), and transport allowance for disabled employees — continue under Schedule III with the same limits.

## Terminal Benefits: Exemptions Become Deductions Under Section 19

This is the most significant structural change in this unit. Five categories of terminal/retirement benefits that were **exemptions under Section 10** (excluded from total income entirely) are now **deductions under Section 19** (deducted while computing income under the head "Salaries").

### Why This Matters

- **Old Act:** Gratuity exempt under Section 10(10) → never entered total income computation
- **New Act:** Gratuity is a deduction under Section 19 → included in gross salary, then deducted

The **net tax effect is identical** — the same amount is excluded from taxable salary. But the legal mechanism is different. The amount now appears in the salary computation and is then deducted, rather than being excluded at the threshold. This affects how salary income is presented in returns and how employers report in TDS certificates.

### Gratuity — Section 19 (Old Section 10(10))

| Category | Exempt / Deductible Limit |
|---|---|
| Government employees | Entire gratuity — fully deductible |
| Employees covered by Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 | Lowest of: (a) 15 days wages for each completed year of service (7 days for piece-rate workers), (b) Rs 25,00,000, or (c) actual gratuity received |
| Other employees | Lowest of: (a) half month's salary for each completed year of service, (b) Rs 25,00,000, or (c) actual gratuity received |

The limit of Rs 25,00,000 (notified w.e.f. 29.03.2018) continues unchanged.

### Leave Encashment — Section 19 (Old Section 10(10AA))

| Category | Deductible Limit |
|---|---|
| Government employees | Entire amount — fully deductible |
| Other employees (on retirement/superannuation) | Lowest of: (a) 10 months' average salary, (b) cash equivalent of leave standing to credit (max 30 days per year of service), (c) Rs 25,00,000, or (d) actual leave encashment received |

The limit was enhanced from Rs 3,00,000 to **Rs 25,00,000** w.e.f. 01.04.2023 and carries forward into the 2025 Act.

### Commuted Pension — Section 19 (Old Section 10(10A))

| Category | Deductible Limit |
|---|---|
| Government employees | Entire commuted pension — fully deductible |
| Non-government, receiving gratuity | One-third of full pension commuted — deductible |
| Non-government, not receiving gratuity | One-half of full pension commuted — deductible |

### Retrenchment Compensation — Section 19 (Old Section 10(10B))

Deductible amount: lower of (a) amount calculated under Section 25F of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, or (b) **Rs 5,00,000**, or (c) actual compensation received.

### VRS Compensation — Section 19 (Old Section 10(10C))

Deductible up to **Rs 5,00,000** for compensation received under a voluntary retirement scheme that meets prescribed conditions. Available only once in a lifetime.

## Exemption vs. Deduction: The Conceptual Distinction

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 10 Exemption) | New Act (Section 19 Deduction) |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage of computation** | Excluded before computing gross total income | Deducted while computing salary income |
| **Appears in gross salary** | No | Yes |
| **Net taxable salary** | Same | Same |
| **Reporting in TDS certificate** | Shown as exempt income | Shown as deduction from salary |
| **Legal basis** | "The following incomes shall not be included in total income" | "In computing income under the head 'Salaries', the following deductions shall be allowed" |

Practitioners must update salary computation templates to reflect this change. The amount deducted remains the same, but the presentation in Form 130 (replacing Form 16) follows the deduction format.

---

## What Is New in the 2025 Act

### Agniveer Corpus Fund — Section 16, Item 12

Contributions made by the Central Government to the Agniveer Corpus Fund on behalf of enrolled Agniveers are excluded from salary computation. This provision was originally introduced by Finance Act 2022. The 2025 Act relocates it from old Section 80CCH into Section 16 of the salary definition.

### Commuting Cost Exclusion — Section 17(2)(e)

The value of any benefit provided by an employer for commuting between the place of residence and the place of work is excluded from the definition of perquisites. This codifies a position that was previously ambiguous and dealt with through administrative circulars.

---

## New Regime Restrictions (Section 202)

Employees opting for the new tax regime under Section 202 forfeit the following salary-related benefits:

| Benefit | Available Under Old Regime | Available Under New Regime |
|---|---|---|
| HRA exemption | Yes (Schedule III, S.No. 11) | **No** |
| LTC/LTA exemption | Yes (Schedule III, S.No. 8) | **No** |
| Entertainment allowance deduction | Yes | **No** |
| Professional tax deduction | Yes | **No** |
| Standard deduction | Rs 50,000 | **Rs 75,000** |

The new regime compensates by offering a higher standard deduction (Rs 75,000 vs. Rs 50,000) and lower slab rates, but employees with significant HRA claims or LTC usage must evaluate whether the trade-off is beneficial.

Terminal benefits under Section 19 (gratuity, leave encashment, commuted pension, retrenchment, VRS) remain deductible under **both regimes** as they are within the salary computation head, not separate exemptions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 10 salary exemptions are disbanded** — allowances move to Schedule III; terminal benefits move to Section 19 as deductions
- **HRA computation is unchanged** — lowest of actual HRA, rent minus 10% salary, or 50%/40% salary — but now sits in Schedule III, S.No. 11
- **LTC/LTA unchanged** — two journeys per four-year block, now in Schedule III, S.No. 8
- **Gratuity, leave encashment, commuted pension, retrenchment, VRS** — reclassified from exemptions to deductions under Section 19; same limits, different legal mechanism
- **Leave encashment limit: Rs 25,00,000** (enhanced from Rs 3,00,000 w.e.f. 01.04.2023)
- **Net tax effect is identical** — the reclassification from exemption to deduction does not change the taxable amount
- **HRA and LTC are not available under Section 202 (new regime)**; terminal benefit deductions under Section 19 remain available under both regimes
- **New additions**: Agniveer Corpus Fund (Section 16, item 12) and commuting cost exclusion from perquisites (Section 17(2)(e))
- **Form impact**: Salary computation in Form 130 (replacing Form 16) must reflect the deduction format for terminal benefits, not the exemption format
