# Unit 4.2: Perquisite Valuation Under New Rules

## Overview

The Income Tax Act, 2025 retains the long-standing approach of delegating perquisite valuation to subordinate Rules rather than embedding monetary limits in the Act itself. Section 17(2) of the 2025 Act uses the phrase **"computed in such manner as may be prescribed"** — identical in effect to the old framework, but now mapped to **Rule 15 of the Draft Income Tax Rules, 2026** (replacing the erstwhile Rule 3 of the Income Tax Rules, 1962). The delegation model is deliberate: it enables CBDT to revise monetary thresholds without requiring Parliamentary amendment.

The substantive change in this unit is not structural — it is **monetary**. Nearly every fixed-rate perquisite valuation has been revised upward, reflecting inflation adjustment after decades of stagnation. One entirely new exclusion — commuting cost — has been introduced.

---

## Where Valuation Rules Live: Old → New Mapping

| | Old Framework | New Framework |
|---|---|---|
| **Governing Rule** | Rule 3, Income Tax Rules, 1962 | Rule 15, Draft Income Tax Rules, 2026 |
| **Enabling provision** | Section 17(2), IT Act, 1961 | Section 17(2), IT Act, 2025 |
| **Delegation language** | "as may be prescribed" | "computed in such manner as may be prescribed" |
| **Amendment authority** | CBDT (via rule notification) | CBDT (via rule notification) — unchanged |

The architecture is unchanged. Valuation has not been elevated to the Act or to a Schedule. The practical implication: future limit revisions require only a CBDT notification, not a Finance Bill.

## Revised Monetary Limits: Rule 15 (2026) vs Rule 3 (1962)

### Motor Car — Employer-Owned, Employer Pays Running Expenses

Where the employer owns or leases a motor car and also bears the running and maintenance expenses, the taxable perquisite value is computed at the following flat monthly rates:

| Item | Old Rate (Rule 3) | New Rate (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Car with engine capacity ≤ 1.6 litre (cc) | Rs 1,800/month | **Rs 5,000/month** | +178% |
| Car with engine capacity > 1.6 litre (cc) | Rs 2,400/month | **Rs 7,000/month** | +192% |
| Driver / chauffeur provided by employer | Rs 900/month | **Rs 3,000/month** | +233% |

### Motor Car — Employee Pays Running and Maintenance Costs

Where the employer owns or leases the car but the employee bears running and maintenance costs, the reduced flat rates apply:

| Item | Old Rate (Rule 3) | New Rate (Rule 15) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Car with engine capacity ≤ 1.6 litre (cc) | Rs 600/month | **Rs 2,000/month** | +233% |
| Car with engine capacity > 1.6 litre (cc) | Rs 900/month | **Rs 3,000/month** | +233% |

### Meals and Food

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax-free meal value per meal | Rs 50/meal | **Rs 200/meal** | +300% |

Meals valued at or below Rs 200 per meal — whether provided in office, through a canteen, or via non-transferable meal vouchers — are not treated as a perquisite. Any amount in excess of Rs 200 per meal is taxable.

### Education

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free education in employer-maintained school | Rs 1,000/month/child | **Rs 3,000/month/child** | +200% |

Where the employer maintains an educational institution and provides free or concessional education to the employee's children, no perquisite value arises if the cost does not exceed Rs 3,000 per month per child. The excess, if any, is the taxable perquisite.

### Gifts

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual gift exemption threshold | Rs 5,000 | **Rs 15,000** | +200% |

Gifts (in cash or kind) from the employer aggregating up to Rs 15,000 in a tax year are excluded from perquisite valuation. The entire amount becomes taxable once the aggregate exceeds Rs 15,000.

### Medical Loans

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employer medical loan exemption | Rs 20,000 | **Rs 2,00,000** | +900% |

This is the most significant percentage increase. Loans advanced by the employer for medical treatment of the employee or family members are exempt from interest-free loan perquisite treatment up to Rs 2,00,000 (previously Rs 20,000). The tenfold increase aligns the exemption with contemporary medical costs.

### Salary Threshold for Section 17(2)(iii) Perquisites

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salary cap for taxable perquisites under Section 17(2)(iii) | Rs 50,000 | **Rs 4,00,000** | +700% |

Certain perquisites listed under Section 17(2)(iii) — broadly, benefits like interest-free loans, use of movable assets, and transfer of assets at concessional rates — are taxable only when the employee's salary income exceeds the prescribed threshold. The threshold has been raised from Rs 50,000 to Rs 4,00,000, effectively removing a significant number of lower-salary employees from the perquisite tax net.

### Overseas Medical Treatment

| Item | Old Limit | New Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gross Total Income (GTI) threshold for exemption | Rs 2,00,000 | **Rs 8,00,000** | +300% |

Where the employer meets expenditure on medical treatment of the employee or family member abroad, the travel and stay costs are not treated as a perquisite if the employee's Gross Total Income (excluding this benefit) does not exceed Rs 8,00,000.

## New Provision: Commuting Cost Exclusion

**Section 17(2)(e) of the 2025 Act** introduces an exclusion that did not exist in the 1961 Act:

> Expenditure incurred or reimbursed by the employer for the employee's commuting between residence and place of work is **excluded from the definition of perquisite**.

| | Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|---|
| **Commuting reimbursement** | No specific exclusion — taxable as perquisite | **Explicitly excluded** under Section 17(2)(e) |

This is not a limit-based exemption — the exclusion applies regardless of the amount. Whether the employer provides a shuttle, reimburses cab charges, or pays for metro passes for the home-to-office commute, the expenditure is not a perquisite.

Practitioners should note the scope limitation: the exclusion covers only the regular commute (residence to place of work and back). Travel for official duties (client visits, inter-office travel) was already outside the perquisite framework as business expenditure. Personal travel remains unaffected.

---

## What Stayed the Same

Not every valuation rule has changed. The following provisions remain substantively identical:

| Provision | Status |
|---|---|
| **Aggregate cap on employer PF + NPS + superannuation contributions** | Unchanged at Rs 7.5 lakh per annum |
| **Interest-free loan valuation method** | SBI lending rate benchmark expected to continue; methodology unchanged |
| **Accommodation valuation** | City classification (population-based), owned vs leased distinction, salary-based percentage — all unchanged |
| **Tea, snacks, and non-alcoholic beverages during working hours** | Fully exempt — no change |
| **Meals provided at remote or offshore locations** | Fully exempt — no change |

## Practical Implications

**For payroll teams:** Every fixed-rate perquisite valuation in payroll software must be updated for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. The motor car rates, meal limits, gift thresholds, and education exemptions are hard-coded in most payroll systems and will require configuration changes before the first salary run under the new Rules.

**For tax advisors:** The raised Section 17(2)(iii) salary threshold (Rs 4,00,000) is the most consequential change in terms of population impact. Employees earning up to Rs 4,00,000 in salary — a bracket that was taxable on certain perquisites under the old Rs 50,000 threshold — are now entirely outside the perquisite net for those benefits. Review client employee data to identify individuals who move out of the taxable bracket.

**For employers providing commuting benefits:** The new Section 17(2)(e) exclusion creates a clean planning opportunity. Employer-funded commuting — shuttle services, cab reimbursements, metro passes — can now be provided without perquisite consequences. Employers who previously grossed-up these benefits should reconsider their compensation structures.

**For documentation:** The shift from Rule 3 to Rule 15 means all internal references, audit working papers, and advisory letters citing the old Rule number must be updated. The substantive methodology is unchanged, but the citation is different.
