# Unit 2.1: The Parallel Operation

## Overview

From April 1, 2026, both the Income Tax Act, 1961 and the Income Tax Act, 2025 operate simultaneously. The 1961 Act is repealed by Section 536(1), but its provisions continue to govern all tax years beginning before April 1, 2026. The 2025 Act governs Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. The e-filing portal will support both Acts concurrently. This parallel operation will persist for years as old assessments, appeals, and reassessments work through the system.

---

## Section 536: The Governing Framework

Section 536 of the Income Tax Act, 2025 is a 22-sub-clause repeal and savings provision. It is the single most important section for transition purposes.

### Section 536(1): The Repeal

> "The Income-tax Act, 1961 is hereby repealed."

### Section 536(2): The Savings — What Survives the Repeal

The repeal does not affect the following:

| Sub-clause | What Is Preserved |
|---|---|
| 536(2)(b) | Rights, privileges, obligations, liabilities acquired under old Act |
| 536(2)(c) | Pending proceedings — assessments, reassessments, appeals, penalties for pre-April 2026 years continue under old Act |
| 536(2)(d) | Penalty proceedings for pre-April 2026 years — initiated and imposed under old Act |
| 536(2)(e) | Proceedings pending before any authority, Tribunal, or Court — continue as if new Act not enacted |
| 536(2)(f) | Elections, declarations, options exercised under old Act — deemed exercised under corresponding new Act provision |
| 536(2)(h) | Condition violations — if deduction/exemption conditions from old Act are violated after April 2026, amount deemed income under new Act |
| 536(2)(j) | Old circulars, notifications, approvals — continue if not inconsistent with new Act |
| 536(2)(k) | Expired limitation — rights that expired under old Act are NOT revived by new Act |
| 536(2)(m) and (n) | Losses brought forward — continue to be carried forward and set off under new Act |
| 536(2)(o) | Amalgamation/demerger condition violations — governed by 2025 Act in year of violation |
| 536(2)(s) | Multi-year deductions (35ABA, 35ABB, etc.) — continue under new Act |
| 536(2)(v) | Search proceedings under old Section 132 — entire proceeding continues under old Act |

### Section 536(3): The Terminology Bridge

> Where the 2025 Act refers to any tax year commencing on or before 1st April, 2025, such reference shall be construed as a reference to the corresponding "previous year" under the repealed Act.

### Section 536(4): General Clauses Act Safety Net

Section 6 of the General Clauses Act, 1897 applies to the repeal. This covers any unforeseen situation not specifically addressed in Section 536(2).

## The Dual-Track System

From April 1, 2026, two independent compliance tracks operate in parallel:

### Track 1: ITA 1961 (Pre-Transition)

| Activity | Details |
|---|---|
| Returns | File AY 2026-27 return (FY 2025-26 income) using old ITR forms, selecting "AY 2026-27" on portal |
| Due dates | 31 July / 31 August / 31 October / 30 November 2026 as applicable |
| Assessments | All assessments for AY 2026-27 and earlier under old Act procedures |
| Appeals | Continue under old Act provisions — Section 246A, CIT(A), ITAT |
| Reassessments | Old Section 148/148A procedure for pre-April 2026 years |
| Penalties | Old Act penalty framework (Section 271C, 271(1)(c), etc.) |
| Forms | Old forms — ITR-1 through ITR-7, Form 16, Form 26AS, Form 3CA/3CB/3CD |

### Track 2: ITA 2025 (New Regime)

| Activity | Details |
|---|---|
| Advance tax | From June 2026 onwards for TY 2026-27, under new Act |
| TDS/TCS | Payments/credits from April 1, 2026 — under Section 392/393/394 |
| Returns | File TY 2026-27 return using new ITR forms, selecting "TY 2026-27" on portal — due July 2027 onwards |
| Assessments | New Act procedures (Section 268-286) for TY 2026-27 onwards |
| Forms | New forms — Form 130 (old 16), Form 168 (old 26AS), Form 26 (old 3CA/3CB/3CD) |

### The Critical Distinction: AY 2026-27 ≠ Tax Year 2026-27

Per KarSetu FAQ Q3.17, these are **two entirely separate compliance obligations**:

| | AY 2026-27 | Tax Year 2026-27 |
|---|---|---|
| **Income period** | FY 2025-26 (Apr 2025 - Mar 2026) | FY 2026-27 (Apr 2026 - Mar 2027) |
| **Governing Act** | Income Tax Act, 1961 | Income Tax Act, 2025 |
| **Return due** | July/August/October/November 2026 | July/August/October/November 2027 |
| **Portal selection** | Select "AY 2026-27" | Select "Tax Year 2026-27" |
| **ITR forms** | Old forms (ITR-1 through ITR-7) | New forms under IT Rules, 2026 |

Non-filing of either return attracts consequences under the respective Act.

## Governing Principles: Which Act, When?

### The Income-Year Rule

The Act applicable to any matter is determined by the **year in which the income was earned**, not by the date on which the proceeding, payment, or filing occurs.

| If income relates to... | Governed by... | Even if action occurs after April 1, 2026 |
|---|---|---|
| FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26) or earlier | ITA 1961 | Yes — assessment, appeal, or penalty order can be passed after April 2026 under old Act |
| FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) | ITA 1961 | Yes — return filed in July 2026 under old Act |
| FY 2026-27 (Tax Year 2026-27) onwards | ITA 2025 | New Act from inception |

### The Earlier-Event Rule (TDS/TCS)

For TDS, the governing Act depends on when the **earlier of credit or payment** occurs (per KarSetu Q6.1):

| Earlier event of credit/payment | Governed by |
|---|---|
| On or before 31 March 2026 | ITA 1961 (old TDS sections 192-194T) |
| On or after 1 April 2026 | ITA 2025 (Sections 392/393/394) |

### Continuity Principles

| Principle | Authority | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Pending proceedings continue under old Act | Section 536(2)(c) and (e) | Assessment orders passed after April 2026 for old years follow old Act procedures |
| Old approvals remain valid | Section 536(2)(j) | Charitable trust registrations, lower withholding certificates valid if not inconsistent |
| Elections/options survive | Section 536(2)(f) | Options exercised under old Act deemed exercised under corresponding new Act provision (if one exists) |
| Expired limitation not revived | Section 536(2)(k) | A right to file appeal that expired under old Act cannot be revived by new Act |
| Losses carry forward seamlessly | Section 536(2)(m) and (n) | Old Act losses retain their character and set-off continues under new Act |

## Portal Operations During Transition

Per KarSetu FAQ Q3.27, the e-filing portal will support both Acts simultaneously:
- Taxpayers select either **Assessment Year** (for old Act filings) or **Tax Year** (for new Act filings) on the portal
- The portal serves the correct forms based on the selection
- Both old format and new format returns can be filed concurrently

### Tax Payment Precautions (KarSetu Q2.23)

During FY 2026-27, taxpayers must ensure correct selection on the e-filing portal:
- **Self-assessment tax for FY 2025-26** → Select "AY 2026-27" on portal (governed by ITA 1961)
- **Advance tax for FY 2026-27** → Select "Tax Year 2026-27" on portal (governed by ITA 2025)

Incorrect selection may result in tax credit being applied to the wrong year.

## How Long Will Parallel Operation Last?

Per KarSetu Q1.22:
- The 1961 Act's provisions continue for **all tax years beginning before 1st April, 2026**
- Assessments, appeals, and other proceedings for pre-April 2026 years will continue under the old Act **until their final resolution**
- This means the parallel operation could persist for **several years** as old appeals work through CIT(A), ITAT, High Courts, and the Supreme Court

---

## Practical Impact

- **Both Acts on one portal**: The e-filing portal supports AY-based and TY-based filings simultaneously
- **Correct year selection is critical**: Selecting the wrong year (AY vs TY) for tax payments results in credit misallocation
- **Old forms for old years**: Even returns filed after April 2026 for FY 2025-26 use old ITR forms and old section numbers
- **Section 536 knowledge is mandatory**: This section governs every transitional question — loss carry-forwards, pending appeals, condition violations, old approvals, expired limitations
- **Old circulars remain valid**: Per Section 536(2)(j), CBDT circulars issued under old Act continue if not inconsistent with the new Act. A circular clarifying Section 194C applies to Section 393 where the intent is unchanged.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 536** is a 22-sub-clause repeal and savings provision governing the entire transition
- **Section 536(1)** repeals the 1961 Act; **Section 536(2)** preserves rights, proceedings, losses, approvals, and options
- **Section 536(3)** bridges "tax year" references to corresponding "previous year" for pre-2026 years
- **AY 2026-27 and Tax Year 2026-27 are separate obligations** — different income periods, different Acts, different forms
- **Income-year rule**: the Act governing any income is determined by the year in which the income was earned
- **Earlier-event rule for TDS**: credit/payment on or before 31 March 2026 → old Act; on or after 1 April 2026 → new Act
- **Expired limitation under old Act is NOT revived** by the new Act (Section 536(2)(k))
- **Parallel operation will persist for years** as pending proceedings under old Act work through the system
- **Portal supports both Acts simultaneously** — correct AY/TY selection on challans and returns is critical
