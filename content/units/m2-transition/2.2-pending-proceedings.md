# Unit 2.2: Pending Proceedings

## Overview

Section 536(2)(c) and (e) of the Income Tax Act, 2025 preserve all pending proceedings — assessments, reassessments, appeals, penalties, revisions, and references — under the old Act. Any proceeding pending on April 1, 2026, or initiated after that date for a pre-April 2026 tax year, continues as if the new Act had not been enacted. The new Act's reassessment and appellate framework (Sections 279-286 and 356-378) applies only to Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. The appellate hierarchy remains unchanged. Time limits for reassessment have been recalibrated from the AY-based 3/5-year structure to a TY-based 4/6-year structure. Expired limitation under the old Act is not revived by the new Act.

---

## Reassessment — Old Act (ITA 1961)

The reassessment framework under the 1961 Act comprises Sections 147-153:

| Section | Subject |
|---|---|
| 147 | Power to assess or reassess escaped income |
| 148 | Issue of notice for reassessment |
| 148A | Procedure before issuance of notice (show cause) |
| 149 | Time limit for notices |
| 150 | Assessment in pursuance of appellate/court orders |
| 151 | Sanction for issue of notice |
| 152 | Rate of tax applicable, dropping of proceedings |
| 153 | Time limit for completion of assessment/reassessment |

### Old Act Time Limits (Section 149)

| Threshold | Show-cause notice | Reassessment notice |
|---|---|---|
| Below the monetary threshold | 3 years from end of AY | 3 years 3 months from end of AY |
| At or above the monetary threshold | 5 years from end of AY | 5 years 3 months from end of AY |

| Subject | Old Section | New Section | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Power to assess/reassess escaped income | 147 | 279 | Moved |
| Issue of notice for reassessment | 148 | 280 | Moved |
| Procedure before issuance (show cause) | 148A | 281 | Moved |

| Assessment per appellate/court orders | 150 | 283 | Moved |

## Reassessment — What Changed

### Time Limits Recalibrated

The new Act replaces the AY-based 3/5-year time limits with a TY-based 4/6-year structure:

| Stage | Old Act (ITA 1961) | New Act (ITA 2025) |
|---|---|---|
| Show-cause notice (below ₹50 lakh) | 3 years from end of AY | 4 years from end of Tax Year (Section 281) |
| Show-cause notice (₹50 lakh or above) | 5 years from end of AY | 6 years from end of Tax Year (Section 281) |
| Reassessment notice (below ₹50 lakh) | 3 years 3 months from end of AY | 4 years 3 months from end of Tax Year (Section 280) |
| Reassessment notice (₹50 lakh or above) | 5 years 3 months from end of AY | 6 years 3 months from end of Tax Year (Section 280) |

### One-Year Moratorium

Section 282(3) introduces a new safeguard: no reassessment notice may be issued within one year from the end of any tax year. This prevents reassessment from being initiated before the original return due date has passed.

### Which Act Governs Reassessment

| Situation | Governing Act |
|---|---|
| Reassessment for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards | ITA 2025 (Sections 279-286) |
| Reassessment for any AY up to 2026-27 | ITA 1961 (old Sections 147-153) |
| Section 148A(1) notice issued before 01.04.2026, but Section 148 notice not yet issued | Section 148 notice can still be issued after 01.04.2026 under old Act — Section 536(2)(c) |
| Fresh reassessment initiated after 01.04.2026 for earlier AYs | Old Act governs, provided limitation has not expired |

---

## Reassessment — What Stayed the Same

- The conceptual framework is identical: information suggesting escaped income → show-cause notice → assessee's response → reassessment notice → reassessment order
- The requirement for prior sanction from a specified authority before issuing notice is retained (Section 151 → Section 284)
- The provision for assessment in pursuance of appellate or court orders is carried forward (Section 150 → Section 283)
- The power to drop proceedings where the escaped income is below the prescribed threshold continues (Section 152 → Section 285)

## Appeals — What Changed

### Revision: 60-Day Residual Period Rule

Section 377(7) of the new Act introduces a minimum residual period requirement. Where the Commissioner exercises revision power under Section 377, the assessee must have at least 60 days remaining in the limitation period after the revision order is passed to file any consequent appeal or application. This is a new safeguard absent in Section 263 of the old Act.

### Which Act Governs Appeals

| Situation | Governing Act |
|---|---|
| Appeal pending on 01.04.2026 | Continues under old Act — no fresh appeal needed (Section 536(2)(e)) |
| Fresh appeal filed after 01.04.2026 for AY 2026-27 or earlier | Old Act governs (Section 536(2)(c)) |
| Appeal for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards | ITA 2025 (Sections 356-378) |
| ITAT remands matter for old AY back to AO | Remand proceedings conducted under old Act |
| Limitation expired under old Act | NOT revived by new Act — Section 536(2)(k) |

---

## Appeals — What Stayed the Same

- The appellate hierarchy is unchanged: AO → JCIT(A)/CIT(A) → ITAT → High Court → Supreme Court
- The Dispute Resolution Committee framework is carried forward without substantive change (Section 245MA → Section 379)
- Advance Ruling provisions are retained, consolidated into fewer sections
- The mechanism for avoiding repetitive appeals is preserved (Sections 158A/158AB → Sections 375/376)
- Revision outer time limit remains two years from the end of the financial year in which the order under revision was passed

---

## Pending Proceedings — The Core Rule

Section 536(2)(c) and (e) together establish a single governing principle:

> All proceedings — assessment, reassessment, rectification, penalty, revision, reference, and appeal — that are either pending on 01.04.2026 or initiated on or after 01.04.2026 for a pre-April 2026 tax year, continue under the Income Tax Act, 1961 as if the new Act had not been enacted.

This applies regardless of when the proceeding is completed. An assessment order passed in 2028 for AY 2024-25 is governed by the old Act. A penalty imposed in 2030 for AY 2025-26 is governed by the old Act.

---

## Practical Impact

- **Dual-track knowledge is mandatory**: Practitioners must maintain working knowledge of both Acts' reassessment and appellate procedures for years to come
- **Longer reassessment windows under new Act**: The shift from 3/5 years (old) to 4/6 years (new) extends the reassessment exposure period by one year in each category
- **One-year moratorium is new**: Section 282(3) prevents hasty reassessment within the first year after a tax year ends — a safeguard not present in the old Act
- **60-day residual period for revision**: Section 377(7) protects assessees from being caught without time to appeal after a revision order — a new procedural right
- **No revival of expired rights**: If the limitation for filing an appeal or initiating reassessment has already expired under the old Act before April 1, 2026, the new Act does not grant a second opportunity
- **In-progress Section 148A matters continue**: Where a show-cause notice under Section 148A(1) was issued before April 1, 2026 but the Section 148 reassessment notice was not yet served, the Assessing Officer may still issue the Section 148 notice after April 1, 2026 under the old Act
- **Old AY reassessments can still be freshly initiated**: Even after April 1, 2026, the department can initiate fresh reassessment proceedings for earlier AYs under the old Act, provided the limitation period under the old Act has not expired

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 536(2)(c) and (e)** preserve all pending proceedings under the old Act — assessment, reassessment, appeals, penalties, revision, and references
- **Reassessment sections map 147-153 (old) to 279-286 (new)** — structure retained, time limits recalibrated
- **New time limits are longer**: 4/6 years from end of Tax Year (new) versus 3/5 years from end of AY (old)
- **Section 282(3)** introduces a one-year moratorium — no reassessment notice within one year from end of tax year
- **Appeals sections map 246-264 (old) to 356-378 (new)** — appellate hierarchy unchanged
- **Section 377(7)** is new: 60-day minimum residual period after revision order
- **Expired limitation is not revived** by the new Act (Section 536(2)(k))
- **ITAT remands for old AYs** continue under old Act procedures
- **DRC and Advance Ruling** frameworks are substantially unchanged
- **In-progress Section 148A matters** can proceed to Section 148 notice after April 1, 2026 under old Act
