# Unit 2.3: Carry-Forward & Continuity

## Overview

Losses, deductions, and credits earned under the 1961 Act do not vanish on April 1, 2026. Section 536(2)(m) and (n) preserve their carry-forward into the new Act — but the carry-forward period counts from the original assessment year, not from the switchover. This unit maps every set-off and carry-forward provision to its new section number, explains the transition rules that govern pre-2026 losses, and flags the traps around belated returns, condition violations, and deduction continuity.

---

## Set-Off & Carry-Forward: Old → New Mapping

The core mechanics of set-off and carry-forward are unchanged. The sections moved; the rules did not.

### Section Mapping

| Subject | Old Section | New Section | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intra-head set-off | 70 | 108 | Renumbered only |
| Inter-head set-off | 71 | 109 | Renumbered only |
| House property loss cap | 71(3A) — ₹2 lakh | 109(1)(b) — ₹2 lakh | Renumbered only |
| Capital loss restriction (against capital gains only) | 71(3) | 109(2) | Renumbered only |
| Business loss carry-forward | 72 | 112 | 8 years retained |
| Capital loss carry-forward | 74 | 111 | 8 years retained |
| Speculation loss carry-forward | 73 | 113 | 4 years retained |
| Specified business loss | 73A | 114 | Renumbered only |
| Race horse loss carry-forward | 74A | 115 | 4 years retained |

### What Stayed the Same

Everything substantive. The ₹2 lakh cap on house property loss set-off against other heads survives as-is. The 8-year window for business and capital losses is unchanged. The 4-year window for speculation and race horse losses is unchanged. The restriction on setting off capital loss against non-capital income persists. Specified business loss continues with unlimited carry-forward.

**Bottom line: Same rules, new section numbers. Update your section references in memos and opinions — nothing else changes in your set-off computations.**

## Transition Rules for Pre-2026 Losses

This is where it gets important. Section 536(2)(m) and (n) govern how losses from pre-April 2026 years flow into the new Act.

### The Four Rules

**1. Losses carry forward seamlessly**
Losses from pre-April 2026 years that were eligible for carry-forward under the old Act continue to be carried forward and set off under the new Act. No re-filing. No fresh claims. The brought-forward loss schedule migrates automatically.

**2. Original character is preserved**
A business loss remains a business loss. A capital loss remains a capital loss. There is no reclassification of the loss character during transition. The loss retains whatever head it belonged to under the 1961 Act and is set off under the corresponding head in the 2025 Act.

**3. The carry-forward clock does NOT reset**
The 8-year or 4-year carry-forward period counts from the **original assessment year** in which the loss was first incurred — not from April 1, 2026.

| Loss Year | Loss Type | Carry-Forward Window | Last Year Available |
|---|---|---|---|
| AY 2023-24 | Business loss (Section 72 → 112) | 8 years from AY 2023-24 | AY 2030-31 (= TY 2030-31 equivalent) |
| AY 2024-25 | Capital loss (Section 74 → 111) | 8 years from AY 2024-25 | AY 2031-32 equivalent |
| AY 2025-26 | Speculation loss (Section 73 → 113) | 4 years from AY 2025-26 | AY 2028-29 equivalent |
| AY 2022-23 | Business loss | 8 years from AY 2022-23 | AY 2029-30 equivalent |

A business loss from AY 2023-24 can be set off in TY 2026-27, TY 2027-28, and so on — up to the year equivalent to AY 2030-31. The transition does not buy extra time.

**4. Belated return under old Act = No carry-forward under new Act**
If the loss return was filed belatedly under the old Act (i.e., after the due date under old Section 139(1)), the loss was never eligible for carry-forward under old Section 80. The new Act cannot cure that defect. ITA 2025 does not override old Act procedural requirements retroactively.

### Watch Out

> **Trap:** Do not assume the transition "resets" the carry-forward window. A client with a business loss from AY 2020-21 has already consumed 5 of the 8 years. Under the new Act, only 3 more years remain — TY 2026-27, TY 2027-28, and AY 2027-28 equivalent. The new Act gives no additional runway.

> **Trap:** A loss return for AY 2025-26 filed after the due date under old Section 139(1) — even if filed before April 1, 2026 — will NOT qualify for carry-forward into TY 2026-27 and beyond. The belated return kills carry-forward permanently. There is no second chance under the new Act.

## Unabsorbed Depreciation

Unabsorbed depreciation from pre-April 2026 years continues to be carried forward under the new Act with **unlimited carry-forward** — same as under old Section 32(2). This is distinct from business losses (which have an 8-year cap). If a client has both unabsorbed depreciation and brought-forward business loss, the depreciation survives indefinitely even after the business loss expires.

**Bottom line: Unabsorbed depreciation has no expiry date, before or after transition.**

## Deductions Continuity: 80C → 123 and Beyond

### Standard Deductions (Chapter VI-A → Chapter VIII)

| Old Section | New Section | Limit | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80C (life insurance, PPF, ELSS, etc.) | 123 (Schedule XV) | ₹1.5 lakh | Renumbered only |
| 80C instruments list | Schedule XV to ITA 2025 | — | Same instruments, new location |

The ₹1.5 lakh limit is unchanged. The qualifying instruments are identical — they have moved from the body of the section to **Schedule XV** of the 2025 Act, but the list itself is the same.

**Not available under new tax regime**: Section 80C deduction (now Section 123) is not available under the new tax regime (Section 202 of ITA 2025). This mirrors the old Act position — no change in substance.

**Timely filing required**: Chapter VIII Part C deductions (which include Section 123) require the return to be filed on or before the due date per **Section 122(5)** of ITA 2025. This mirrors old Section 80AC.

### Profit-Linked Deductions (80-IA, 80-IB, etc.)

These are the ones to watch. The old profit-linked deductions have been moved to transitional sections:

| Old Section | New Transitional Section | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 80-IA | 138 | Continue for remaining eligible period only |
| 80-IAB | 139 | Continue for remaining eligible period only |
| 80-IAC | 141 | Continue for remaining eligible period only |
| 80-IB | 142 | Continue for remaining eligible period only |

**Critical distinction**: These deductions continue only for the **remaining** eligible period. If a unit was eligible for a 10-year deduction under old Section 80-IA and has already claimed for 7 years, only 3 years of deduction remain under the transitional provision.

**Deduction quantum changes**: The deduction amount is computed on the basis of **business income as computed under ITA 2025**, not as it would have been computed under the old Act. If the 2025 Act computes a different business income (due to different depreciation rates, for instance), the deduction quantum will differ.

### Options and Elections

Per **Section 536(2)(f)**, options or elections exercised under the old Act are deemed to have been exercised under the corresponding provision of the new Act — **but only if a corresponding provision exists**. If the new Act has no equivalent provision, the old election has no operative effect.

### Watch Out

> **Trap:** Do not compute profit-linked deductions (80-IA etc.) using old Act computation methods. From TY 2026-27, the business income is computed under ITA 2025 rules. The deduction percentage applies to the new Act income figure, which may differ from what the old Act would have produced.

## MAT/AMT Credit Carry-Forward

| Old Section | Subject | New Act Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 115JAA | MAT credit carry-forward | Unutilised credit treated as eligible credit under new Act |
| 115JD | AMT credit carry-forward | Unutilised credit treated as eligible credit under new Act |

- Carry-forward period: **15 years** from the year the credit first became available (unchanged)
- Can be set off from **TY 2026-27 onwards** under ITA 2025 conditions
- The credit amount does not get recalculated — the pre-2026 credit quantum carries forward as-is

**Bottom line: MAT/AMT credits survive the transition intact. The 15-year window counts from the original year, same as losses.**

## Condition Violations After April 1, 2026

Section 536(2)(h) governs a scenario that will catch many practitioners off guard.

### The Rule

If a deduction or exemption was claimed under the old Act, and the **conditions** attached to that claim are violated **after** April 1, 2026:
- The amount of the earlier deduction/exemption is **deemed income** in the year of violation
- It is taxed under the **income head as per the old Act** (i.e., the head under which the original deduction/exemption was claimed)
- The tax is levied under ITA 2025 (because the violation year falls under the new Act)

### Practical Example

A client claimed **Section 54 exemption** on capital gains by purchasing a new residential house. The 3-year lock-in condition requires the house not to be sold within 3 years. If the client sells the new house in January 2028 — i.e., after April 1, 2026 — the earlier exempted capital gain is **taxed in TY 2027-28** (the year of violation) under the head "Capital Gains" as per the old Act classification, but taxed under the ITA 2025 rate structure.

### Watch Out

> **Trap:** This applies to all condition-based exemptions and deductions claimed under the old Act — Section 54, 54EC, 54F, 10AA, 80-IA conditions, and any other provision where a post-claim condition existed. Advise clients with active lock-in periods that the condition does not lapse just because the old Act was repealed.

> **Trap:** The income is taxable "under the head as per the old Act" — meaning the characterisation follows the old Act even though the tax is computed under ITA 2025. The old Act's classification of the income head survives for this limited purpose.

## Key Takeaways

- **Set-off and carry-forward rules are substantively unchanged** — only section numbers have moved (Sections 70-74A → Sections 108-115)
- **Pre-2026 losses carry forward into the new Act** under Section 536(2)(m) and (n), retaining their original character
- **Carry-forward period counts from the original AY**, not from the transition date — the clock does not reset
- **Belated loss returns under the old Act kill carry-forward permanently** — the new Act cannot cure old Act procedural defects
- **Unabsorbed depreciation** carries forward with no time limit, same as before
- **Section 80C → Section 123**: same ₹1.5 lakh limit, same instruments, still unavailable under new tax regime
- **Profit-linked deductions (80-IA etc.)** continue under transitional sections (138, 139, 141, 142) for the remaining eligible period only, computed on ITA 2025 business income
- **MAT/AMT credits** survive the transition and can be set off from TY 2026-27 onwards, with the 15-year carry-forward window counting from the original year
- **Condition violations after April 1, 2026** trigger deemed income under Section 536(2)(h) — the exempted amount is taxed in the year of violation under ITA 2025
- **Options/elections under old Act** are deemed exercised under new Act per Section 536(2)(f), but only if a corresponding provision exists
