# Unit 2.4: TDS During Transition

## Overview

TDS is where the transition hits your daily work hardest. Starting April 1, 2026, every deduction, every challan, every return, and every certificate follows a different Act depending on a single question: when did the earlier of credit or payment occur? Get the date wrong, cite the wrong section, or file the wrong form — and you're looking at processing errors and correction statements. This unit covers the full TDS switchover mechanics.

---

## The Fundamental Rule: Earlier of Credit or Payment

This is the one rule that governs every TDS decision during transition.

| Earlier event of credit or payment | Governing Act | TDS sections |
|---|---|---|
| On or before 31 March 2026 | ITA 1961 | Old sections 192–194T |
| On or after 1 April 2026 | ITA 2025 | Sections 392 / 393 / 394 |

The test is not when the contract was signed, not when the invoice was raised, and not when the service was rendered. It is solely the **earlier of credit in the books or actual payment**. A contractor who worked through March 2026 but receives payment on 5 April 2026 triggers the new Act. No ambiguity.

**Bottom line: One date determines which Act applies. Everything else — section number, form, return, certificate — follows from that date.**

---

## Section Quoting: The Change That Will Cause the Most Errors

For any payment or credit on or after 1 April 2026, you must quote the **new Act section reference**. Quoting old section numbers (194C, 194J, 194H) for post-01.04.2026 transactions may cause system-level validation errors on the e-filing portal.

### How to Quote Correctly

The new TDS structure uses a table-based system under Section 393. The citation format is:

> **Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. X(y)]**

### Example

| Scenario | Wrong Citation | Correct Citation |
|---|---|---|
| Payment to contractor on 5 April 2026 | Section 194C | Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. 6(i)] |

### What Happens If You Quote the Old Section?

Citing an old section (e.g., 194C) for a post-01.04.2026 payment may lead to:
- Processing errors on the portal
- Challan mismatch
- Need to file a **correction statement**

This is not a theoretical risk. The portal validates section references against the payment date. Old sections for new-date transactions will fail validation.

> **Watch out:** Your tax software may still default to old section numbers if not updated. Verify that your software maps to Section 393 table references before processing the first April 2026 deductions. A bulk correction statement for an entire month is not how you want to start the new Act.

**Bottom line: Muscle memory will make you type 194C. Train yourself — and your team — to cite 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. X] from Day 1.**

## Cross-Period Contracts

Ongoing contracts that straddle March–April 2026 do **not** need to be amended or re-executed.

### How It Works

For a monthly retainer contract running through the transition:

| Payment / Credit Date | Governing Act | Section to Quote |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly payment on 31 March 2026 | ITA 1961 | Old section (e.g., 194C) |
| Monthly payment on 30 April 2026 | ITA 2025 | Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. X] |
| Monthly payment on 31 May 2026 | ITA 2025 | Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. X] |

The deductor simply applies the applicable TDS provision based on the credit/payment date. The contract itself is irrelevant to the Act determination.

**Bottom line: Same contract, same vendor, same amount — but different sections in March vs April. No contract amendment needed.**

---

## TDS Rates: Nothing Changed

The consolidation of 30+ TDS sections into Section 393 is **presentational, not substantive**. Rates and monetary thresholds remain identical.

- No change in TDS rates
- No change in TCS rates
- No change in monetary thresholds for deduction
- The Section 393 table reproduces the same rates that existed across old Sections 192–194T

This is purely a reorganization exercise. If your client asks whether TDS rates changed under the new Act, the answer is no.

**Bottom line: Same rates, same thresholds. The only change is where you find them — one consolidated table instead of 30+ scattered sections.**

## Deposit Timelines

| Period of Deduction | Due Date for Deposit | Governed By |
|---|---|---|
| January – February 2026 | 7th of the following month | ITA 1961 (Rule 30) |
| March 2026 (non-government deductors) | 30 April 2026 | ITA 1961 (Rule 30) |
| March 2026 (government with challan) | 7 April 2026 | ITA 1961 (Rule 30) |
| April 2026 onwards | 7th of the following month | ITA 2025 (Rule 218, IT Rules 2026) |

The deposit timeline structure is unchanged — 7th of the next month remains the standard. The governing rule shifts from Rule 30 (old IT Rules) to Rule 218 (IT Rules, 2026) for April 2026 deductions onwards.

> **Watch out:** March 2026 deductions for non-government deductors have the extended 30 April 2026 deadline. But April 2026 deductions revert to the standard 7 May 2026 deadline under the new rules. Don't confuse the two.

## TDS Returns During Transition

This is where the form numbers change completely. The old quarterly return forms (24Q, 26Q, 27Q, 27EQ) are replaced by new forms under ITA 2025.

| Quarter | Period | Governed By | Form | Due Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q4 FY 2025-26 | Jan – Mar 2026 | ITA 1961 | 24Q / 26Q / 27Q / 27EQ | 31 May 2026 |
| Q1 TY 2026-27 | Apr – Jun 2026 | ITA 2025 | Salary: **Form 138** · Non-salary: **Form 140** · Non-resident: **Form 144** · TCS: **Form 143** | 31 July 2026 |
| Q2 TY 2026-27 | Jul – Sep 2026 | ITA 2025 | New forms (138 / 140 / 144 / 143) | 31 October 2026 |

### The Form Mapping

| Old Form | Coverage | New Form |
|---|---|---|
| 24Q | Salary TDS | Form 138 |
| 26Q | Non-salary TDS | Form 140 |
| 27Q | Non-resident TDS | Form 144 |
| 27EQ | TCS | Form 143 |

> **Watch out:** Q4 FY 2025-26 (Jan–Mar 2026) is the last quarter filed under old forms. You file 24Q/26Q/27Q/27EQ by 31 May 2026. The very next quarter — Q1 TY 2026-27 — uses entirely different form numbers. Ensure your TDS software supports the new forms before the 31 July 2026 deadline.

**Bottom line: Old forms for old quarters, new forms for new quarters. No mixing. The cut-off is clean — March 2026 and before on old forms, April 2026 onwards on new forms.**

## TDS Certificates

| Description | For Period | Governed By | Form | Due Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TDS on Salary | FY 2025-26 | ITA 1961 | Form 16 | 15 June 2026 |
| TDS Non-salary | Jan – Mar 2026 (Q4) | ITA 1961 | Form 16A | 15 June 2026 |
| TDS on Salary | TY 2026-27 | ITA 2025 | **Form 130** | 15 June 2027 |
| TDS Non-salary | Apr – Jun 2026 (Q1) | ITA 2025 | **Form 131** | 15 August 2026 |

### The Certificate Mapping

| Old Form | New Form |
|---|---|
| Form 16 (salary) | Form 130 |
| Form 16A (non-salary) | Form 131 |

Note the overlap in June 2026: you issue Form 16 (old Act, full-year salary for FY 2025-26) and Form 16A (old Act, Q4 non-salary) — both due 15 June 2026. The new Act certificates start with Form 131 for Q1 non-salary (due 15 August 2026) and Form 130 for TY 2026-27 salary (due 15 June 2027).

**Bottom line: June 2026 is a dual-certificate month. Old Act Form 16 and Form 16A are due alongside normal work under the new Act. Plan capacity accordingly.**

## Challan-cum-TDS Statements

The old Act required four separate forms for different transaction types. The new Act consolidates them into one.

| Old Act Forms | Transaction Type | New Act Form |
|---|---|---|
| Form 26QB | Sale of immovable property | **Form 141** |
| Form 26QC | Rent by individuals/HUFs | **Form 141** |
| Form 26QD | Payment to resident contractor/professional by individuals/HUFs | **Form 141** |
| Form 26QE | Virtual digital asset transfer | **Form 141** |

- Transaction where earlier event occurs on or before 31 March 2026 → use old form (26QB / 26QC / 26QD / 26QE)
- Transaction where earlier event occurs on or after 1 April 2026 → use **Form 141** (single form for all four types)

**Bottom line: Four forms become one. Form 141 handles all challan-cum-statement transactions from April 2026. Simpler, but remember to use the old forms for pre-April transactions even if filing after April 1.**

## Salary TDS: The Reset

Salary TDS requires a full computation reset on 1 April 2026. The employer cannot simply continue the FY 2025-26 computation into the new tax year.

### The Switchover

| Salary | Payment Date | Governing Act | Section |
|---|---|---|---|
| March 2026 salary | Paid on 31 March 2026 | ITA 1961 | Section 192 |
| April 2026 salary | Paid on 30 April 2026 | ITA 2025 | Section 392(1) |

### What the Employer Must Do on 1 April 2026

Starting TY 2026-27, the employer must:
- **Reset TDS computation** — fresh projected income for the new tax year
- **Apply deductions under ITA 2025 sections** — not old Act sections (e.g., deductions under the corresponding new Act provisions, not 80C/80D)
- **Determine the tax regime for TY 2026-27** — obtain employee's regime choice under the new Act
- **Issue Form 16 for FY 2025-26** by 15 June 2026 under the old Act, separately from the new Act computation

> **Watch out:** If salary for March 2026 is paid in April 2026 (common for some employers), the earlier event matters. If March salary was credited in the books on 31 March but paid on 5 April, the credit date (31 March) governs — ITA 1961 applies. But if neither credit nor payment occurred until April, the new Act applies to that March salary. Verify your payroll system's credit date logic.

**Bottom line: April 1 is a hard reset. New projected income, new section references, new regime determination. The payroll team needs advance preparation — this is not a switchover that can happen on the fly.**

## Lower Withholding Certificates

### Old Certificates: Do They Survive?

A certificate issued under **old Section 197** remains valid for payments made on or after 1 April 2026, **provided** it was issued for lower/nil deduction for projected income receivable during TY 2026-27.

This means: if the certificate covers the period and the income projection extends into TY 2026-27, it does not expire on 31 March 2026 merely because the Act changed.

### New Certificates

| Old Act | New Act |
|---|---|
| Section 197 certificate | Section 395(1) certificate via **Form 128** |

New applications for lower withholding from April 2026 onwards are filed under Section 395(1) using Form 128.

**Bottom line: Existing Section 197 certificates valid for TY 2026-27 income continue to work. New applications go through Section 395(1) / Form 128.**

## TDS Credit: Two Separate Streams

Tax deducted before and after the cut-off flows into two entirely separate credit systems.

| Tax Deducted In | Credit Appears In | Governed By |
|---|---|---|
| March 2026 (or earlier) | AY 2026-27 credit | ITA 1961 |
| April 2026 (or later) | TY 2026-27 credit | ITA 2025 |

### Two Separate AIS Statements

The taxpayer will receive **two separate Annual Information Statements**:
1. **AIS for AY 2026-27** — under ITA 1961, covering TDS/TCS and financial transactions for FY 2025-26
2. **Form 168 for TY 2026-27** — under ITA 2025, covering TDS/TCS and financial transactions for FY 2026-27

These are independent documents under independent Acts. TDS credit from one cannot be claimed in the other.

> **Watch out:** A deduction made in March 2026 credited to AY 2026-27 cannot be claimed as credit in TY 2026-27, and vice versa. If an employer incorrectly deposits March 2026 TDS under the new Act (selecting TY 2026-27 on the portal), the credit lands in the wrong statement. The employee will need a correction — and cannot claim the credit until it is fixed.

**Bottom line: Two Acts, two credit streams, two AIS statements. Ensure every challan selects the correct year — AY for pre-April deductions, TY for post-April deductions.**

---

## Key Takeaways

- **One rule governs everything**: the earlier of credit or payment determines ITA 1961 vs ITA 2025 — no exceptions
- **Section quoting changes completely**: post-01.04.2026 transactions must cite Section 393(1) [Table: Sl. No. X], not old sections like 194C or 194J — wrong citations cause portal errors
- **Rates and thresholds are unchanged**: the consolidation into Section 393 is presentational, not substantive
- **Cross-period contracts need no amendment**: the deductor applies the correct Act based on credit/payment date, regardless of contract terms
- **TDS return forms change entirely**: 24Q/26Q/27Q/27EQ for Q4 FY 2025-26 (last old-form quarter); Form 138/140/144/143 from Q1 TY 2026-27
- **TDS certificate forms change**: Form 16 → Form 130 (salary), Form 16A → Form 131 (non-salary)
- **Four challan-cum-statement forms become one**: 26QB/26QC/26QD/26QE → Form 141
- **Salary TDS requires a full reset on 1 April 2026**: new projected income, new deduction sections, new regime choice
- **Old Section 197 certificates survive** if issued for TY 2026-27 income; new certificates via Section 395(1) / Form 128
- **Two separate AIS statements** will exist: one for AY 2026-27 (old Act), one for TY 2026-27 (Form 168, new Act) — TDS credit cannot cross between them
