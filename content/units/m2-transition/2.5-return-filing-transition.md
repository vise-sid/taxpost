# Unit 2.5: Return Filing During Transition

## Overview

Return filing sections map directly from the 1961 Act to the 2025 Act: Section 139 becomes Section 263, with sub-section numbering largely preserved. Due dates, late filing fees, and the belated/revised/updated return framework remain substantively unchanged. The critical transition rule is that AY 2026-27 returns (for FY 2025-26 income) are filed entirely under the old Act using old forms, even though the filing occurs after April 1, 2026. Tax Year 2026-27 returns (for FY 2026-27 income) are the first returns filed under the new Act. The e-filing portal supports both Acts simultaneously, serving the correct forms based on the taxpayer's AY or TY selection. Forms have been extensively renumbered and, in several cases, merged — most notably Form 15G and 15H into a single Form 121, and Forms 3CA/3CB/3CD into a single Form 26.

## Return Filing — Section Mapping

| Subject | Old Section (ITA 1961) | New Section (ITA 2025) |
|---|---|---|
| Filing of return | 139(1) | 263(1) |
| Belated return | 139(4) | 263(4) |
| Revised return | 139(5) | 263(5) |
| Updated return (ITR-U) | 139(8A) | 263(6) |

---

## Due Dates for Return Filing

Under Section 263(1)(c) of the 2025 Act, the due dates for filing returns are identical to those under the old Act:

| Person Category | Due Date |
|---|---|
| Partner of firm with transfer pricing provisions (Section 172) | 30 November |
| Company / person requiring audit / partner of audited firm | 31 October |
| Business or profession income (no audit required) / partner of non-audited firm | 31 August |
| Any other assessee | 31 July |

---

## No Double Filing — The AY vs TY Distinction

AY 2026-27 and Tax Year 2026-27 are **separate obligations for different income periods**. There is no double filing for the same income.

| | AY 2026-27 | Tax Year 2026-27 |
|---|---|---|
| **Income period** | FY 2025-26 (April 2025 – March 2026) | FY 2026-27 (April 2026 – March 2027) |
| **Governing Act** | Income Tax Act, 1961 | Income Tax Act, 2025 |
| **Return due** | July – November 2026 | July – November 2027 |
| **Forms** | Old ITR forms (ITR-1 through ITR-7) | New ITR forms under IT Rules, 2026 |
| **Portal selection** | Select "AY 2026-27" | Select "Tax Year 2026-27" |

---

## AY 2026-27 — Old Act Governs Entirely

The return for FY 2025-26 income is filed under the Income Tax Act, 1961, even though the filing occurs after April 1, 2026. The following rules apply:

| Activity | Governing Provision | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Original return | Old Section 139(1) | Filed using old ITR forms, due per old Act schedule |
| Belated return | Old Section 139(4) | Deadline: 31 December 2026 |
| Revised return | Old Section 139(5) | Deadline: before 31 March 2027 |
| Updated return (ITR-U) | Old Section 139(8A) | Within prescribed time under old Act |
| Late filing fee | Old Section 234F | ₹1,000 if total income ≤ ₹5 lakh; ₹5,000 otherwise |

### Returns for AY 2025-26 and Earlier

Revised or belated returns for AY 2025-26 or any earlier Assessment Year **cannot** be filed after April 1, 2026. The time limits for those returns will have already expired under the old Act. Section 536(2)(k) confirms that expired limitation is not revived by the new Act.

---

## Time Limits — Belated, Revised, and Updated Returns Under New Act

From Tax Year 2026-27 onwards, the following time limits apply:

| Type | Section (ITA 2025) | Time Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Belated return | 263(4) | 9 months from end of relevant tax year, or before completion of assessment, whichever is earlier |
| Revised return | 263(5) | 12 months from end of relevant tax year, or before completion of assessment, whichever is earlier |
| Updated return (ITR-U) | 263(6) | 48 months from end of financial year succeeding the relevant tax year |

### Comparison with Old Act

| Type | Old Act Time Limit | New Act Time Limit | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Belated return | End of relevant AY or before assessment | 9 months from end of tax year or before assessment | Explicitly quantified — 9 months |
| Revised return | End of relevant AY or before assessment | 12 months from end of tax year or before assessment | Extended — 12 months |
| Updated return | 24/36/48 months from end of relevant AY | 48 months from end of FY succeeding relevant TY | Retained at outer limit |

## Late Filing Fee

| Act | Section | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| ITA 1961 | 234F | ₹1,000 if total income ≤ ₹5 lakh; ₹5,000 otherwise |
| ITA 2025 | 428 | ₹1,000 if total income ≤ ₹5 lakh; ₹5,000 otherwise |

The amounts are identical. Section 428 applies from Tax Year 2026-27 onwards. For AY 2026-27 and earlier, old Section 234F applies.

---

## Loss Returns — Carry-Forward Implications

The filing of loss returns during the transition period has direct consequences for carry-forward under the new Act:

| Situation | Carry-Forward Under New Act |
|---|---|
| Loss from FY 2025-26, return filed within due date | **Available** — Section 536(2)(m) preserves carry-forward |
| Loss from FY 2025-26, return filed belatedly | **Not available** — ITA 2025 cannot cure defects of old Act; belated filing forfeits carry-forward |

Filing within the due date remains a prerequisite for carry-forward of losses. The new Act preserves losses that were validly filed under the old Act but does not override the old Act's requirement of timely filing.

## Tax Audit During Transition

Section 63 of the 2025 Act corresponds to Section 44AB of the old Act. The thresholds for mandatory tax audit remain unchanged.

| Tax Year | Governing Act | Audit Form | Due Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) | ITA 1961 | Old forms (3CA/3CB/3CD) | 30 September 2026 |
| FY 2026-27 (TY 2026-27) | ITA 2025 | Form 26 (single merged form) | 30 September 2027 |

### Form 26 — Key Features

Form 26 replaces the three old audit forms with a single consolidated report. Its key features include:

- Consolidated disclosure replacing the separate 3CA (positive opinion), 3CB (negative opinion), and 3CD (particulars) structure
- ITR-aligned schedules for seamless cross-referencing with the return of income
- UDIN (Unique Document Identification Number) mandatory for all audit reports
- Dedicated schedules for losses, depreciation, and prior period items

## E-Filing Portal — Dual-Act Support

Per KarSetu FAQ Q3.27, the e-filing portal supports both old and new form formats simultaneously:

- Taxpayers select either **Assessment Year** (for old Act filings) or **Tax Year** (for new Act filings) on the portal
- The portal serves the correct forms based on the selection
- Old forms continue to be available for AY 2026-27 and earlier years
- New forms are served for Tax Year 2026-27 onwards
- Both old-format and new-format returns can be filed concurrently during the transition period

## Practical Impact

- **AY 2026-27 returns use old forms and old Act exclusively**: Even though filed after April 2026, the old Act governs all aspects — forms, due dates, belated/revised return deadlines, and late filing fees
- **No double filing**: AY 2026-27 and TY 2026-27 are separate obligations for separate income periods — practitioners must file both, but each covers a different financial year
- **Form 15G and 15H merge into Form 121**: A single self-declaration form replaces the age-based bifurcation — applicable from TY 2026-27 onwards
- **Tax audit consolidation**: Three audit forms (3CA/3CB/3CD) merge into Form 26 — a significant simplification, but only for TY 2026-27 onwards
- **Four challan-cum-TDS forms merge into Form 141**: Forms 26QB, 26QC, 26QD, and 26QE are consolidated into a single form
- **Loss carry-forward requires timely filing**: A loss from FY 2025-26 filed belatedly cannot be carried forward under the new Act — the 2025 Act preserves validly filed losses but does not cure old Act defects
- **Expired return deadlines are final**: Revised or belated returns for AY 2025-26 or earlier cannot be filed after April 1, 2026 — time has already expired under the old Act
- **Portal year selection remains critical**: Selecting AY vs TY on the portal determines which Act's forms are served — incorrect selection leads to wrong forms and potential processing errors

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 139 maps to Section 263** — sub-section structure preserved for original, belated, revised, and updated returns
- **Due dates are unchanged**: 31 July / 31 August / 31 October / 30 November, depending on person category
- **AY 2026-27 and TY 2026-27 are separate obligations** — different income periods, different Acts, different forms, different filing years
- **AY 2026-27 returns are governed entirely by the old Act** — old ITR forms, old section numbers, old belated/revised return deadlines
- **Belated return under new Act**: 9 months from end of tax year; **Revised return**: 12 months from end of tax year
- **Updated return (ITR-U)**: 48 months from end of FY succeeding relevant tax year — Section 263(6)
- **Late filing fee (Section 428 = old Section 234F)**: ₹1,000 / ₹5,000 — amounts unchanged
- **Loss carry-forward requires timely filing** — belated loss returns forfeit carry-forward; the new Act does not cure old Act defects
- **Form mergers**: 15G + 15H → Form 121; 3CA + 3CB + 3CD → Form 26; 26QB + 26QC + 26QD + 26QE → Form 141
- **Tax audit threshold unchanged** (Section 63 = old Section 44AB); FY 2025-26 uses old forms, TY 2026-27 uses Form 26
- **E-filing portal supports both Acts simultaneously** — correct AY/TY selection serves correct forms
