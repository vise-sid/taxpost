# Unit 5.1: Income from House Property

## Overview

The House Property chapter is one of the cleanest migrations in the 2025 Act — eight sections (22 through 27) compressed into six (20 through 25), with the word count trimmed from 1,658 to 1,177. Most of this is structural tidying: provisos and explanations converted into numbered sub-sections, unrealised rent merged into the deductions section. But two substantive changes buried in this cleanup will affect real client situations — one on the pre-construction interest cap, one on self-occupied property eligibility. Everything else is renumbering.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 22 | **20** | Chargeability | Renumbered only |
| 23 | **21** | Annual value determination (incl. self-occupied, deemed let-out) | Renumbered + substantive change |
| 24 | **22** | Deductions (30% standard deduction + interest on borrowed capital) | Renumbered + substantive clarification |
| 25 (unrealised rent) | **22** (partly merged) | Unrealised rent | Merged into deductions |
| 25A | **23** | Arrears / unrealised rent received subsequently | Renumbered only |
| 26 | **24** | Co-owners | Renumbered only |
| 27 (deemed owner) | **21** (partly merged) + **25** | Deemed ownership / definition of "owner" | Restructured |

### Structural Observation

The old chapter had unrealised rent as a standalone section (Section 25) and deemed ownership definitions buried in Section 27 with its own set of provisos. The 2025 Act merges unrealised rent into the deductions section (Section 22) and splits old Section 27 between Section 21 (deemed ownership situations folded into annual value determination) and Section 25 (interpretation — definition of "owner"). Cleaner, but you need to know where things moved.

---

## What Changed — The Two Substantive Changes

### CHANGE 1: Pre-Construction Interest Cap Clarified (Section 22(2))

**This is the change that resolves years of ambiguity.**

Under old Section 24(b), the Rs 2,00,000 cap on interest deduction for self-occupied property applied to "interest payable on borrowed capital." Pre-construction interest (deductible in 5 equal annual installments from the year of completion) was allowed separately, and the interaction between the two was never explicitly stated in the statute. ITAT decisions went both ways. Most practitioners applied a combined cap, but the statutory text left room for argument.

**New Section 22(2) settles this.** The Rs 2,00,000 cap explicitly includes both:
- Current year interest, AND
- The 1/5th pre-construction interest installment for that year

Combined. No ambiguity. The aggregate of both cannot exceed Rs 2,00,000.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 24(b)) | New Act (Section 22(2)) |
|---|---|---|
| Cap for self-occupied property | Rs 2,00,000 | Rs 2,00,000 |
| Pre-construction interest treatment | Deductible in 5 installments; interaction with cap unclear in statute | Deductible in 5 installments; **explicitly included within the Rs 2L cap** |
| Statutory clarity | Ambiguous — relied on circulars and tribunal rulings | **Codified in the Act itself** |

**Practical impact:** If you had clients claiming pre-construction interest as an additional deduction over and above the Rs 2L cap, that position is now untenable. For most practitioners who were already applying the combined cap, this changes nothing in practice — it just removes the litigation risk from the other side challenging your computation.

---

### CHANGE 2: Self-Occupied Property — "Any Reason" Liberalization (Section 21)

**This one benefits NRIs and multi-city professionals significantly.**

Under old Section 23(2), a property could be treated as self-occupied (nil annual value) only if the owner could not occupy it "by reason of his employment, business or profession carried on at any other place" and had to reside at that other place in a building not owned by him.

This change was introduced by Finance Act 2025 as an amendment to old Section 23(2) of the 1961 Act, already operative from AY 2025-26. The 2025 Act carries it forward. **Nil annual value can now be claimed for non-occupation for "any reason"** — not just employment/business/profession at another location.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 23(2)) | New Act (Section 21) |
|---|---|---|
| Nil annual value eligibility | Only if non-occupation due to employment/business/profession at another place | Non-occupation for **"any reason"** |
| NRI with property in India | Had to demonstrate employment abroad as the reason | **Eligible regardless of reason** for non-occupation |
| CA with ancestral home in another city | Covered only if residing elsewhere for professional reasons | **Covered unconditionally** |
| 2 self-occupied property limit | Applied | **Still applies** — this liberalization does not change the 2-property cap |

**Practical impact:** Review NRI client portfolios. Properties that were being offered to tax as deemed let-out (because the NRI could not satisfy the "employment at another place" condition) may now qualify for nil annual value treatment. Same for clients who own properties in their hometown that they don't occupy for personal (non-professional) reasons.

> **Watch out:** The 2-property limit still applies. This liberalization expands *who* can claim nil annual value, not *how many* properties qualify. If your client owns three or more properties, one is still deemed let-out regardless of the reason for non-occupation.

## What Stayed the Same

This is the longer list — and that is the point. The HP computation framework is largely untouched:

- **Standard deduction**: 30% of Net Annual Value — retained (Section 22)
- **Interest deduction cap**: Rs 2,00,000 for self-occupied property — retained (Section 22)
- **No cap on interest** for let-out property — retained
- **2 self-occupied property limit** — retained
- **Municipal taxes**: Deductible on actual payment basis — retained
- **Vacancy allowance**: Available for let-out property that was vacant for part of the year — retained
- **Pre-construction interest**: Deductible in 5 equal annual installments from year of completion — retained
- **Deemed ownership**: All 6 categories (transferee under transfer for inadequate consideration, holder of impartible estate, member of co-operative society allotted a flat, person in possession under Part Performance, person with rights under power of attorney, lessee with leasehold rights) — all retained
- **Arrears/unrealised rent received subsequently**: 30% standard deduction available — retained (Section 23)
- **Co-owner taxation**: Each co-owner taxed on their share proportionately — retained (Section 24)

**Bottom line: If you know how to compute HP income under the old Act, you know how to compute it under the new Act. Update section references. Apply the two substantive changes above. Everything else carries over.**

## Practical Impact

| Area | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Section references** | Replace Section 22/23/24/25/25A/26/27 with Sections 20-25 in all computation sheets, opinions, and templates |
| **Pre-construction interest** | Confirm that all ongoing computations treat the 1/5th installment as part of the Rs 2L cap, not in addition to it. No new action if you were already doing this. |
| **NRI clients** | Reassess properties classified as deemed let-out. If the only reason was inability to prove "employment at another place," the property may now qualify for nil annual value under the "any reason" rule. |
| **Multi-city professionals** | Same reassessment — ancestral homes or secondary properties not occupied for personal reasons now eligible for self-occupied treatment (subject to 2-property cap). |
| **Unrealised rent** | Now sits in Section 22 (deductions), not a standalone section. Update cross-references. Substance unchanged. |
| **Deemed ownership** | Definition of "owner" now in Section 25 (interpretation), deemed ownership situations partly in Section 21. Update references; no change in who qualifies as deemed owner. |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **6 sections** (20-25) replace 8 sections (22-27) — a cleaner, shorter chapter with provisos converted to sub-sections.
- **Two substantive changes** — pre-construction interest cap clarification (Section 22(2)) and "any reason" liberalization for self-occupied property (Section 21). Everything else is renumbering.
- **Pre-construction interest**: The Rs 2L cap now explicitly covers both current-year interest and the 1/5th pre-construction installment combined. Ambiguity eliminated.
- **Self-occupied eligibility**: Non-occupation for "any reason" qualifies for nil annual value — not just employment/business/profession. Major relief for NRIs and multi-city property owners.
- **30% standard deduction, interest caps, vacancy allowance, deemed ownership, co-owner rules** — all unchanged in substance.
- **Unrealised rent** merged into deductions section (old Section 25 → new Section 22). Arrears/unrealised rent received subsequently still gets 30% standard deduction (Section 23).
- **The HP computation itself is identical.** Update your section numbers. Apply the two changes. Move on.
