# Unit 9.4: Education & Housing Deductions (Sections 129-132, 134)

## Overview

The education and housing deduction cluster under the 1961 Act — Sections 80E, 80EE, 80EEA, 80EEB, and 80GG — has been renumbered to Sections 129, 130, 131, 132, and 134 under the 2025 Act. The critical practical distinction within this group is between **operative deductions** and **sunset deductions**. Of the five sections, only **Section 129 (education loan interest)** and **Section 134 (rent without HRA)** remain fully operative for new claims. Sections 130, 131, and 132 relate to housing and electric vehicle loan interest deductions that were tied to specific time-bound windows; those windows have closed, and no new eligibility can arise. However, existing claims that originated within the eligible window **continue under transitional provisions**.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Status | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 80E | **129** | Interest on education loan | **Operative** | Renumbered only |
| 80EE | **130** | Additional interest — first-time homebuyers (FY 2016-17 loans) | **Sunset** | Renumbered only |
| 80EEA | **131** | Additional interest — affordable housing (2019-2022 loans) | **Sunset** | Renumbered only |
| 80EEB | **132** | Interest on electric vehicle loan (2019-2023 loans) | **Sunset** | Renumbered only |
| 80GG | **134** | Rent paid by individuals not receiving HRA | **Operative** | Renumbered only |

## Section 129: Interest on Education Loan (Old Section 80E)

Section 129 provides a deduction for interest paid on a loan taken for **higher education**. This is one of the more generous deductions in the Act — there is no monetary cap on the deduction.

### Key Parameters

| Parameter | Position | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| Eligible assessee | Individual only | No |
| Purpose of loan | Higher education of self, spouse, children, or student for whom the assessee is legal guardian | No |
| "Higher education" | Any course of study pursued after passing Senior Secondary Examination (Class 12) or equivalent, at a recognised institution in India or abroad | No |
| Deduction amount | **Entire interest component** — no monetary cap | No |
| Duration | Available for **8 consecutive assessment years**, starting from the year in which interest is first paid, or until the interest is fully repaid, whichever is earlier | No |
| Loan source | Financial institution or approved charitable institution | No |
| Principal repayment | Not deductible under this section (may qualify under Section 123 if other conditions met) | No |

### Regime Availability

Section 129 is available under **both the old and new tax regimes**.

> **No substantive change.** All conditions, the no-cap structure, and the 8-year window continue from old Section 80E.

## Section 130: Additional Interest — First-Time Homebuyers (Old Section 80EE) — SUNSET

Section 130 carries forward old Section 80EE, which provided an **additional deduction of Rs 50,000** for interest on a housing loan for first-time homebuyers. This deduction is effectively **sunset** — no new eligibility can arise.

### Eligibility Window

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Loan sanctioned during | **FY 2016-17** (1 April 2016 to 31 March 2017) |
| Loan amount | Does not exceed Rs 35,00,000 |
| Property value | Does not exceed Rs 50,00,000 |
| Assessee | Individual; should not own any other residential house on the date of sanction |
| Deduction amount | Rs 50,000 per year (over and above Section 24(b) / new Section 44 limit) |

### Current Status

The loan sanction window closed on 31 March 2017. No new loans can qualify. However, assessees who took eligible loans within the window and have outstanding interest **continue to claim the deduction** in subsequent years until the loan is fully repaid. These continuing claims are preserved through the transitional provisions of the 2025 Act.

### Regime Availability

Available **only under the old tax regime** (for continuing claims).

> **No substantive change.** The sunset nature is inherent in the original provision's design — the 2025 Act merely carries forward an already time-expired benefit.

## Section 131: Additional Interest — Affordable Housing (Old Section 80EEA) — SUNSET

Section 131 carries forward old Section 80EEA, which provided an **additional deduction of Rs 1,50,000** for interest on a housing loan for affordable housing.

### Eligibility Window

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Loan sanctioned during | **1 April 2019 to 31 March 2022** |
| Stamp duty value of property | Does not exceed Rs 45,00,000 |
| Assessee | Individual; should not own any other residential house on the date of sanction |
| Deduction amount | Rs 1,50,000 per year (over and above Section 24(b) / new Section 44 limit) |
| Co-existence with Section 130 | Cannot be claimed if deduction under Section 130 (old 80EE) is claimed for the same property |

### Current Status

The loan sanction window closed on 31 March 2022. No new loans can qualify. Continuing claims for loans sanctioned within the window are preserved through transitional provisions.

### Regime Availability

Available **only under the old tax regime** (for continuing claims).

> **No substantive change.** The 2025 Act carries forward an already time-expired benefit.

---

## Section 132: Interest on Electric Vehicle Loan (Old Section 80EEB) — SUNSET

Section 132 carries forward old Section 80EEB, which provided a deduction of up to **Rs 1,50,000** for interest on a loan taken to purchase an electric vehicle.

### Eligibility Window

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Loan sanctioned during | **1 April 2019 to 31 March 2023** |
| Vehicle | Electric vehicle (as defined — a vehicle propelled by electric motor whose traction energy is supplied by a battery, ultracapacitor, fuel cell, or other energy storage device) |
| Assessee | Individual |
| Deduction amount | Up to Rs 1,50,000 per year on the interest component |

### Current Status

The loan sanction window closed on 31 March 2023. No new loans can qualify. Continuing claims for loans sanctioned within the window are preserved through transitional provisions.

### Regime Availability

Available **only under the old tax regime** (for continuing claims).

> **No substantive change.** The 2025 Act carries forward an already time-expired benefit. The government has not extended the window despite the push towards EV adoption.

## Section 134: Rent Paid Without HRA (Old Section 80GG)

Section 134 provides a deduction for rent paid by individuals who do **not receive House Rent Allowance (HRA)** from their employer. This section is fully **operative** and relevant for self-employed individuals, pensioners, and employees whose salary structure does not include HRA.

### Eligibility Conditions

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Assessee | Individual (salaried or self-employed) |
| HRA status | Must **not** be receiving HRA that is exempt under Section 15(14)(ii) / old Section 10(13A) |
| Self/spouse/minor child | Must **not** own a residential house at the place of employment/business |
| Owned property elsewhere | If the assessee owns a residential house at any other place, it must be treated as self-occupied and no income should be claimed from it under "Income from House Property" at the other place |
| Form 10BA | A declaration in Form 10BA must be filed |

### Deduction Amount

The deduction is the **least** of the following three amounts:

| Limb | Amount |
|---|---|
| (a) | Rent paid minus 10% of Adjusted Total Income |
| (b) | **Rs 5,000 per month** (i.e., Rs 60,000 per year) |
| (c) | 25% of Adjusted Total Income |

"Adjusted Total Income" means the gross total income after deducting all deductions under Chapter VIII except this section.

### Regime Availability

Section 134 is available **only under the old tax regime**.

> **No substantive change.** All conditions, limits (including the Rs 5,000/month cap), and computational mechanics continue from old Section 80GG.

## Operative vs. Sunset — Summary

| New Section | Old Section | Subject | Status | New Claims Possible? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **129** | 80E | Education loan interest | **Operative** | Yes — any new education loan |
| **130** | 80EE | First-time homebuyer interest | **Sunset** | No — loan window closed Mar 2017 |
| **131** | 80EEA | Affordable housing interest | **Sunset** | No — loan window closed Mar 2022 |
| **132** | 80EEB | Electric vehicle loan interest | **Sunset** | No — loan window closed Mar 2023 |
| **134** | 80GG | Rent without HRA | **Operative** | Yes — ongoing eligibility |

### Transitional Provisions for Sunset Sections

The 2025 Act does not extinguish claims that originated during the eligible window. Assessees who obtained qualifying loans within the specified periods and continue to pay interest may continue to claim the deduction under the relevant section of the 2025 Act (Sections 130, 131, or 132), subject to the same conditions and limits. The transitional provisions in the Seventh Schedule ensure continuity.

| Topic | Old Position | New Position | Nature of Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Section mapping | 80E, 80EE, 80EEA, 80EEB, 80GG | 129, 130, 131, 132, 134 | Renumbered |
| Education loan interest (Section 129) | No cap, 8 years | No cap, 8 years | No change |
| Education loan regime | Both regimes | Both regimes | No change |
| First-time homebuyer (Section 130) | Sunset (FY 2016-17 loans) | Sunset — continuing claims preserved | No change |
| Affordable housing (Section 131) | Sunset (2019-2022 loans) | Sunset — continuing claims preserved | No change |
| Electric vehicle (Section 132) | Sunset (2019-2023 loans) | Sunset — continuing claims preserved | No change |
