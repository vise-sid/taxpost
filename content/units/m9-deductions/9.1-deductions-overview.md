# Unit 9.1: Deductions Overview (Chapter VIII, Sections 122-154)

## Overview

The deduction framework under the 1961 Act — the sprawling Chapter VI-A with Sections 80A through 80U — has been reorganised under **Chapter VIII** of the 2025 Act, spanning Sections 122 to 154. The consolidation reduces the section count substantially, but the underlying deduction architecture is preserved. The general rules governing deductions (aggregate cap, double-deduction bar, return-filing condition) have been gathered into a single umbrella provision — **Section 122** — replacing the scattered Sections 80A, 80AB, and 80AC. The deductions themselves are split into **Part B (Sections 123-137)**, covering payment-based deductions, and **Part C (Sections 138-154)**, covering income-based and business-specific deductions. A critical practical distinction: the timely return-filing requirement under Section 122(5) applies **only to Part C deductions**, not to Part B.

## Old → New Mapping: General Provisions

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80A | **122** | General rules — aggregate limit, double-deduction bar | Consolidated |
| 80AB | **122** | Deduction computed with reference to income included in GTI | Merged into 122 |
| 80AC | **122** | Return-filing condition for certain deductions | Merged into 122 |
| Chapter VI-A, Part B | **Part B (Sections 123-137)** | Payment-based deductions (investment, insurance, health, rent, donations) | Reorganised |
| Chapter VI-A, Part C | **Part C (Sections 138-154)** | Income-based / business-specific deductions (profits, royalty, cooperatives, interest) | Reorganised |

## Section 122: General Rules Governing Deductions

Section 122 consolidates the overarching rules that apply to all deductions under Chapter VIII. Previously, these rules were scattered across three separate sections (80A, 80AB, 80AC) and had to be read together. The 2025 Act brings them under one roof.

### Section 122(1): Deductions from Gross Total Income

Deductions under Chapter VIII are allowed from the **Gross Total Income (GTI)** of the assessee. GTI is the total income computed before allowing deductions under this chapter.

### Section 122(2): Aggregate Cap

The aggregate amount of deductions under Chapter VIII **cannot exceed the GTI** of the assessee. If the total of all claimed deductions exceeds GTI, the deductions are restricted to GTI. This prevents deductions from creating or enlarging a loss.

This is a direct carryover of the old Section 80A(2) rule. No substantive change.

### Section 122(3): Deduction Computed with Reference to Income

Where a deduction is allowable with reference to income included in GTI, the deduction is computed on the **net income** (i.e., income as computed under the relevant head, after deducting allowable expenditure). This prevents deductions being claimed on gross receipts when the law intends them to apply to net income.

This is a carryover of old Section 80AB. No substantive change.

### Section 122(4): Double Deduction Bar

No deduction is allowed under Chapter VIII in respect of any amount that has already been allowed as a deduction under any other provision of the Act (whether under the same head of income or a different head). The principle is straightforward: the same expenditure or income cannot generate a deduction twice.

This is a carryover of old Section 80A(4). No substantive change.

### Section 122(5): Timely Return-Filing Condition — Part C Only

This is the provision that requires the most careful attention. A deduction under **Part C (Sections 138-154)** is available only if the return of income is filed **on or before the due date** specified under Section 263(1) (old Section 139(1)).

**Critical distinction:** This condition applies **only to Part C deductions** (income-based and business-specific deductions such as deductions for business profits, cooperative income, royalty income, etc.). It does **not** apply to **Part B deductions** (Sections 123-137), which cover payment-based deductions like 80C investments, health insurance, NPS, education loan interest, rent, and donations. Part B deductions remain available even if the return is filed after the due date (subject to the return being filed within the permissible belated period).

| Deduction Type | Sections | Timely Filing Required? |
|---|---|---|
| **Part B** — Payment-based | 123-137 | **No** |
| **Part C** — Income-based / Business-specific | 138-154 | **Yes** |

> **Practical Tip:** Old Section 80AC imposed the return-filing condition on select deductions (80-IA, 80-IAB, 80-IB, 80-IC, 80-ID, 80-IE, 80JJA, 80JJAA, 80LA, 80P, 80PA, 80QQB, 80RRB). The 2025 Act simplifies this by applying the condition to the entirety of Part C, rather than listing individual sections. The scope is effectively the same — the business/income-based deductions that always required timely filing continue to require it.

## New Regime vs. Old Regime — Deduction Availability

A key practical concern is which deductions survive under the **new (default) tax regime**. The 2025 Act continues the position established by Finance Act 2023: the new regime disallows most Chapter VIII deductions, with specific exceptions.

### Deductions Available Under BOTH Regimes

| New Section | Subject | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **124** | Employer NPS contribution | 14% of salary |
| **125** | Agniveer Corpus Fund (government contribution) | Actual |
| **126** | Health insurance premiums | Rs 25,000 / Rs 50,000 |
| **129** | Education loan interest | No cap (8 years) |
| **145** | New employee cost (80JJAA) | 30% of additional employee cost |
| **146** | Offshore banking / IFSC (80LA) | 100% |
| **153** | Savings account / deposit interest | Rs 10,000 / Rs 50,000 |

### Deductions Available ONLY Under Old Regime

All other deductions under Sections 123, 127, 128, 130-132, 134-144, 147-150, 154 are available **only** to assessees who opt out of the default new regime.

## Key Takeaways

| Topic | Old Position | New Position | Nature of Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| General rules | Sections 80A, 80AB, 80AC (three sections) | **Section 122** (single section) | Drafting consolidation |
| Aggregate cap (deductions ≤ GTI) | 80A(2) | 122(2) | Carried forward |
| Double deduction bar | 80A(4) | 122(4) | Carried forward |
| Timely filing condition | 80AC (listed individual sections) | 122(5) (applies to entire Part C) | Simplified; same scope |
| Part B vs. Part C | No formal split | Formal split in Act structure | Structural improvement |
| Part B — no timely filing needed | Implicit | Explicit under 122(5) | Clarification |
| 80TTA + 80TTB | Separate sections | Merged into Section 153 | Drafting consolidation |
