# Unit 9.3: Health & Disability Deductions (Sections 126-128, 154)

## Overview

The health and disability deduction cluster — Sections 80D, 80DD, 80DDB, and 80U under the 1961 Act — has been renumbered to Sections 126, 127, 128, and 154 under the 2025 Act. **All monetary limits remain unchanged.** The single most important practical point for assessees under the new (default) tax regime is that **Section 126 (health insurance) is available under both regimes** — a significant benefit that many practitioners overlook. The remaining three sections (127, 128, and 154) continue to be available only under the old regime.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80D | **126** | Health insurance premiums and preventive health checkup | Renumbered only |
| 80DD | **127** | Maintenance including medical treatment of disabled dependent | Renumbered only |
| 80DDB | **128** | Medical treatment of specified diseases | Renumbered only |
| 80U | **154** | Person with disability — self | Renumbered only |

> **Note on Section 154 placement:** Old Section 80U has been placed at Section 154, which falls within Part C of Chapter VIII. Despite this placement, the deduction is person-based (not income-based), and the timely return-filing condition under Section 122(5) applies to Part C deductions. Practitioners should ensure timely filing to safeguard this claim.

---

## Section 126: Health Insurance Premiums (Old Section 80D)

Section 126 allows a deduction for premiums paid towards health insurance policies and preventive health checkups.

### Eligible Payments

| Payment | For Whom | Eligible |
|---|---|---|
| Health insurance premium | Self, spouse, dependent children | Yes |
| Health insurance premium | Parents (dependent or independent) | Yes — separate limit |
| Preventive health checkup | Self, spouse, dependent children, parents | Yes — within overall limit |
| Medical expenditure (in lieu of insurance) | Senior citizen (self or parents) without health insurance | Yes — treated as premium |

### Deduction Limits

| Category | Maximum Deduction | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| Self, spouse, dependent children — **below 60 years** | **Rs 25,000** | No |
| Self, spouse, dependent children — **60 years or above** (senior citizen) | **Rs 50,000** | No |
| Parents — **below 60 years** | **Rs 25,000** (separate limit) | No |
| Parents — **60 years or above** (senior citizen) | **Rs 50,000** (separate limit) | No |
| **Maximum combined deduction** (self family + parents, both senior) | **Rs 1,00,000** | No |

### Preventive Health Checkup

The deduction for preventive health checkup expenditure is **Rs 5,000**, but this is **not an additional deduction** — it falls within the overall limits specified above.

| Feature | Position |
|---|---|
| Limit | Rs 5,000 |
| Scope | Within the Rs 25,000 / Rs 50,000 ceiling |
| Payment mode | Cash payment is permitted (unlike insurance premiums, which must be paid by non-cash modes) |

### Regime Availability

**Section 126 is available under BOTH the old and new tax regimes.**

This is a significant practical benefit. An assessee under the default new regime who pays health insurance premium for self and parents can claim deductions of up to Rs 1,00,000 (in the case of senior citizen parents and senior citizen self). This is one of the few deductions that survives the new regime's stripped-down deduction framework.

> **No substantive change.** All limits, conditions, and eligibility criteria continue from the 1961 Act position. The availability under both regimes was established by Finance Act 2023 and is carried forward.

## Section 127: Maintenance of Disabled Dependent (Old Section 80DD)

Section 127 provides a deduction to a **resident individual or HUF** who incurs expenditure on the medical treatment, training, and rehabilitation of a dependent with a disability, or who deposits amounts in specified schemes for the maintenance of such dependent.

### Who is a "Dependent"?

- For an individual: spouse, children, parents, brothers, sisters
- For an HUF: any member of the HUF

The dependent must be a person with a disability as certified by the prescribed medical authority.

### Deduction Amounts

| Disability Level | Deduction (Fixed Amount) | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| Disability (40% or more) | **Rs 75,000** | No |
| Severe disability (80% or more) | **Rs 1,25,000** | No |

These are **flat deductions** — they are not dependent on the actual expenditure incurred. The assessee claims either Rs 75,000 or Rs 1,25,000 based on the level of disability of the dependent.

### Key Conditions

- A **disability certificate** from a prescribed medical authority must be obtained.
- If the dependent predeceases the assessee, or the assessee fails to maintain the insurance/deposit, the deduction is reversed.
- The deduction is available even if no actual expenditure is incurred — the mere existence of a disabled dependent entitles the assessee to the flat deduction.
- The dependent should **not have claimed a deduction under Section 154 (old 80U)** on their own — only one of the two deductions (127 for the caregiver or 154 for the person with disability) can be claimed, not both for the same individual.

### Regime Availability

Section 127 is available **only under the old tax regime**.

> **No substantive change.** All conditions, limits, and restrictions continue from old Section 80DD.

## Section 128: Medical Treatment of Specified Diseases (Old Section 80DDB)

Section 128 provides a deduction for expenditure actually incurred on the **medical treatment** of specified diseases or ailments (as prescribed in the rules) for the assessee or a dependent.

### Eligible Assessees

- **Resident individuals** — for self or dependent
- **Resident HUFs** — for any member

### Specified Diseases

The list of specified diseases is prescribed by Rule 11DD and includes:

- Neurological diseases (dementia, dystonia musculorum deformans, motor neuron disease, ataxia, chorea, hemiballismus, aphasia, Parkinson's disease)
- Malignant cancers
- AIDS
- Chronic renal failure
- Haematological disorders (haemophilia, thalassaemia)

### Deduction Limits

| Category of Patient | Maximum Deduction | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| Patient **below 60 years** | **Rs 40,000** | No |
| Patient **60 years or above** (senior citizen) | **Rs 1,00,000** | No |

The deduction is limited to the **actual expenditure incurred** or the maximum limit, whichever is lower. If the expenditure is reimbursed by an insurer or employer, the reimbursed portion must be excluded.

### Regime Availability

Section 128 is available **only under the old tax regime**.

> **No substantive change.** All conditions, limits, disease specifications, and restrictions continue from old Section 80DDB.

## Section 154: Deduction for Person with Disability — Self (Old Section 80U)

Section 154 provides a deduction to a **resident individual** who suffers from a disability. Unlike Section 127 (which is for the caregiver of a disabled dependent), Section 154 is claimed by the **disabled person themselves**.

### Deduction Amounts

| Disability Level | Deduction (Fixed Amount) | Changed? |
|---|---|---|
| Disability (40% or more) | **Rs 75,000** | No |
| Severe disability (80% or more) | **Rs 1,25,000** | No |

### Key Conditions

- A **disability certificate** from a prescribed medical authority must be furnished.
- The certificate must be obtained before the due date for filing the return of income.
- The disability must be certified as existing during the relevant tax year.
- Disability is defined under the Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, or the National Trust for Welfare of Persons with Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation and Multiple Disabilities Act, 1999.

### Regime Availability

Section 154 is available **only under the old tax regime**.

> **Placement Note:** Section 154 falls in Part C of Chapter VIII. This means the **timely return-filing condition under Section 122(5) applies**. The return must be filed on or before the due date to claim this deduction.

> **No substantive change.** All conditions, limits, and requirements continue from old Section 80U.

| Topic | Old Position | New Position | Nature of Change |

| Section mapping | 80D, 80DD, 80DDB, 80U | 126, 127, 128, 154 | Renumbered |

| Preventive checkup within limit | Rs 5,000 | Rs 5,000 | No change |
| Disabled dependent (Section 127) | Rs 75K / Rs 1.25L | Rs 75K / Rs 1.25L | No change |
| Specified disease (Section 128) | Rs 40K / Rs 1L | Rs 40K / Rs 1L | No change |
| Self-disability (Section 154) | Rs 75K / Rs 1.25L | Rs 75K / Rs 1.25L | No change |
| Section 126 under new regime | Available (Finance Act 2023) | **Available — both regimes** | Carried forward |
