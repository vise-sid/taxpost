# Unit 9.5: Donations & Contributions (Sections 133-137)

## Overview

The 2025 Act consolidates the four donation-related deduction provisions — Sections 80G, 80GGA, 80GGB, and 80GGC of the 1961 Act — into Sections 133 through 137. The four-tier structure of Section 80G (100% without limit, 50% without limit, 100% with qualifying limit, 50% with qualifying limit) is preserved intact under Section 133. The cash donation restriction (no deduction for donations exceeding Rs 2,000 in cash) continues. The cap based on 10% of adjusted total income applies to donations in the "qualifying limit" tiers. There are no substantive changes to eligibility, limits, or computation — the consolidation is presentational, bringing all donation-related deductions under a contiguous block within the unified deduction regime of the 2025 Act.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80G | **133** | Donations to certain funds, charitable institutions, etc. | Renumbered — improved presentation |
| 80GGA | **135** | Donations for scientific research or rural development | Renumbered only |
| 80GGB | **136** | Contributions by companies to political parties | Renumbered only |
| 80GGC | **137** | Contributions by any person (other than company) to political parties | Renumbered only |

## Section 133: Donations to Funds and Charitable Institutions (Old Section 80G)

### Four-Tier Deduction Structure

Section 133 retains the well-established four-tier framework for computing the allowable deduction on donations:

| Tier | Deduction Rate | Qualifying Limit Applies? | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | 100% | No | National Defence Fund, PM's National Relief Fund, National Foundation for Communal Harmony |
| **Tier 2** | 50% | No | PM's Drought Relief Fund, National Children's Fund |
| **Tier 3** | 100% | Yes (10% of adjusted total income) | Government or approved local authority for family planning, approved institution for medical relief to the poor |
| **Tier 4** | 50% | Yes (10% of adjusted total income) | Any approved charitable institution or trust (the residual category — most 80G registrations fall here) |

### Qualifying Limit — 10% Cap

For donations falling in Tier 3 and Tier 4, the aggregate deduction cannot exceed **10% of the adjusted total income** of the assessee. Adjusted total income is the gross total income reduced by all deductions under Sections 123 to 154 **except** the donation deduction itself, and reduced by exempt income, LTCG, STCG under Section 111A (old Act terms), and income referred to in Sections 115A-115D of the old Act.

The 10% cap operates on the aggregate of Tier 3 and Tier 4 donations combined — not independently for each tier.

### Cash Donation Restriction

No deduction is allowable for any donation made in cash exceeding **Rs 2,000**. This restriction was introduced by Finance Act 2017 (reducing the earlier threshold of Rs 10,000) and is carried forward without change.

> **Practitioner tip:** The Rs 2,000 cash limit applies per donation, not per aggregate. A donation of Rs 2,000 in cash is eligible; Rs 2,001 in cash is not. Advise donors to use cheque, demand draft, or electronic modes for all donations above Rs 2,000.

### Registration Requirement

The donee institution must hold a valid registration under Section 80G (now Section 133). Under the 2025 Act, this registration regime continues as part of the consolidated trust/institution framework. The requirement for the institution to file Form 10BD (Statement of Donations) and issue Form 10BE (Donation Certificate) to each donor continues — the deduction claim must be supported by the certificate.

### No Substantive Change

The four-tier framework, the 10% qualifying limit, the cash restriction, and the registration requirements are all carried forward without modification. The consolidation is structural and presentational.

## Section 135: Scientific Research and Rural Development Donations (Old Section 80GGA)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** Any assessee whose gross total income does **not** include income from business or profession. (Assessees with business income claim research-related deductions under the business income provisions instead.)
- **What:** Donations to approved scientific research associations, universities, IITs, national laboratories, and approved programmes for rural development.

### Deduction

**100% of the amount donated** — no qualifying limit cap applies.

### Cash Donation Restriction

Same as Section 133: no deduction for cash donations exceeding **Rs 10,000** (note: the Rs 10,000 threshold applies under Section 80GGA/135, distinct from the Rs 2,000 threshold under Section 80G/133).

**No substantive change.**

---

## Section 136: Company Contributions to Political Parties (Old Section 80GGB)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** Indian companies only.
- **What:** Contributions to any political party registered under Section 29A of the Representation of the People Act, 1951, or to an electoral trust.

### Deduction

**100% of the amount contributed** — no cap.

### Electoral Bond — Struck Down

The Electoral Bond Scheme, which permitted anonymous contributions to political parties through bearer instruments purchased from authorised banks, was **struck down by the Supreme Court** in *Association for Democratic Reforms v. Union of India* (February 2024). The Court held that the scheme violated the right to information under Article 19(1)(a) of the Constitution.

Post the Supreme Court ruling, contributions to political parties must be made through identified modes — cheque, demand draft, electronic transfer, or electoral trust. The Electoral Bond route is no longer available.

> **Practitioner alert:** Any client (company or individual) inquiring about Electoral Bonds should be informed that the scheme is defunct following the Supreme Court's 2024 ruling. Contributions must now be through transparent, identified modes only.

### Cash Contribution Restriction

No deduction for contributions made in **cash** — this is an absolute bar (not merely a threshold). All contributions must be through non-cash modes.

**No substantive change** to the deduction provision itself. The practical landscape has changed due to the Electoral Bond ruling, but the statutory text of Section 136 (old 80GGB) is unchanged.

---

## Section 137: Individual/Non-Company Contributions to Political Parties (Old Section 80GGC)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** Any person other than a company (individuals, HUFs, firms, AOPs, etc.). Excludes local authorities and artificial juridical persons wholly or partly funded by the Government.
- **What:** Contributions to any registered political party or electoral trust.

### Deduction

**100% of the amount contributed** — no cap.

### Cash Contribution Restriction

No deduction for contributions made in **cash** — same absolute bar as Section 136.

### Electoral Bond Impact

Same as Section 136 — the Electoral Bond route is no longer available following the 2024 Supreme Court ruling. Non-company assessees must use cheque, demand draft, electronic transfer, or electoral trust for deductible contributions.

**No substantive change.**

---

## Section 133 vs. Sections 135-137: Quick Comparison

| Aspect | Section 133 (80G) | Section 135 (80GGA) | Section 136 (80GGB) | Section 137 (80GGC) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Charitable donations | Scientific/rural development | Political (company) | Political (non-company) |
| **Deduction rate** | 100% or 50% (tier-based) | 100% | 100% | 100% |
| **Cap** | 10% ATI (Tiers 3 & 4 only) | None | None | None |
| **Cash restriction** | > Rs 2,000 not eligible | > Rs 10,000 not eligible | Cash not eligible at all | Cash not eligible at all |
| **Who** | Any assessee | Non-business assessees | Indian companies only | Any person except company |
| **Registration needed** | Donee must be 80G-registered | Donee must be approved | Party must be registered | Party must be registered |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 80G → 133**: Four-tier donation deduction (100%/50%, with/without qualifying limit). 10% of adjusted total income cap on qualifying-limit tiers. Cash donations exceeding Rs 2,000 ineligible. No substantive change.
- **Section 80GGA → 135**: Scientific research and rural development donations. 100% deduction. Only for non-business assessees. No substantive change.
- **Section 80GGB → 136**: Company political contributions. 100% deduction. Cash bar. Electoral Bond Scheme struck down by Supreme Court (February 2024) — transparent modes only.
- **Section 80GGC → 137**: Non-company political contributions. 100% deduction. Cash bar. Same Electoral Bond impact.
- **No substantive legislative changes**: The 2025 Act carries forward all four provisions with improved presentation and contiguous numbering. The real-world change is the Electoral Bond ruling, which is a judicial development, not a statutory one.
