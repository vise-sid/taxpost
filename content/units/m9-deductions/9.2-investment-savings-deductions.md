# Unit 9.2: Investment & Savings Deductions (Sections 123-125)

## Overview

The three most commonly claimed investment deductions — the composite Section 80C basket, the NPS deduction under Section 80CCD, and the Agniveer deduction under Section 80CCH — have been renumbered to Sections 123, 124, and 125 respectively. The substance is largely carried forward, with one harmonisation worth noting: **Section 124(2) standardises the employer NPS contribution deduction at 14% of salary for all employees**, eliminating the prior distinction between Central Government employees (14%) and others (10%). The rest of the provisions, including limits, eligible instruments, and regime applicability, continue unchanged.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80C + 80CCC | **123** | Investment/savings deduction — life insurance, PPF, ELSS, NSC, tuition fees, pension fund | Merged; Schedule XV lists eligible instruments |
| 80CCD | **124** | National Pension System (NPS) — employee and employer contributions | Renumbered; employer cap harmonised at 14% |
| 80CCH | **125** | Agniveer Corpus Fund | Renumbered only |

---

## Section 123: Investment and Savings Deduction (Old Sections 80C + 80CCC)

Section 123 consolidates old Sections 80C and 80CCC into a single provision. The eligible instruments are listed in **Schedule XV** to the 2025 Act (replacing the long list within old Section 80C(2)).

### Eligible Instruments (Schedule XV)

The following categories of investments and payments qualify for deduction under Section 123:

- Life insurance premium (for self, spouse, children)
- Contribution to Public Provident Fund (PPF)
- Contribution to recognised provident fund (employee's own contribution)
- Investment in National Savings Certificates (NSC)
- Five-year fixed deposit with a scheduled bank or post office
- Investment in Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) — 3-year lock-in
- Tuition fees for up to two children (full-time education in India)
- Contribution to approved superannuation fund
- Contribution to pension fund under Section 80CCC (now merged)
- Repayment of housing loan principal
- Stamp duty and registration charges for house purchase
- Investment in Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana
- Investment in Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS)
- NABARD Rural Bonds

### Overall Limit

| Parameter | Position |
|---|---|
| Maximum deduction | **Rs 1,50,000** per tax year |
| Combined limit | Sections 123 + 124(1) (employee NPS) share this Rs 1,50,000 ceiling |
| Lock-in periods | Instrument-specific (PPF 15 years, ELSS 3 years, FD 5 years, etc.) |
| Premature withdrawal | If an investment is withdrawn before the lock-in period, the deduction claimed is reversed and added back to income in the year of withdrawal |

### Regime Availability

Section 123 is available **only under the old tax regime**. Assessees under the new (default) regime cannot claim this deduction.

> **No substantive change.** The merger of 80C and 80CCC is a drafting exercise. The Rs 1,50,000 limit, eligible instruments, and old-regime-only restriction all continue from the pre-existing position.

## Section 124: National Pension System (Old Section 80CCD)

Section 124 deals with contributions to the National Pension System (NPS) and carries the most notable delta in this group.

### Section 124(1): Employee's Own Contribution

- Deduction for the assessee's own contribution to NPS
- Limited to **10% of salary** (for employees) or **20% of gross total income** (for non-employees / self-employed)
- Falls within the aggregate Rs 1,50,000 ceiling shared with Section 123
- Available **only under the old regime** (as part of the combined ceiling)

### Section 124(1B): Additional Contribution — Rs 50,000

- Old Section 80CCD(1B) allowed an **additional deduction of Rs 50,000** for the assessee's own NPS contribution, over and above the Rs 1,50,000 limit
- This continues under the old regime
- **Not available under the new regime**

### Section 124(2): Employer's Contribution — 14% Harmonisation

This is the substantive change in this section.

| Parameter | Old Position (80CCD(2)) | New Position (Section 124(2)) |
|---|---|---|
| Central Government employees | 14% of salary | **14% of salary** |
| State Government employees | 14% of salary | **14% of salary** |
| Other employees (private sector, PSUs) | **10% of salary** | **14% of salary** |

Under old Section 80CCD(2), the employer contribution deduction was capped at 14% of salary for Central and State Government employees but only 10% for all other employees. **Section 124(2) harmonises the cap at 14% for all employees**, removing the government vs. private sector disparity.

> **Delta: Employer NPS Cap Harmonised.** This is a genuine improvement under the 2025 Act for private-sector employees. An employee with a basic salary of Rs 10,00,000 can now claim a deduction for employer NPS contribution up to Rs 1,40,000 (instead of Rs 1,00,000 previously). The employer must actually contribute this amount — it is not a self-contribution route.

### Regime Availability for Section 124(2) — Employer Contribution

The employer NPS contribution deduction under Section 124(2) is available under **both the old and new tax regimes**. This makes it one of the few surviving deductions under the new regime, and the harmonisation at 14% enhances its value.

### Section 124: Summary Table

| Component | Limit | Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Employee's own contribution — 124(1) | 10% of salary / 20% of GTI (within Rs 1.5L) | Old only |
| Additional contribution — 124(1B) | Rs 50,000 (over and above Rs 1.5L) | Old only |
| Employer contribution — **124(2)** | **14% of salary** (for ALL employees) | **Both regimes** |

## Section 125: Agniveer Corpus Fund (Old Section 80CCH)

Section 125 provides a deduction in respect of contributions to the Agniveer Corpus Fund, introduced for individuals enrolled under the Agnipath Scheme.

### Structure

| Component | Deduction | Regime |
|---|---|---|
| Agniveer's own contribution | Deductible from total income | Both regimes |
| **Central Government's contribution** | Deductible from total income | **Both regimes** |

The fund accumulations are exempt at the time of receipt on completion of service (or on death/disability), creating an EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) regime for the Agniveer Corpus Fund.

> **No substantive change under the 2025 Act.** Section 80CCH was introduced by Finance Act 2023 and is carried forward. The availability under both regimes continues.

## Key Takeaways

| Topic | Old Position | New Position | Nature of Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80C + 80CCC | Separate sections | Merged into **Section 123** | Drafting consolidation |
| Eligible instruments list | Within Section 80C(2) | Moved to **Schedule XV** | Structural improvement |
| Section 123 limit | Rs 1,50,000 | Rs 1,50,000 | No change |
| Section 123 regime | Old regime only | Old regime only | No change |
| Employer NPS — private sector | 10% of salary | **14% of salary** | **Substantive — harmonised** |
| Employer NPS — government | 14% of salary | 14% of salary | No change |
| Employer NPS regime | Both regimes | Both regimes | No change |
| Additional NPS (old 80CCD(1B)) | Rs 50,000, old regime | Rs 50,000, old regime | No change |
| Agniveer (Section 125) | Finance Act 2023 | Carried forward | Not a 2025 Act change |
