# Unit 9.7: Other Deductions (Sections 149-154)

## Overview

The 2025 Act consolidates the remaining Chapter VI-A deductions — covering co-operative societies, author royalties, patent royalties, savings interest, and self-disability — into Sections 149 through 154. Most provisions are carried forward without substantive change. The one **genuine structural change** is the merger of Sections 80TTA (savings account interest for non-senior citizens) and 80TTB (interest income for senior citizens) into a single **Section 153**, eliminating the long-standing confusion about which section applies based on the assessee's age. All monetary limits remain unchanged. These deductions are available only under the old tax regime (except Section 126 — health insurance — which is available under both regimes).

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 80P | **149** | Deduction in respect of income of co-operative societies | Renumbered only |
| 80QQB | **151** | Deduction for royalty income of authors | Renumbered only |
| 80RRB | **152** | Deduction for royalty on patents | Renumbered only |
| 80TTA | **153** | Deduction for interest on savings account (non-senior citizens) | **Merged with 80TTB** |
| 80TTB | **153** | Deduction for interest income (senior citizens) | **Merged with 80TTA** |
| 80U | **154** | Deduction for person with disability | Renumbered only |

## Section 149: Co-operative Societies (Old Section 80P)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** A co-operative society engaged in specified activities.
- **What:** Deduction of the **whole or part** of the profits and gains attributable to specified activities.

### Activities and Deduction

| Activity | Deduction |
|---|---|
| Banking or providing credit facilities to members | **100% of profits** from such business |
| Cottage industry | **100% of profits** |
| Marketing of agricultural produce of members | **100% of profits** |
| Purchase of agricultural implements, seeds, livestock, or other articles for supply to members | **100% of profits** |
| Processing (without aid of power) of agricultural produce of members | **100% of profits** |
| Collective disposal of labour of members | **100% of profits** |
| Fishing or allied activities | **100% of profits** |
| Primary milk or oilseeds or fruits and vegetables supply society | **100% of profits** |
| Other income (interest on securities, house property income, or other sources) | **Limited deduction** — up to Rs 50,000 for consumer co-operatives; varying for others |

### Exclusion

Section 149 **does not apply** to a co-operative bank (other than a primary agricultural credit society or a primary co-operative agricultural and rural development bank). This exclusion, introduced by Finance Act 2020 under the old Act, is carried forward.

> **Practitioner tip:** Distinguish between a co-operative society carrying on banking business (eligible under Section 149) and a co-operative bank as defined under the Banking Regulation Act (ineligible). The Supreme Court's ruling in *Citizen Co-operative Society v. ACIT* (2017) established that the mere act of accepting deposits from members does not make a co-operative society a "co-operative bank."

**No substantive change.**

## Section 151: Author Royalty (Old Section 80QQB)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** An individual who is a **resident** in India and is the author or joint author of a book.
- **What:** Royalty or copyright fees received in respect of any book (other than textbooks prescribed for educational curricula).

### Deduction

**100% of income or Rs 3,00,000, whichever is lower.**

### Key Conditions

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Excluded works | **Textbooks** for schools, colleges, and universities (i.e., books prescribed or adopted for a syllabus) |
| Lump sum vs. royalty | Both lump sum payments and periodic royalty are covered |
| Foreign royalty | If royalty is earned outside India, it must be brought into India in convertible foreign exchange within **6 months** of the end of the previous year (or such extended time as the RBI allows) |
| Form | Prescribed certificate from the payer/publisher required |

> **Practitioner tip:** The textbook exclusion applies to books prescribed for any educational syllabus — a guide or reference book that is not itself prescribed as a textbook remains eligible. The distinction can be fact-intensive; verify whether the institution has formally adopted the book as part of its syllabus.

**No substantive change.**

---

## Section 152: Patent Royalty (Old Section 80RRB)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** An individual who is a **resident** in India and is the **patentee** (the true and first inventor of an invention).
- **What:** Royalty income in respect of a patent registered on or after 1 April 2003 under the Patents Act, 1970.

### Deduction

**100% of income or Rs 3,00,000, whichever is lower.**

### Key Conditions

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Must be the true and first inventor | Assignees and licensees are **not** eligible — only the original patentee |
| Patent must be registered | Under the Patents Act, 1970 |
| Foreign royalty | Must be brought into India in convertible foreign exchange within 6 months |
| Form | Prescribed certificate from the payer required |

**No substantive change.**

## Section 153: Savings and Deposit Interest — Merged Provision (Old Sections 80TTA + 80TTB)

### The Structural Change

Under the 1961 Act, interest income deductions were split across two provisions:

| Old Section | Who | Interest Covered | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **80TTA** | Individuals and HUFs (non-senior citizens) | Interest on **savings accounts** only | Rs 10,000 |
| **80TTB** | Senior citizens (age 60+) | Interest on **savings accounts, fixed deposits, and recurring deposits** | Rs 50,000 |

This bifurcation created practical confusion: assessees turning 60 mid-year had to determine which section applied, and practitioners frequently needed to verify whether the broader coverage of 80TTB (including FD/RD interest) applied or only the narrower 80TTA scope (savings account interest only).

The 2025 Act **merges both provisions into a single Section 153**, preserving the differentiated limits and scope:

### Section 153 — Unified Structure

| Category | Interest Covered | Deduction Limit |
|---|---|---|
| **General** (individuals and HUFs below age 60) | Interest on **savings accounts** with a bank, co-operative society, or post office | **Rs 10,000** |
| **Senior citizens** (individuals aged 60 or above at any time during the tax year) | Interest on **savings accounts, fixed deposits, and recurring deposits** with a bank, co-operative society, or post office | **Rs 50,000** |

### What Is and Is Not Changed

| Aspect | Changed? | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Limits | No | Rs 10,000 (general), Rs 50,000 (senior citizens) — unchanged |
| Scope of interest covered | No | Savings-only for general; savings + FD + RD for senior citizens — unchanged |
| Age threshold | No | 60 years — unchanged |
| Eligibility | No | Individuals and HUFs — unchanged |
| **Section structure** | **Yes** | Two sections merged into one — single provision with age-based differentiation |

### Why It Matters

The merger is a **simplification** that:

1. Eliminates the need to determine which of two sections applies — a single section reference suffices.
2. Reduces return-filing errors where assessees (or software) incorrectly claimed 80TTB limits while filing under 80TTA, or vice versa.
3. Aligns with the 2025 Act's consolidation philosophy of reducing the total section count.

> **Practitioner tip:** When advising senior citizen clients, the key point is unchanged — they receive a Rs 50,000 deduction on interest from savings accounts, fixed deposits, and recurring deposits combined. The section number changes from 80TTB to 153, and the same provision now also houses the Rs 10,000 deduction for non-senior citizens (old 80TTA).

## Section 154: Self-Disability (Old Section 80U)

### Eligibility

- **Who:** A **resident individual** who is certified as a person with disability.
- **What:** A flat deduction (not linked to any expenditure) for the assessee's own disability.

### Deduction

| Category | Deduction Amount |
|---|---|
| Person with disability (40% or more, but less than 80%) | **Rs 75,000** |
| Person with severe disability (80% or more) | **Rs 1,25,000** |

### Key Conditions

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Certificate | Must be issued by a medical authority (a Government hospital) as prescribed |
| Validity | Certificate must be valid for the relevant assessment year; periodic renewal may be required depending on the nature of disability |
| Self only | The deduction is for the assessee's own disability — for expenditure on a disabled dependent, the separate provision (Section 125, old Section 80DD) applies |

### Disability Definitions

Disability includes blindness, low vision, leprosy-cured, locomotor disability, mental illness, mental retardation (intellectual disability), autism, cerebral palsy, and hearing impairment — as defined under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016.

**No substantive change.**

## Tax Regime Applicability

A critical point for all deductions covered in this unit:

| Deduction | Old Regime (Default for non-salaried) | New Regime (Section 115BAC / Section 192 of 2025 Act) |
|---|---|---|
| Section 149 (co-operative societies) | Available | **Not available** |
| Section 151 (author royalty) | Available | **Not available** |
| Section 152 (patent royalty) | Available | **Not available** |
| Section 153 (savings/deposit interest) | Available | **Not available** |
| Section 154 (self-disability) | Available | **Not available** |

> **Practitioner alert:** Section 126 (health insurance, old Section 80D) is the notable exception — it is available under **both** the old and new tax regimes. The deductions in this unit (Sections 149-154) are available only under the old regime. Clients who have opted for the new regime cannot claim any of these deductions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 80P → 149, 80QQB → 151, 80RRB → 152, 80TTA+80TTB → 153, 80U → 154.** Five old sections consolidated into five new sections.
- **Structural change — Section 153**: Sections 80TTA (savings interest, Rs 10,000, non-senior citizens) and 80TTB (savings + FD + RD interest, Rs 50,000, senior citizens) are **merged** into a single Section 153 with age-based differentiation. This is a genuine simplification, not merely a renumbering.
- **All limits unchanged**: Rs 3,00,000 (author/patent royalty), Rs 10,000/50,000 (savings interest), Rs 75,000/1,25,000 (self-disability).
- **Old regime only**: All deductions in this unit are available only under the old tax regime (except Section 126 — health insurance — which is available under both).
- **Co-operative banks excluded** from Section 149 (as per Finance Act 2020 amendment, carried forward).
