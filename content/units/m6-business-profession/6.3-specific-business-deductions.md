# Unit 6.3: Specific Business Deductions (Sections 44-52)

## Overview

The old Act scattered special deductions across Sections 33AB through 44A, with scientific research in 35, specified business in 35AD, preliminary expenses in 35D, and telecom amortization in 35ABA/35ABB. The 2025 Act collects all of these into a clean Sections 44-52 block. The single biggest substantive change: weighted deduction for scientific research is gone — it is 100% only, no more 150%/200%. Everything else is renumbering and reorganization.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 35(1)/(2)/(2AA)/(2AB) | **45** | Scientific research expenditure | Renumbered + **substantive change** |
| 35AD | **46** | Deduction for specified business | Renumbered only |
| 35D | **44** | Amortization of preliminary expenses | Renumbered only |
| 35CCC + 35CCD | **47** | Expenditure on agricultural extension project / skill development project | Merged |
| 33AB | **48** | Tea/coffee/rubber development account | Renumbered only |
| 33ABA | **49** | Site restoration fund (petroleum/natural gas) | Renumbered only |
| 44A | **50** | Deductions for companies carrying on special business | Renumbered only |
| 35E | **51** | Expenditure on prospecting for minerals | Renumbered only |
| 35ABA + 35ABB + 35DD + 35DDA | **52** | Expenditure on telecom licence / spectrum / reorganization of business | Merged |

---

## Section 45: Scientific Research (Old Section 35) — Weighted Deduction Removed

### What Changed

This is the only substantive change in this unit, and it was already in effect through Finance Act amendments — the 2025 Act simply codifies the final position.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 35) | New Act (Section 45) |
|---|---|---|
| In-house research expenditure (revenue) | 100% deduction | 100% deduction — same |
| In-house research expenditure (capital) | 100% deduction | 100% deduction — same |
| Payment to approved research association/university/college/institution | Was 150%/200% (weighted), reduced to 100% by Finance Act 2020 | **100% only** — codified |
| Payment to National Laboratory/university/IIT for approved research | Was 150%/200% (weighted), reduced to 100% | **100% only** — codified |
| Payment to a company for scientific research | 100% | 100% — same |

**The weighted deduction regime (150%/200%) for payments to research bodies is permanently gone.** The Finance Act 2020 had already sunset the weighted deduction effective from AY 2021-22. The 2025 Act carries forward only the 100% deduction — no provision for weighted deduction exists in the new law.

### What Stayed the Same

- In-house R&D expenditure (both revenue and capital): 100% deduction — unchanged
- Approval mechanism for research associations and institutions: retained
- Conditions for claiming the deduction (expenditure must be on scientific research related to the assessee's business): retained

### Practical Impact

If your clients were still hoping for a revival of the weighted deduction (some were), the 2025 Act closes that door. All R&D deductions are at 100%, period. Update computation sheets to reference Section 45 and ensure no legacy templates still show 150% or 200% rates.

## Section 46: Specified Business (Old Section 35AD)

### What Stayed the Same — Everything

The deduction for specified business is carried forward intact:

- **100% deduction** of capital expenditure in the year it is incurred (not depreciation — full deduction)
- **14 categories** of specified business (cold chain facility, warehousing for agricultural produce, affordable housing, hospital with 100+ beds, fertilizer production, etc.)
- **8-year lock-in**: If the asset is transferred within 8 years, the deduction is clawed back (amount treated as PGBP income in the year of transfer)
- **Cannot be set off** against income from any other business

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 35AD) | New Act (Section 46) |
|---|---|---|
| Deduction rate | 100% of capital expenditure | 100% — same |
| Number of specified business categories | 14 | 14 — same |
| Lock-in period | 8 years | 8 years — same |
| Set-off restriction | Only against specified business income | Same |

**Bottom line: Section number changed (35AD → 46). Everything else — categories, rates, lock-in, set-off rules — is identical. Update references only.**

---

## Section 44: Preliminary Expenses (Old Section 35D)

Amortization of preliminary expenses over 5 years — retained without change. Covers expenditure on:
- Feasibility reports, project reports, market surveys
- Legal and other charges for formation of a company
- Preparation of reports as required by any law

Cap: 5% of cost of the project (or capital employed, for existing companies). Amortized in 5 equal annual installments beginning from the year the business commences.

**Renumbered only. No substantive change.**

---

## Section 47: Agricultural Extension & Skill Development (Old Sections 35CCC + 35CCD — Merged)

Two standalone sections — 35CCC (agricultural extension project) and 35CCD (skill development project) — merged into one section. Both provided 100% deduction of expenditure on notified projects. The merger is organizational; no change to the deduction amount or conditions.

**Bottom line: Two sections became one. Same 100% deduction for both project types.**

---

## Section 48: Tea/Coffee/Rubber Development Account (Old Section 33AB)

Deposit into a special account with NABARD for tea, coffee, or rubber development. Deduction limited to the amount deposited or 40% of profits (whichever is lower). Amount must be used for purposes specified in the approved scheme.

**Renumbered only. No substantive change.**

---

## Section 49: Site Restoration Fund (Old Section 33ABA)

Deposit into a site restoration fund for petroleum and natural gas operations. Deduction limited to 20% of profits. Must be used for site restoration as per approved scheme.

**Renumbered only. No substantive change.**

---

## Section 50: Special Business Deductions for Companies (Old Section 44A)

Deductions available to companies carrying on special business (where income was computed under specific rules before the 2025 Act). Carried forward without change.

**Renumbered only.**

---

## Section 51: Mineral Prospecting (Old Section 35E)

Expenditure on prospecting, extraction, or production of specified minerals. Amortized over 10 years. The provision was already limited in applicability (primarily mining companies). Carried forward intact.

**Renumbered only. No substantive change.**

---

## Section 52: Telecom and Business Reorganization Amortization (Old Sections 35ABA + 35ABB + 35DD + 35DDA — Merged)

Four sections collapsed into one:

| Old Section | Subject | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 35ABA | Expenditure on telecom licence | Amortized over licence period |
| 35ABB | Expenditure on spectrum purchase | Amortized over spectrum assignment period |
| 35DD | Expenditure on amalgamation/demerger | Amortized over 5 years |
| 35DDA | Expenditure on VRS (voluntary retirement scheme) | Amortized over 5 years |

The 2025 Act consolidates all four into Section 52. The amortization periods, conditions, and computation methods are retained without substantive change.

**Bottom line: Four sections became one. Update cross-references. No change to any amortization period or amount.**

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Scientific research (35 → 45)**: Weighted deduction is gone permanently. 100% only — for in-house and payments to approved institutions alike.
- **Specified business (35AD → 46)**: 100% capital expenditure deduction, 14 categories, 8-year lock-in — all unchanged. Renumbered only.
- **Preliminary expenses (35D → 44)**: 5-year amortization, 5% cap — unchanged.
- **Agricultural extension + skill development (35CCC + 35CCD → 47)**: Merged. Same 100% deduction.
- **Tea/coffee/rubber (33AB → 48)**, **site restoration (33ABA → 49)**, **special business (44A → 50)**, **mineral prospecting (35E → 51)**: All renumbered only.
- **Telecom/reorganization (35ABA + 35ABB + 35DD + 35DDA → 52)**: Four sections merged into one. Same amortization rules.
- **Overall**: One substantive change (weighted deduction removal, already effective since AY 2021-22). Everything else is reorganization. Update section references across the board.
