# Unit 6.1: Profits & Gains Overview (Sections 26-27)

## Overview

The PGBP charging and computation framework migrated with minimal substantive change — old Sections 28 and 29 become Sections 26 and 27, the same 11 categories of PGBP income are retained, and the computation method still starts with profits and subtracts allowable deductions. If you know the old structure, this is a section-number update and nothing more. The real action in Module 6 starts from Unit 6.2 onward.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 28 | **26** | Charging section — income chargeable under PGBP | Renumbered only |
| 29 | **27** | Computation of income — apply deductions under Sections 28-52 | Renumbered only |
| 145 | **276** | Method of accounting | Renumbered only |

**Bottom line: Three sections, all renumbered, zero substantive changes.**

---

## Section 26: Charging Section (Old Section 28)

### The 11 Categories — Unchanged

The charging section lists 11 categories of income taxable under PGBP. Every one of them carries over from old Section 28 without addition, removal, or modification:

1. Profits and gains of any business or profession
2. Compensation for management of an Indian company (or agency modification/termination)
3. Income from trade/professional/similar associations from specific services performed for members
4. Export incentives (profit on sale of import licences, cash assistance, duty drawback)
5. Benefits/perquisites arising from business or profession
6. Interest, salary, bonus, commission, or remuneration from a partnership firm
7. Any sum received under a Keyman insurance policy
8. Income from speculative transactions (deemed a distinct business)
9. Fair market value of inventory converted to capital asset
10. Sum received for not carrying out any activity or not sharing any know-how
11. Any sum received on demolition/destruction of a depreciable asset

### What Stayed the Same

- **Speculative transactions** continue to be treated as a distinct business — losses from speculation can only be set off against speculative profits (this is in the loss set-off provisions, but the foundation sits here in the charging section).
- **No new categories** were added. Nothing was removed.
- **Residential rental income** remains excluded from PGBP — it stays under Income from House Property (Sections 20-25), regardless of the scale of rental activity.

### Practical Impact

Update section references from "Section 28" to "Section 26" in computation statements, assessment replies, and advisory opinions. No substantive change to memorize.

---

## Section 27: Computation of Income (Old Section 29)

Old Section 29 stated that income under PGBP is computed after allowing deductions under Sections 30 to 43D. New Section 27 states the same — compute income after deductions under **Sections 28 to 52**.

The deduction section range expanded (28-52 vs. 30-43D) because the 2025 Act reorganized and renumbered the deduction provisions. The computation principle itself is identical.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 29) | New Act (Section 27) |
|---|---|---|
| Computation method | Profits minus deductions | Profits minus deductions |
| Deduction range | Sections 30-43D | Sections 28-52 |
| Substantive change | — | **None** |

---

## Section 276: Method of Accounting (Old Section 145)

Old Section 145 governed the method of accounting — mercantile or cash. New Section 276 carries this forward without substantive change.

- Assessee must follow either the cash or mercantile system **consistently**.
- The Central Government may notify income computation and disclosure standards (ICDS) — this power is retained.
- If the Assessing Officer is not satisfied with the correctness or completeness of accounts, or if the prescribed method has not been regularly followed, best judgment assessment applies — same as before.

**The section number jumped significantly** (145 → 276) because accounting provisions were moved to a later chapter in the 2025 Act's reorganized structure. Substance is unchanged.

> **Watch out:** The ICDS notifications issued under old Section 145(2) will need to be re-notified or mapped under Section 276. Until CBDT issues a clarification or fresh notifications, monitor whether existing ICDS continue to apply by reference to the saving clauses in the transition provisions.

---

## What You Don't Need to Relearn

This unit exists primarily to give you the mapping. The entire PGBP charging and computation framework is a language modernization exercise:

- Same 11 income categories
- Same computation method (profits minus deductions)
- Same method of accounting rules
- Same treatment of speculative transactions as a distinct business
- Same exclusion of residential rental income

The deductions themselves — where the real changes happened — are covered in Units 6.2, 6.3, and 6.4.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 28 → Section 26**: Charging section with 11 categories of PGBP income. No additions, no removals.
- **Section 29 → Section 27**: Computation = profits minus deductions (now Sections 28-52). Same principle.
- **Section 145 → Section 276**: Method of accounting (cash/mercantile). Renumbered only. Watch for ICDS re-notification.
- **Speculative transactions** remain a distinct business. Residential rental remains excluded from PGBP.
- **This is the easiest unit in Module 6.** The substantive changes start in Unit 6.2 (deductions) and peak in Unit 6.4 (presumptive taxation).
