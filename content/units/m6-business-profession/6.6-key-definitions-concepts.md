# Unit 6.6: Key Definitions & Concepts (Sections 39-43)

## Overview

The definitional backbone of the business/profession chapter — actual cost, written-down value, forex treatment, stamp duty deeming, financial institution income recognition, and construction contract accounting — has been migrated from a cluster of Section 43 sub-sections and standalone provisions (41, 43A, 43AA, 43C, 43CA, 43CB, 43D) into a cleaner sequence of Sections 39 through 43, supplemented by Sections 38, 53, 56, and 57. The substance is almost entirely unchanged. No thresholds have been altered, no formulas have been modified, and no new concepts have been introduced. This is a reorganisation exercise: definitions that were previously crammed into sub-sections of Section 43 now sit in their own dedicated sections, and the deemed profit recovery provision of old Section 41 has been repositioned as Section 38. Practitioners who understand these concepts under the 1961 Act need only update their section references.

| Old Section / Sub-Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |

| 43C — Cost on amalgamation/gift | **40** | Cost of depreciable asset acquired on amalgamation, succession, or gift | Renumbered only |
| 43 — WDV definition | **41** | Written-down value | Renumbered + formula codified |
| 43A — Forex capitalisation | **42** | Adjustment to actual cost for exchange rate fluctuations | Renumbered only |

| 41 — Deemed profits | **38** | Recovery of losses/deductions previously allowed | Renumbered only |

### Structural Observation

Under the 1961 Act, Section 43 was an omnibus provision — its sub-sections and explanations defined actual cost (43(1)), WDV (43(6)), and housed forex capitalisation (43A), forex taxation (43AA), amalgamation cost rules (43C), stamp duty deeming (43CA), construction contracts (43CB), and financial institution income (43D). The 2025 Act breaks this apart: each concept gets its own section number. The result is more navigable, but it means that references that previously pointed to "Section 43(1)" or "Explanation 1 to Section 43(1)" must now be updated to "Section 39."

---

## Section 39: Actual Cost (Old Section 43(1))

**Scope:** Defines the "actual cost" of an asset acquired by the assessee for the purposes of depreciation and other capital allowance provisions.

**Key features:**

- **Cash payment restriction:** Any payment exceeding Rs 10,000 in a single day made otherwise than by account payee cheque, account payee bank draft, or electronic clearing system is excluded from actual cost. This replicates the old disallowance under Section 43(1) read with Rule 6DD. The threshold of Rs 10,000 per day is unchanged.

- **13 special circumstances:** Section 39 enumerates 13 circumstances under which the actual cost is determined differently from the price paid. These cover situations such as:
  - Assets acquired by way of gift or inheritance
  - Assets acquired before the previous year
  - Assets acquired from a related party at above-market value
  - Assets acquired on amalgamation, demerger, or succession
  - Assets where a portion of cost is met by government subsidy or grant
  - Assets previously used for non-business purposes
  - Assets acquired under hire-purchase or instalment
  - Conversion of personal asset to business asset (actual cost = fair market value on date of conversion)

These 13 circumstances correspond to the Explanations that were appended to old Section 43(1). The 2025 Act converts them into structured sub-sections rather than explanations and provisos, improving readability without altering the rules.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 43(1)) | New Act (Section 39) |
|---|---|---|
| Cash payment cap | Rs 10,000/day | Rs 10,000/day — unchanged |
| Special circumstances | Explanations 1-13 to Section 43(1) | Sub-sections within Section 39 — restructured, not changed |
| Subsidy/grant treatment | Deducted from actual cost | Deducted from actual cost — unchanged |
| Gift/inheritance | FMV / WDV of previous owner | Same — unchanged |

## Section 40: Cost on Amalgamation / Gift of Depreciable Asset (Old Section 43C)

**Scope:** Determines the cost of a depreciable asset acquired by way of amalgamation, succession, or gift for the purpose of computing depreciation in the hands of the successor/donee.

The rule is straightforward: the actual cost to the successor is the WDV of the asset in the hands of the predecessor as on the date of transfer. This ensures continuity of depreciation — the successor does not get a stepped-up cost base merely by reason of the amalgamation or gift.

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 43C to Section 40.

---

## Section 41: Written-Down Value (Old Section 43(6) / WDV Definition)

**Scope:** Defines the WDV of a block of assets for the purpose of computing depreciation under Section 46.

The formula, now codified explicitly:

**WDV = [(A - D) + B - C] - E**

Where:
- **A** = WDV of the block at the beginning of the previous year
- **B** = Actual cost of assets acquired during the year and added to the block
- **C** = Moneys payable in respect of assets sold, discarded, demolished, or destroyed during the year (plus insurance/compensation received)
- **D** = Depreciation actually allowed in respect of the block
- **E** = Adjustments on account of corporate transfers (amalgamation, demerger, succession)

**Corporate transfer rules:** Where an asset is transferred by way of amalgamation, demerger, or succession of a firm/sole proprietorship to a company, the WDV is computed as if the transfer had not taken place — i.e., the transferor's block continues in the transferee's hands without reset. This replicates the old treatment and ensures depreciation continuity across reorganisations.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 43) | New Act (Section 41) |
|---|---|---|
| Formula | Implicit across sub-sections and explanations | Explicitly codified as [(A-D)+B-C]-E |
| Corporate transfer continuity | Present but spread across provisos | Consolidated into Section 41 |
| Block concept | Retained | Retained — unchanged |

**Practical note:** The formula itself produces identical results. The codification into a single explicit expression is a drafting improvement — useful for teaching and reference, but it does not alter any computation.

## Section 42: Forex Capitalisation (Old Section 43A)

**Scope:** Where an asset is acquired from outside India, and the cost of that asset is subject to a change in exchange rate between the date of acquisition and the date of payment (or the last day of the previous year, whichever is earlier), the actual cost of the asset is adjusted by the forex difference.

This provision ensures that exchange rate fluctuations on capital account items (assets acquired in foreign currency) are capitalised into the cost of the asset rather than being treated as revenue income or expenditure.

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 43A to Section 42.

---

## Section 43: Forex Taxation (Old Section 43AA)

**Scope:** Deals with the taxation of gains and losses arising from foreign exchange fluctuations on monetary items (receivables, payables, loans) — as distinct from capital items covered by Section 42.

Foreign exchange differences on monetary items are recognised as income or expenditure on the basis of the accounting standards (Ind AS or old AS) followed by the assessee. This provision was introduced by the Finance Act 2018 and remains unchanged in substance.

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 43AA to Section 43.

---

## Section 53: Stamp Duty Value for Stock-in-Trade (Old Section 43CA)

**Scope:** Where immovable property is transferred as stock-in-trade for a consideration less than the stamp duty value (SDV) of the property, the SDV is deemed to be the full value of consideration for the purpose of computing PGBP income.

**Tolerance threshold:** The deeming provision does not apply if the actual consideration is at least **110% of the stamp duty value** — i.e., a 10% tolerance band is available. If the consideration received is within 10% of the SDV, the actual consideration is accepted. This 10% tolerance replicates the position under the 1961 Act (introduced by Finance Act 2020 for Section 43CA).

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 43CA) | New Act (Section 53) |
|---|---|---|
| Deeming trigger | Consideration < SDV | Consideration < SDV — unchanged |
| Tolerance | 110% threshold | 110% threshold — unchanged |
| Applicable to | Stock-in-trade (real estate developers) | Stock-in-trade — unchanged |

**Practical note:** This provision primarily affects real estate developers who hold property as stock-in-trade. The parallel provision for capital asset transfers exists separately under capital gains. The 110% tolerance means that a sale at Rs 55 lakh against an SDV of Rs 50 lakh (i.e., 110% of SDV) will not trigger the deeming provision.

---

## Section 56: Financial Institution Income (Old Section 43D)

**Scope:** Provides a special income recognition rule for scheduled banks, public financial institutions, and specified entities — interest income on non-performing assets (NPAs) is recognised only on actual receipt, not on accrual basis.

This carve-out from the accrual principle prevents banks from paying tax on interest income that may never be collected. The categories of eligible institutions and the conditions remain unchanged.

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 43D to Section 56.

---

## Section 57: Construction Contracts (Old Section 43CB)

**Scope:** Mandates percentage-of-completion method (POCM) for recognising income from construction contracts and service contracts that extend beyond one financial year. This aligns tax recognition with the accounting standard treatment (Ind AS 115 / old AS 7).

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 43CB to Section 57.

---

## Section 38: Deemed Profits (Old Section 41)

**Scope:** Where a deduction or allowance has been granted in an earlier year, and the assessee subsequently:
- Recovers the amount (e.g., bad debt recovered after write-off), or
- Obtains a benefit in respect of the deduction (e.g., balancing charge on sale of depreciable asset above WDV),

the recovery or benefit is deemed to be income of the year in which it is received or obtained, chargeable under the head "Profits and gains of business or profession."

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 41 to Section 38. The deemed profit mechanism, the conditions for chargeability, and the treatment of successor businesses all remain identical.

## Practical Impact

| Area | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Section references** | This is the primary action item. Update all references: 43(1) → 39, 43C → 40, 43(6)/WDV → 41, 43A → 42, 43AA → 43, 43CA → 53, 43D → 56, 43CB → 57, 41 → 38. |
| **Depreciation computations** | The WDV formula is now explicitly codified in Section 41. No change in computation — the formula [(A-D)+B-C]-E produces the same result as before. |
| **Actual cost determinations** | The 13 special circumstances are now sub-sections of Section 39 instead of Explanations to Section 43(1). Same rules, better structure. |
| **Real estate developers** | Section 53 (old 43CA) — 110% SDV tolerance threshold continues. No change. |
| **Banks / financial institutions** | Section 56 (old 43D) — NPA interest recognition on receipt basis continues. No change. |
| **Construction contractors** | Section 57 (old 43CB) — POCM continues. No change. |
| **Amalgamation / demerger** | Section 40 (old 43C) and Section 41 (corporate transfer rules) — depreciation continuity on reorganisation continues without change. |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Reorganisation, not reform.** Every definition and concept in this unit has been renumbered and restructured, but none has been substantively amended. The rules, thresholds, formulas, and conditions are identical to the 1961 Act.
- **Section 43 unbundled.** The omnibus Section 43 has been broken into dedicated sections (39-43), each addressing a single concept. This is a significant navigational improvement.
- **WDV formula codified.** The formula [(A-D)+B-C]-E is now explicitly stated in Section 41 rather than being inferred from sub-sections and explanations.
- **Rs 10,000/day cash cap** for actual cost determination — unchanged (Section 39).
- **110% SDV tolerance** for stock-in-trade property transfers — unchanged (Section 53).
- **Deemed profit recovery** (old Section 41) is now Section 38 — same mechanism, new number.
- **No new concepts introduced.** The practitioner's task is limited to updating section references across all working papers, opinions, and computation templates.
