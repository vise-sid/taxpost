# Unit 6.7: Maintenance & Audit (Sections 62-63)

## Overview

The tax audit framework under the 2025 Act retains the same thresholds and obligations that practitioners have operated under for years, but overhauls the reporting infrastructure entirely. Sections 62 and 63 carry forward old Sections 44AA and 44AB with no change in substance — the same businesses must maintain books, the same turnover thresholds trigger audit, and the same deadlines apply. The real change is procedural: the three existing audit report forms (3CA, 3CB, and 3CD) are replaced by a single unified **Form 26**, structured into four parts (A through D) with built-in digital infrastructure disclosures, simplified depreciation reporting, and a categorised audit observation framework. This is the most significant change to tax audit reporting since Form 3CD was last overhauled. Rule 47 of the Income Tax Rules 2026 governs the new form.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section / Form (1961 Act) | New Section / Form (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 44AA | **62** | Books of account — maintenance obligation | Renumbered only |
| 44AB | **63** | Tax audit — thresholds and obligations | Renumbered only |
| Form 3CA | **Form 26 (Parts A-D)** | Audit report for persons whose accounts are audited under another law | Replaced |
| Form 3CB | **Form 26 (Parts A-D)** | Audit report for other persons | Replaced |
| Form 3CD | **Form 26 (Parts A-D)** | Statement of particulars | Replaced |
| Rule 6G of IT Rules 1962 | **Rule 47 of IT Rules 2026** | Prescribed form and particulars for tax audit report | Replaced |

### Structural Observation

Under the 1961 Act, tax audit reporting required two parallel tracks: Form 3CA + 3CD for assessees whose accounts were already audited under another statute (e.g., the Companies Act), and Form 3CB + 3CD for all others. Form 3CD — the statement of particulars — was common to both tracks but had grown unwieldy over successive amendments, with 44 clauses covering everything from depreciation schedules to GST reconciliations to land transaction details. The 2025 Act collapses all three forms into a single Form 26 with a structured four-part architecture, eliminating the dual-track system and modernising the disclosure framework.

## Section 62: Books of Account (Old Section 44AA)

**Scope:** Prescribes the obligation to maintain books of account for persons carrying on business or profession.

**Key provisions — all unchanged:**

- Every person carrying on a **profession** listed in Section 62 (legal, medical, engineering, accountancy, technical consultancy, interior decoration, and other notified professions) must maintain books of account and other documents as may enable the Assessing Officer to compute total income.
- For persons carrying on **business**, books of account must be maintained if:
  - Income from business exceeds Rs 2,50,000, or
  - Total sales / turnover / gross receipts exceed Rs 25,00,000 in any of the three preceding years
- The books must be maintained at the **principal place of business** and kept for a period of **6 years** from the end of the relevant assessment year (8 years where assessment is reopened).

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 44AA) | New Act (Section 62) |
|---|---|---|
| Profession threshold | All notified professions | Same — unchanged |
| Business income threshold | Rs 2,50,000 | Rs 2,50,000 — unchanged |
| Business turnover threshold | Rs 25,00,000 | Rs 25,00,000 — unchanged |
| Retention period | 6 years (8 years if reopened) | 6 years (8 years if reopened) — unchanged |

**No substantive change.** Renumbered from Section 44AA to Section 62.

## Section 63: Tax Audit (Old Section 44AB)

**Scope:** Requires every person carrying on business or profession to get accounts audited if turnover/receipts exceed the prescribed thresholds, and to furnish the audit report by the prescribed date.

### Thresholds — Unchanged

| Category | Threshold | Condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Business** (general) | **Rs 1 crore** | Total sales / turnover / gross receipts exceed Rs 1 crore |
| **Business** (digital transactions) | **Rs 10 crore** | Aggregate cash receipts ≤ 5% of total receipts AND aggregate cash payments ≤ 5% of total payments — the Rs 10 crore enhanced threshold applies |
| **Profession** | **Rs 50 lakh** | Gross receipts exceed Rs 50 lakh |
| **Presumptive taxation opt-out** | As applicable | Persons who opt for presumptive taxation under Sections 58 (old 44AD/44ADA) but declare income below the presumptive rate |

The Rs 1 crore / Rs 10 crore / Rs 50 lakh thresholds are carried forward without any change. The digital transaction condition for the enhanced Rs 10 crore limit (cash receipts and payments each not exceeding 5% of total) is also unchanged.

### Filing Deadline

The tax audit report must be furnished **one month before the due date for filing the return of income:**

| Assessee Category | ITR Due Date | Tax Audit Report Due Date |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate assessees / persons requiring TP report | 30 November | **31 October** |
| Other assessees requiring audit | 31 October | **30 September** |

### Audit Report: Form 26 — The New Unified Form

Form 26 replaces Forms 3CA, 3CB, and 3CD. It is structured into four parts:

#### Part A: General Information

- Assessee details: name, PAN, address, nature of business, constitution (individual/firm/company/LLP/AOP)
- Accounting year, audit period, and statutory audit status
- Whether accounts are audited under any other law (this replaces the old 3CA/3CB bifurcation — the auditor indicates the status within Part A, and the same form continues)

#### Part B: Financial Statements & Quantitative Details

- Profit and loss account summary (turnover, gross profit, net profit, key expense heads)
- Balance sheet summary (assets, liabilities, capital/reserves)
- Quantitative details of principal items of goods traded or manufactured
- **Simplified depreciation schedule** — the depreciation table within Form 26 is rationalised compared to old Form 3CD Clause 18; block-wise reporting continues but the format is streamlined to align with the codified WDV formula in Section 41

#### Part C: Tax-Specific Disclosures

This part covers the substantive compliance disclosures that were previously spread across Clauses 10-44 of Form 3CD. Key features:

- **Structured Yes/No triggers:** Instead of requiring narrative answers for every clause, Form 26 uses a trigger-based approach. The auditor first indicates whether a particular provision is applicable (Yes/No). Detailed particulars are required only where the answer is "Yes." This reduces reporting burden for clauses that do not apply to a particular assessee.

- **Digital infrastructure disclosure (new):** Assessees must disclose:
  - Accounting software used (name, version, whether source code is available)
  - Cloud computing services used for storing books of account
  - ERP systems deployed
  - IP addresses of servers where books are maintained (if maintained electronically)
  - Whether digital audit trail / edit log is maintained in accounting software

  This is a substantively new disclosure requirement with no equivalent in old Form 3CD. It reflects the growing regulatory focus on digital record-keeping and audit trail requirements introduced by the Companies (Accounts) Rules and extended to tax audit.

- **Rationalised GST reporting:** The GST reconciliation requirements (old Clause 44 of Form 3CD, which was notified but repeatedly deferred) are now integrated into Form 26 in a simplified format. The reconciliation of GST turnover with income reported in financial statements is retained, but the format is more structured and less onerous than the deferred Clause 44.

- **Land/immovable property transactions:** Reporting of transactions where stamp duty value exceeds consideration — aligned with the 110% tolerance threshold in Section 53 (old 43CA) and corresponding capital gains provisions.

#### Part D: Audit Observations

- **Categorised observations:** Audit observations are now classified into predefined categories:
  - Qualifications
  - Emphasis of matter
  - Adverse remarks
  - Compliance deviations
  - Other observations
- This replaces the free-text audit observation in old Forms 3CA/3CB, providing a structured framework that enables easier regulatory review and data analytics by the Department.

### UDIN and FRN Requirements

- **UDIN (Unique Document Identification Number):** Mandatory for all tax audit reports filed under Section 63. The UDIN must be generated through the ICAI portal before filing. This continues the existing requirement.
- **FRN (Firm Registration Number):** The audit firm's FRN must be quoted in Form 26. Individual practitioners must quote their membership number.

### Rule 47 of Income Tax Rules 2026

Rule 47 prescribes:
- The form of the audit report (Form 26)
- The particulars to be furnished in each part
- The manner of electronic filing
- The digital signature requirements (DSC of the signing partner/proprietor of the audit firm)

Rule 47 replaces old Rule 6G of the Income Tax Rules 1962.

## Old Form 3CD vs New Form 26 — Key Differences

| Feature | Old (Forms 3CA/3CB + 3CD) | New (Form 26) |
|---|---|---|
| Number of forms | 3 (3CA or 3CB + 3CD) | **1 unified form** |
| Structure | Free-form clauses (44 clauses in 3CD) | **4 structured parts (A-D)** |
| Applicability trigger | Separate forms for statutory audit vs non-statutory | **Single form with Yes/No indicator** in Part A |
| Clause format | Narrative answers for all clauses | **Yes/No trigger** — details only if applicable |
| Digital infrastructure | Not disclosed | **Mandatory disclosure** — software, cloud, ERP, IP, audit trail |
| Depreciation schedule | Detailed (Clause 18) | **Simplified** — aligned with Section 41 formula |
| GST reconciliation | Clause 44 — notified but repeatedly deferred | **Integrated and simplified** |
| Audit observations | Free-text | **Categorised** — qualifications, emphasis, adverse, deviations |
| Governing rule | Rule 6G (IT Rules 1962) | **Rule 47 (IT Rules 2026)** |

## Practical Impact

| Area | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Section references** | Replace 44AA with Section 62 and 44AB with Section 63 in all engagement letters, audit checklists, and client communications. |
| **Audit report form** | Transition from Forms 3CA/3CB/3CD to **Form 26**. Update all audit templates, working paper formats, and review checklists. |
| **Digital infrastructure disclosure** | Collect information from clients on accounting software (name, version), cloud services, ERP systems, and server IP addresses. This is a new data point — build it into your audit information request letter. |
| **Audit trail / edit log** | Verify whether client's accounting software maintains a digital audit trail. This will be a disclosure item in Form 26 Part C. |
| **Thresholds** | No change — Rs 1 crore / Rs 10 crore / Rs 50 lakh. No action required on threshold assessment. |
| **Filing deadline** | No change — 30 September / 31 October (one month before ITR due date). |
| **UDIN** | Continue generating UDIN for all tax audit reports. No change in process. |
| **GST reconciliation** | Prepare for the integrated GST reconciliation in Form 26. Unlike old Clause 44 which was perpetually deferred, the Form 26 version is expected to be operative from the first year. |
| **Audit observations** | Adapt observation drafting to the new categorisation framework (qualifications, emphasis of matter, adverse remarks, compliance deviations, other). |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Sections 62 and 63** are straight renumbers of old Sections 44AA and 44AB. No thresholds, no obligations, and no deadlines have changed.
- **Form 26** replaces Forms 3CA, 3CB, and 3CD — a single unified tax audit report with four parts (A through D). This is the most significant procedural change in this unit.
- **Yes/No trigger architecture** in Form 26 reduces reporting burden by requiring detailed disclosures only where a provision is applicable.
- **Digital infrastructure disclosure is new** — accounting software, cloud services, ERP, server IPs, and audit trail status must now be reported. Build this into client data collection immediately.
- **Depreciation reporting simplified** — aligned with the codified WDV formula in Section 41.
- **GST reconciliation integrated** — the repeatedly deferred Clause 44 equivalent is now embedded in Form 26 in a simplified format.
- **Audit observations categorised** — free-text replaced by a structured classification system.
- **Rule 47 of IT Rules 2026** governs Form 26 (replacing old Rule 6G).
- **UDIN mandatory, FRN required** — no change from current practice.
- **Filing deadline unchanged** — one month before ITR due date (30 September or 31 October).
