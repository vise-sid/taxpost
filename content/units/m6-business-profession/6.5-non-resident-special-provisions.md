# Unit 6.5: Non-Resident Special Provisions (Sections 59-61)

## Overview

The 2025 Act consolidates all non-resident business taxation provisions into three sections (59 through 61), replacing a patchwork of five sections (44B, 44BB, 44BBA, 44BBB, 44C, and 44DA) that had accumulated over decades as Parliament added sector-specific presumptive regimes one at a time. Section 61 is the centrepiece: it merges four old presumptive sections into a single six-row table, adding two entirely new categories (cruise ship operations and electronics manufacturing) that had no equivalent in the 1961 Act. Section 59 carries forward the mandatory audit regime for non-residents earning royalty or fees for technical services through a permanent establishment, and Section 60 preserves the head office expense limitation. The consolidation is clean, but the new categories and the single-table format demand attention.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 44DA | **59** | Royalty / FTS income of non-resident with PE in India | Renumbered + restructured |
| 44C | **60** | Head office expenditure allocation | Renumbered only |
| 44B | **61 (Sl. 1)** | Shipping operations — presumptive income | Consolidated into table |
| *(New)* | **61 (Sl. 2)** | Cruise ship operations — presumptive income | **New provision** |
| 44BBA | **61 (Sl. 3)** | Aircraft operations — presumptive income | Consolidated into table |
| 44BBB | **61 (Sl. 4)** | Turnkey power projects — presumptive income | Consolidated into table |
| 44BB | **61 (Sl. 5)** | Mineral oil exploration services — presumptive income | Consolidated into table |
| *(New)* | **61 (Sl. 6)** | Electronics manufacturing — presumptive income | **New provision** |

### Structural Observation

Under the 1961 Act, each non-resident presumptive category sat in its own standalone section with its own set of conditions, provisos, and cross-references. A practitioner handling a non-resident shipping client referred to Section 44B; an aircraft operator required Section 44BBA; a turnkey power project contractor used Section 44BBB. The 2025 Act eliminates this scatter by housing all six categories in a single table within Section 61. The result is a uniform framework — one section, one table, one set of overarching rules — with category-specific rates assigned by serial number.

---

## Section 59: Royalty / Fees for Technical Services with PE (Old Section 44DA)

**Scope:** Non-residents and foreign companies earning royalty or fees for technical services (FTS) that are effectively connected with a permanent establishment (PE) in India.

**Key provisions:**

- Income is computed under the head "Profits and gains of business or profession" — not under "Income from other sources" and not at the concessional rate applicable to non-PE royalty/FTS under the Act or DTAA.
- **Mandatory audit** under Section 63 (old Section 44AB) — accounts must be audited and the report furnished by the prescribed due date.
- **Restricted deductions:** Only expenditure that is wholly and exclusively incurred for earning such royalty or FTS through the PE is deductible. Head office expenditure allocation is subject to the Section 60 limitation (see below).

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 44DA) | New Act (Section 59) |
|---|---|---|
| Chargeability | PGBP head | PGBP head — unchanged |
| Audit | Mandatory under 44AB | Mandatory under Section 63 |
| Deduction restriction | Expenditure "wholly and exclusively" for earning income through PE | Same — unchanged |
| Head office expenses | Subject to 44C limitation | Subject to Section 60 limitation |

**Practical note:** The substance is unchanged. Update cross-references in PE-based royalty/FTS computations from Section 44DA to Section 59.

---

## Section 60: Head Office Expenditure (Old Section 44C)

**Scope:** Non-residents and foreign companies carrying on business in India through a branch or PE.

The provision limits the deduction for head office (HO) expenditure allocated to the Indian branch. Only the lower of the following is deductible:

1. The amount of HO expenditure attributable to the Indian business (on a reasonable basis), or
2. 5% of the **adjusted total income** of the Indian branch

**No substantive change from old Section 44C.** The computation methodology, the 5% cap, and the definition of adjusted total income remain identical. This is a straight renumber.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 44C) | New Act (Section 60) |
|---|---|---|
| Cap | 5% of adjusted total income | 5% of adjusted total income — unchanged |
| Allocation basis | Reasonable basis | Reasonable basis — unchanged |
| Scope | NR / foreign company with Indian PE/branch | Same — unchanged |

## Section 61: Non-Resident Presumptive Taxation (Old Sections 44B, 44BB, 44BBA, 44BBB)

This is the consolidation that matters. Four old sections, each governing a specific non-resident activity, are merged into a single table. Two new categories are added. The table structure is as follows:

### Section 61 — Presumptive Income Table

| Sl. No. | Category | Old Section | Deemed Profit (% of Gross Receipts) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ship operations** — carriage of passengers, livestock, mail, or goods shipped at any Indian port | 44B | **7.5%** |
| 2 | **Cruise ship operations** — operation of cruise ships arriving at or departing from any Indian port | *New* | **20%** |
| 3 | **Aircraft operations** — carriage of passengers, livestock, mail, or goods from any Indian airport | 44BBA | **5%** |
| 4 | **Turnkey power projects** — civil construction, erection, testing, or commissioning of power projects under a turnkey contract | 44BBB | **10%** |
| 5 | **Mineral oil exploration services** — provision of services or facilities in connection with prospecting, extraction, or production of mineral oils | 44BB | **10%** |
| 6 | **Electronics manufacturing** — manufacture of electronic equipment, including assembly and testing | *New* | **25%** |

### The Two New Categories

**Sl. 2 — Cruise Ship Operations (20%):**

This is an entirely new category with no equivalent in the 1961 Act. Old Section 44B (shipping) covered "carriage of passengers, livestock, mail, or goods" but did not separately address cruise ship operations. The 2025 Act carves out cruise operations with a significantly higher deemed profit rate of 20% — nearly three times the 7.5% applicable to regular shipping. This reflects the high-margin nature of cruise tourism relative to freight shipping.

**Sl. 6 — Electronics Manufacturing (25%):**

Also entirely new. This provision targets non-resident entities engaged in manufacturing electronic equipment in India — a category that gained prominence with the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme attracting foreign electronics manufacturers to set up Indian operations. The 25% deemed profit rate is the highest in the table, reflecting the value-add embedded in electronics manufacturing.

### Lower Profit Claims — Sl. 4 and Sl. 5 Only

For **turnkey power projects (Sl. 4)** and **mineral oil exploration services (Sl. 5)** only, the assessee may claim that the actual profits are lower than the deemed percentage. To do so:

- The assessee must maintain books of account as prescribed under Section 62.
- The accounts must be **audited** under Section 63, and the audit report must be furnished by the prescribed due date.

This carve-out replicates the old provisions of Sections 44BBB and 44BB, which also permitted lower-profit claims with audit. The remaining four categories (Sl. 1, 2, 3, and 6) do not permit lower-profit claims — the deemed percentage is final.

| Category | Lower Profit Claim Permitted? | Audit Required for Lower Claim? |
|---|---|---|
| Sl. 1 — Ship operations | No | N/A |
| Sl. 2 — Cruise ships | No | N/A |
| Sl. 3 — Aircraft operations | No | N/A |
| Sl. 4 — Turnkey power projects | **Yes** | **Yes** — Section 62 books + Section 63 audit |
| Sl. 5 — Mineral oil services | **Yes** | **Yes** — Section 62 books + Section 63 audit |
| Sl. 6 — Electronics manufacturing | No | N/A |

### Complete Bar on Losses and Deductions

For all six categories, the following bar applies:

- **No loss** can be claimed from any of the businesses covered by Section 61.
- **No deduction** under Sections 44 through 58 (the general PGBP deduction provisions) is available against the deemed income.
- **No set-off** of any loss or allowance is permitted against the presumptive income computed under this section.

This is the standard presumptive taxation trade-off: simplified computation at a fixed percentage in exchange for a complete bar on deductions and losses. It replicates the bar that existed individually under old Sections 44B, 44BB, 44BBA, and 44BBB.

## Practical Impact

| Area | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Section references** | Replace 44B/44BB/44BBA/44BBB with Section 61 (specific serial number). Replace 44DA with Section 59. Replace 44C with Section 60. |
| **Cruise ship operators** | New category — non-resident cruise operators servicing Indian ports now face a 20% deemed profit. No lower-profit claim permitted. Assess DTAA interaction for specific treaty countries. |
| **Electronics manufacturers** | Non-resident electronics manufacturers with Indian operations — assess whether the 25% deemed profit under Sl. 6 applies or whether a PE-based computation under regular provisions would yield a lower effective rate. |
| **Turnkey power / mineral oil** | Lower-profit claims still available with audit — no change in substance. Update section references from 44BBB/44BB to Section 61, Sl. 4/Sl. 5. |
| **PE-based royalty/FTS** | Update 44DA to Section 59. Audit requirement continues under Section 63. Cross-reference head office expenses to Section 60 instead of 44C. |
| **Head office expenses** | Straight renumber from 44C to 60. No computational change. |

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Three sections** (59-61) replace six old sections (44B, 44BB, 44BBA, 44BBB, 44C, 44DA) — significant consolidation of non-resident business taxation.
- **Section 61** merges four presumptive regimes into a single six-row table with uniform rules.
- **Two new categories** — cruise ship operations at 20% and electronics manufacturing at 25% — have no equivalent in the 1961 Act.
- **Lower-profit claims** are permitted only for turnkey power projects (Sl. 4) and mineral oil services (Sl. 5), subject to books and audit. The remaining four categories are locked to their deemed percentages.
- **Complete bar** on losses and deductions against deemed income — applies uniformly across all six categories.
- **Section 59** (royalty/FTS with PE) and **Section 60** (head office expenses) are substantively unchanged — renumbered only.
- **Rates are unchanged** for the four carried-over categories (7.5%, 5%, 10%, 10%). The new categories (20%, 25%) are fresh additions.
