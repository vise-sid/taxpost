# Unit 6.4: Presumptive Taxation (Section 58)

## Overview

This is the most important unit in Module 6 — and arguably the one that will generate the most client queries starting April 1. The old Act spread presumptive taxation across three sections (44AD, 44ADA, 44AE). The 2025 Act consolidates all three into a single **Section 58** with a structured table containing 3 serial numbers. The thresholds and deemed profit rates are unchanged, but **three substantive changes** buried in the consolidation will alter how you advise eligible clients. Miss these, and you will get the audit trigger wrong, the loss set-off wrong, or the eligibility wrong.

---

## Old → New Mapping

| Old Section (1961 Act) | New Section (2025 Act) | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| 44AD (business) | **58** (Table — S.No. 1) | Presumptive income for eligible business | Consolidated + **substantive changes** |
| 44ADA (profession) | **58** (Table — S.No. 2) | Presumptive income for eligible profession | Consolidated + **substantive changes** |
| 44AE (transport) | **58** (Table — S.No. 3) | Presumptive income for goods carriage operators | Consolidated |

**Three sections became one — but this is not just renumbering. Read on.**

---

## The New Structure: Section 58 Table

Section 58 organizes presumptive taxation into a single table with three serial numbers:

| S.No. | Eligible Assessee | Nature of Business/Profession | Turnover/Receipts Threshold | Deemed Profit Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Resident individual, HUF, or partnership firm (not LLP) | Any eligible business | Up to Rs 2 crore (Rs 3 crore if cash receipts ≤ 5% of total) | **6%** of digital turnover + **8%** of cash turnover |
| 2 | Resident individual (or partnership firm engaged in specified profession) | Specified profession (legal, medical, engineering, architecture, accountancy, technical consultancy, interior decoration, or notified profession) | Up to Rs 50 lakh (Rs 75 lakh if cash receipts ≤ 5% of total) | **50%** of gross receipts |
| 3 | Any person owning goods carriages | Plying, hiring, or leasing goods carriages | No turnover threshold — per vehicle basis | Rs 1,000 per ton of gross vehicle weight per month (heavy); Rs 7,500 per month (other than heavy) |

### Thresholds — Unchanged

The enhanced thresholds (Rs 3 crore for business, Rs 75 lakh for profession) introduced by Finance Act 2023 are carried forward into Section 58. The 5%-cash-receipt condition for the enhanced threshold is also retained.

**Bottom line: Same thresholds, same rates, same eligible assessees. The table format is new — the numbers are not.**

---

## The Three Substantive Changes

### CHANGE 1: Assessee-Level Disqualification

**This is the change that will trip up the most people.**

Under the old Act, the eligibility test for Section 44AD was applied at the business level. An assessee could opt for presumptive taxation for one business while having other income sources (including commission, agency, or professional income) without disqualifying the presumptive election.

**Section 58 disqualifies at the assessee level.** If the assessee earns any income from:
- Commission or brokerage
- Agency business
- A profession specified in S.No. 2 of the Section 58 table

...the **entire assessee** is disqualified from presumptive taxation under S.No. 1 (business). Not just the commission/agency/profession income — all business income loses presumptive eligibility.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 44AD) | New Act (Section 58) |
|---|---|---|
| Disqualification scope | Business-level — specific types of business excluded | **Assessee-level** — any commission/agency/profession income disqualifies the assessee entirely |
| CA running a shop on the side | Could claim 44AD for the shop (it is a separate business) | **Cannot claim presumptive for the shop** — the CA has profession income, which disqualifies the assessee |
| Insurance agent with a kirana store | Could claim 44AD for the kirana store | **Cannot claim presumptive for the kirana store** — commission income disqualifies the assessee |

> **Watch out:** This will affect a large number of small assessees who have mixed income — a small business alongside commission or professional work. Review your client list. Anyone who was claiming Section 44AD for a side business while earning commission, agency, or professional income is now ineligible for presumptive taxation on any business.

### Practical Impact

- Identify all clients currently on presumptive taxation who also have commission, brokerage, agency, or professional income
- These clients must now maintain books of account for their business income and get audited if turnover exceeds the threshold
- The tax computation may change significantly — from deemed 6%/8% profit to actual profit/loss

---

### CHANGE 2: Broader Loss and Deduction Bar

**Old Section 44AD(2):** An assessee opting for presumptive taxation could not claim deductions under Sections 30 to 38 — i.e., the specific PGBP deductions (rent, repairs, depreciation, etc.). Other deductions (Chapter VI-A, for example) were unaffected.

**New Section 58(4):** The bar now covers **all deductions and all losses** allowable under the Act against the presumptive income. Not just Sections 30-38 — the bar is worded broadly to cover any deduction or any loss that might otherwise reduce the deemed income.

| Aspect | Old Act (Section 44AD(2)) | New Act (Section 58(4)) |
|---|---|---|
| Deductions barred | Sections 30-38 only (specific PGBP deductions) | **All deductions allowable under the Act** |
| Losses barred | Not explicitly barred (only Sections 30-38 deductions) | **All losses allowable under the Act** |
| Chapter VI-A deductions | Available against presumptive income | **Read the section carefully** — the bar is against the income computed under Section 58 |
| Brought-forward business loss | Could be set off against presumptive income (debatable, but many tribunals allowed it) | **Explicitly barred** by the broader language |

> **Watch out:** The exact scope of "all deductions and all losses" will generate litigation. Does it bar Chapter VI-A deductions (80C, 80D, etc.) against presumptive income? The conservative reading is that Section 58(4) bars deductions/losses against the *business income computation*, and Chapter VI-A deductions operate at the *total income* stage (post-aggregation). But the language is broader than before, and assessees who were setting off brought-forward losses against presumptive income will certainly lose that ability. Monitor CBDT clarifications and early tribunal decisions.

---

### CHANGE 3: Outcome-Based Audit Trigger

**This restructuring changes when the audit requirement kicks in.**

Under old Section 44AD(4)/(5), the audit trigger was tied to the 5-year continuity rule: if an assessee opted for presumptive taxation and then opted out before completing 5 successive years, they had to maintain books and get audited for the next 5 years. Declaring income below the deemed rate (6%/8%) also triggered audit.

**Section 58 reorganizes this into an outcome-based test.** Audit is triggered if EITHER:

1. **5-year continuity violation**: The assessee opts for presumptive taxation and then opts out before 5 successive assessment years — audit required for the remaining years of the 5-year period, OR
2. **Income below deemed rate**: The assessee declares total income below the deemed profit rate (6%/8% for business, 50% for profession) — audit required for that year

The two triggers now operate as distinct, independent conditions under the same section.

| Trigger | Old Act | New Act (Section 58) |
|---|---|---|
| Opt out before 5 years | Audit required for next 5 years | Audit required for remaining years of the 5-year block |
| Declare income below deemed rate | Audit required if total income exceeds basic exemption limit | Audit required — **no basic exemption limit condition** |
| Both triggers | Scattered across sub-sections | **Clearly delineated as two independent grounds** |

### Transport Operators — Audit Relief

For goods carriage operators (S.No. 3 in the table), the 5-year continuity rule and the audit trigger are **relaxed**. Transport operators can opt in and out without the same continuity requirement. This carries forward the position under old Section 44AE, which did not have the 5-year lock-in that 44AD imposed.

---

## What Stayed the Same

Despite the three substantive changes, the core framework is retained:

- **Eligible assessees**: Resident individual, HUF, partnership firm (not LLP) for business. Resident individual or partnership for profession.
- **Turnover thresholds**: Rs 2 crore (Rs 3 crore with ≤5% cash) for business. Rs 50 lakh (Rs 75 lakh with ≤5% cash) for profession.
- **Deemed profit rates**: 6% digital + 8% cash for business. 50% for profession.
- **Transport operator rates**: Rs 1,000/ton/month (heavy vehicle), Rs 7,500/month (other).
- **Advance tax**: Single installment by March 15 for presumptive assessees — retained.
- **No books of account required**: If income declared is at or above deemed rate — retained.
- **Partner remuneration/interest**: Deductible from presumptive income for firms — retained.

## Practical Impact — What to Do Now

| Action | Details |
|---|---|
| **Audit client lists for mixed income** | Any client on presumptive taxation with commission/brokerage/agency/profession income is now disqualified at the assessee level. Identify and transition these clients. |
| **Update loss set-off computations** | Clients with brought-forward business losses cannot set them off against presumptive income. Recompute multi-year tax plans. |
| **Review audit trigger logic** | The outcome-based test means declaring income below deemed rate triggers audit independently of the 5-year continuity rule. Advise clients to either stay above deemed rates or be prepared for audit. |
| **Section reference update** | Replace 44AD/44ADA/44AE with Section 58 (S.No. 1/2/3) in all returns, computation sheets, and advisory notes. |
| **Transport operator advisory** | No change in substance for transport operators. Audit relief continues. Update section reference only. |

> **Watch out:** The assessee-level disqualification (Change 1) will hit hardest in the first filing season. Many small assessees — insurance agents with side businesses, doctors with rental income from clinics treated as business income, CAs running training businesses — will discover they are ineligible only at the time of filing. Proactive client communication before March 31 is critical.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Three sections (44AD + 44ADA + 44AE) consolidated into one Section 58** with a 3-row table. Thresholds and rates are unchanged.
- **Change 1 — Assessee-level disqualification**: Any commission, agency, or profession income disqualifies the entire assessee from business presumptive taxation. This is no longer a business-level test.
- **Change 2 — Broader loss/deduction bar**: Section 58(4) bars all losses and deductions, not just Sections 30-38. Brought-forward loss set-off against presumptive income is gone.
- **Change 3 — Outcome-based audit trigger**: Either 5-year continuity violation or income below deemed rate triggers audit independently. No basic exemption limit escape hatch.
- **Transport operators**: Audit relief retained. No continuity lock-in. Substance unchanged.
- **Thresholds unchanged**: Rs 2Cr/3Cr (business), Rs 50L/75L (profession), 6%/8%/50% deemed rates.
- **This unit will generate the most client queries in Module 6.** Prepare your team before April 1.
