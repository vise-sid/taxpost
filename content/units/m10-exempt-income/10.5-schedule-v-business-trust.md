# Unit 10.5: Schedule V — Business Trust & Investment Fund

## Overview

Schedule V of the 2025 Act consolidates the exempt-income provisions relating to **business trusts** (REITs and InvITs), **investment funds** (Category I and II AIFs), **venture capital companies/funds**, and **sovereign wealth funds/pension funds**. Under the 1961 Act, these exemptions were scattered across various clauses of Section 10 — clauses 23D, 23DA, 23FB, 23FBA, 23FBC, 23FBD, 23FE, and 23FF. The 2025 Act gathers all eight entries into a single schedule, with cross-references to the newly numbered form provisions.

There are **no substantive changes** from the 2025 Act. The entries are a structural reorganisation — identical conditions, thresholds, and sunset windows have been carried forward. The only practical impact is the **renumbering of prescribed forms** used for claiming these exemptions.

## Key Entries — Detail

### S.No.1: Investment Fund — Non-Business Income (Pass-Through)

Income accruing to or received by an **investment fund** (Category I or Category II AIF registered with SEBI), other than income chargeable under the head "Profits and gains of business or profession," is exempt at the fund level. The income is instead taxed in the hands of the unit-holders on a pass-through basis under the corresponding charging provisions.

This is a straight lift of old clause 10(23FBB). The pass-through mechanism — under which the fund is a conduit and the unit-holder bears the tax liability — remains unchanged.

### S.No.3: Business Trust SPV — Interest and Dividend

Interest received by a **business trust** (REIT or InvIT) from a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) in which the trust holds a controlling interest, and dividends received by the trust from such SPV, are exempt at the trust level. These amounts are instead taxable in the hands of the unit-holders under the distributed-income provisions.

The SPV must be a company in which the business trust holds the controlling interest (directly or indirectly). The trust acts as a pass-through vehicle for these flows.

### S.No.4: REIT — Direct Rental Income

Where a REIT directly owns real estate (as opposed to holding it through an SPV), the **rental income** from such directly held property is exempt at the trust level. This income is taxed in the hands of unit-holders as distributed income.

This provision was introduced by the Finance Act 2020 (old clause 10(23FCA)) to accommodate REIT structures where the trust directly owns and lets out property rather than routing through an SPV. No change in the 2025 Act.

### S.No.6: Venture Capital Company / Venture Capital Fund

Income of a **Venture Capital Company (VCC)** or **Venture Capital Fund (VCF)** from investments in a **Venture Capital Undertaking (VCU)** is exempt. The VCU must be engaged in a business specified in the regulations made by SEBI.

The provision is a carryover of old clause 10(23FB). VCC/VCF structures pre-date the AIF regime and are grandfathered. New funds are typically registered as Category I AIFs under the investment fund pass-through regime (S.No.1).

### S.No.7: Sovereign Wealth Fund / Pension Fund — Specified Investments

This is the high-profile exemption for **sovereign wealth funds (SWFs)** and **pension funds** of foreign governments, provided they invest in specified Indian infrastructure and other notified investments.

**Key conditions (unchanged from old clause 10(23FE)):**

- The fund must be wholly owned and controlled by a foreign government (or its central bank).
- Investment must be made **on or after 1 April 2020 and before 1 April 2030** (the 10-year investment window).
- The fund must hold the investment for a **minimum period of 3 years**.
- The investment must be in specified categories: infrastructure debt funds, infrastructure investment trusts, domestic companies engaged in specified infrastructure activities, or other notified investments.
- The fund must not carry on any commercial activity in India.
- Earnings of the fund must be credited to the account of the foreign government or used for sovereign purposes.

> **Sunset Window:** The investment must be made between April 2020 and March 2030. Investments made outside this window do not qualify. The 3-year holding requirement applies separately — an investment made in March 2030 must be held until March 2033 to retain exempt status.

### S.No.8: Sovereign Wealth Fund / Pension Fund — Additional Investments

This extends the S.No.7 exemption to additional categories of income earned by qualifying SWFs and pension funds from specified investments. The conditions mirror S.No.7, including the investment window and holding period.

## Form Renumbering

The 2025 Act renumbers the prescribed forms used for claiming Schedule V exemptions:

| Old Form | New Form | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Form 64A | Form 76 | Statement of income distributed by business trust to unit-holders |
| Form 64E | Form 72 | Statement of income of investment fund — Category I/II AIF |
| Form 64F | Form 73 | Statement of income distributed by investment fund to unit-holders |
| Form 64 | Form 74 | Statement of income of VCC/VCF distributed to investors |

> **Practical Note:** The form content and filing obligations remain identical. Only the form numbers have changed. Software vendors and compliance teams must update form references in their systems for the tax year 2025-26 onward.

## Delta Summary: What Actually Changed

| Aspect | Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Genuine Change? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Substantive conditions | Various clauses of Section 10 | Schedule V, S.No.1-8 | **No** — reorganisation only |
| Pass-through mechanism for AIF | Section 10(23FBB) | Schedule V, S.No.1 | **No** |
| Business trust SPV exemption | Section 10(23FC), 10(23FCA) | Schedule V, S.No.3-4 | **No** |
| SWF/pension fund exemption | Section 10(23FE), 10(23FF) | Schedule V, S.No.7-8 | **No** |
| Investment window (SWF) | April 2020 - March 2030, 3-year hold | Same | **No** |
| VCC/VCF exemption | Section 10(23FB) | Schedule V, S.No.6 | **No** |
| Prescribed forms | 64, 64A, 64E, 64F | 74, 76, 72, 73 | **Form numbers only** |

**Bottom line:** Schedule V is a pure reorganisation exercise. No conditions, thresholds, or sunset dates have been altered. The only action item is updating form number references in compliance workflows.
