# Unit 10.6: Schedule VI — IFSC & Specific Non-Resident

## Overview

Schedule VI of the 2025 Act consolidates the exempt-income provisions applicable to **International Financial Services Centre (IFSC)** units and **specific non-resident** transactions. Under the 1961 Act, these exemptions were strewn across clauses 4D, 4E, 4F, 4G, 23BBE, 23BBF, 23D, and others within Section 10, supplemented by various sub-clauses introduced over successive Finance Acts to promote the GIFT City IFSC ecosystem. The 2025 Act gathers all twelve entries into Schedule VI, rationalising the reference structure without altering the underlying exemption conditions.

There are **no substantive changes** from the 2025 Act. The entries represent a structural reorganisation — the same conditions, sunset dates, and operational requirements carry forward. The practical impact is limited to **form renumbering** and the convenience of having all IFSC-related exemptions in a single, locatable schedule.

## Key Entries — Detail

### S.No.1: Capital Gains on IFSC Recognised Exchange

Capital gains arising to **any person** from the transfer of specified securities, listed derivatives, or units of specified mutual funds, where the transaction is carried out on a **recognised stock exchange located in an IFSC**, are exempt from tax.

"Specified securities" includes bonds, government securities, derivatives, and other instruments notified by the Central Government. The exemption applies to both residents and non-residents, provided the transaction occurs on the IFSC exchange. This is a straight carryover of old clause 10(4E).

### S.No.2: Non-Indian Securities — Non-Resident

Capital gains arising to a **non-resident** from the transfer of **non-Indian securities** (foreign currency denominated bonds, GDRs of foreign companies, and similar instruments) through an IFSC, are exempt. The securities must be denominated in foreign currency and the transaction must be settled in foreign currency.

This provision (old clause 10(4F)) is designed to make the IFSC a tax-neutral venue for non-residents trading in offshore instruments.

### S.No.5: Non-Resident Derivatives with OBU

Income arising to a **non-resident** from any transaction in **derivatives** entered into with an **Offshore Banking Unit (OBU)** in an IFSC is exempt. This covers over-the-counter derivative transactions — interest rate swaps, currency forwards, credit default swaps — entered into between a non-resident and an OBU.

The exemption (old clause 10(4D)) ensures that non-residents dealing in derivatives through IFSC-based OBUs face no Indian tax on such income, supporting the IFSC's role as a derivatives hub.

### S.No.6: Aircraft / Ship Lease Royalty — Operations by March 2030

Royalty or interest income earned by a **non-resident** from an **IFSC unit engaged in aircraft or ship leasing**, where the IFSC unit commences operations **on or before 31 March 2030**, is exempt.

This is a targeted exemption (old clause 10(23BBF)) introduced to attract global aircraft and ship leasing operations to the GIFT City IFSC. The sunset date of March 2030 for commencement of operations has been carried forward without change. The exemption covers both operating lease rentals (treated as royalty) and finance lease interest.

> **Sunset Date:** The IFSC leasing unit must commence operations on or before 31 March 2030. Operations commenced after that date do not qualify for this exemption. There is no sunset on the duration of the exemption once the unit has commenced operations within the window.

### S.No.7: Portfolio Management / Fund Management in IFSC

Income from **fund management** or **portfolio management** activities carried out in an IFSC is exempt. This supports the IFSC as a base for global fund managers, allowing fund management income earned through IFSC entities to remain tax-free.

This was originally introduced as a proviso to clause 10(4E) and has been elevated to a standalone entry in Schedule VI.

### S.No.8: Equity in IFSC Leasing Units — 10-Year Window

Capital gains arising from the transfer of **equity shares** in an IFSC unit engaged in **aircraft or ship leasing** are exempt, subject to a **10-year holding window**. The equity must be held in an IFSC unit that has commenced leasing operations within the prescribed period.

This provision (previously a proviso to clause 10(4F)) complements S.No.6 by exempting the exit gains on equity in IFSC leasing units, making the entire lifecycle — operational income and exit — tax-neutral for investors.

## Form Renumbering

| Old Form | New Form | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Form 10-IG | Form 68 | Application for exemption — specified fund in IFSC |
| Form 10-IH | Form 69 | Application for exemption — Category III AIF in IFSC |
| Form 10-IK | Form 70 | Application for exemption — specified activities in IFSC |
| Form 10-II | Form 178 | Report for IFSC unit — aircraft/ship leasing |

> **Practical Note:** The form content and filing requirements remain the same. Only the form numbers have changed. IFSC units and their advisors must update internal references for the tax year 2025-26 onward.

## Delta Summary: What Actually Changed

| Aspect | Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Genuine Change? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Substantive conditions | Various clauses of Section 10 | Schedule VI, S.No.1-12 | **No** — reorganisation only |
| IFSC exchange capital gains exemption | Section 10(4E) | Schedule VI, S.No.1 | **No** |
| Non-resident derivative exemption | Section 10(4D) | Schedule VI, S.No.5 | **No** |
| Aircraft/ship lease royalty sunset | March 2030 | March 2030 | **No** |
| IFSC leasing equity — 10-year window | Proviso to 10(4F) | Schedule VI, S.No.8 | **No** |
| Category III AIF in IFSC | Proviso to 10(23BBE) | Schedule VI, S.No.12 | **No** |
| Prescribed forms | 10-IG, 10-IH, 10-IK, 10-II | 68, 69, 70, 178 | **Form numbers only** |

**Bottom line:** Schedule VI is a structural reorganisation. The IFSC incentive framework — sunset dates, eligible activities, non-resident exemptions — remains entirely intact. The primary practical task is updating form references in IFSC compliance and advisory workflows.
