# Unit 10.4: Schedule IV — Non-Resident Exemptions

## Overview

Schedule IV contains **14 entries** covering income exempt exclusively for non-residents. These provisions were scattered across various sub-clauses of Section 10 in the 1961 Act — diplomatic exemptions, NRE interest, foreign enterprise employees, ship lease rentals, and crude oil storage income. The 2025 Act reorganizes them into a single schedule with no substantive changes. This is pure reorganization — the rules, limits, and conditions are carried forward as-is.

## What Stayed the Same — All of It

Schedule IV is a pure reorganization exercise. Every entry carries forward the identical substance from the corresponding Section 10 clause. No rates changed, no conditions modified, no scope expanded or narrowed.

### S.No. 1: NRE Account Interest

Old Section 10(4)(i) → Schedule IV, S.No. 1. Interest earned on NRE accounts by a person resident outside India remains fully exempt. The exemption ceases when the individual becomes a resident — FEMA requires conversion of NRE account to a resident account, and interest becomes taxable from the date of return.

### S.No. 2: Diplomatic Staff Remuneration

Old Section 10(6)(i) → Schedule IV, S.No. 2. Remuneration received by ambassadors, high commissioners, trade commissioners, and other diplomatic officials (subject to reciprocity) continues to be exempt. This tracks the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations.

### S.No. 3: Foreign Enterprise Employee — 90-Day Rule

Old Section 10(6)(vi) → Schedule IV, S.No. 3. Salary paid by a foreign enterprise to a non-resident individual for services in India is exempt if:
- The individual is present in India for not more than 90 days in the tax year
- The foreign enterprise is not engaged in any trade or business in India
- The salary is not deductible from the employer's Indian income

All three conditions are unchanged. The 90-day threshold, the "not engaged in trade or business in India" requirement, and the non-deductibility condition carry forward as-is.

### S.No. 4: Foreign Ship Salaries

Old Section 10(6)(viii) → Schedule IV, S.No. 4. Salary received by a non-resident for services rendered on a foreign ship, with aggregate stay in India not exceeding 90 days, remains exempt. The provision targets merchant navy personnel on foreign-flagged vessels making port calls in India.

### S.No. 9: Cruise Ship Lease Rentals

Old Section 10(48) → Schedule IV, S.No. 9. Income of a foreign company by way of lease rentals from an Indian company for ships used for cruise tourism, under a scheme notified by the Central Government, continues to be exempt. This was introduced to promote cruise tourism and remains unchanged.

### S.No. 10–13: Crude Oil / Strategic Petroleum Reserves

Old Sections 10(48A)/(48B)/(48C)/(48D) → Schedule IV, S.No. 10–13. These four entries exempt income of foreign companies involved in strategic petroleum reserve operations in India — covering sale of leftover crude oil, storage income, and extended storage agreements. Introduced to facilitate India's SPR programme (Visakhapatnam, Mangalore, Padur facilities), all four provisions carry forward without change.

## Practical Impact

### Citation Updates Only

Since no substance has changed, the only action item is updating references in documentation:

| Old Citation | New Citation |
|---|---|
| Section 10(4)(i) | Section 11, Schedule IV, S.No. 1 |
| Section 10(6)(vi) | Section 11, Schedule IV, S.No. 3 |
| Section 10(48) | Section 11, Schedule IV, S.No. 9 |

Any tax opinion, Form 10F filing guidance, or DTAA-related advisory referencing non-resident exemptions under Section 10 must switch to the Schedule IV citation format.

### No Software Logic Changes

Tax computation software does not need to modify any calculation logic for Schedule IV items. Only the section reference labels in the UI, reports, and certificates need updating.

### Watch Out

**Schedule IV vs. DTAA benefits.** Non-resident clients often claim exemptions under both domestic law (old Section 10 / new Schedule IV) and the applicable DTAA. The reorganization does not affect DTAA applicability — treaty benefits operate independently of domestic section numbering. However, when citing domestic law provisions in Form 10F or tax residency certificate documentation, use the new Schedule IV references from 1 April 2026.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Schedule IV has 14 entries — all non-resident-specific exemptions, reorganized from old Section 10 sub-clauses.**
- **No substantive changes whatsoever — this is pure renumbering and schedule reorganization.**
- **Key entries: NRE interest (S.No. 1), diplomatic staff (S.No. 2), foreign enterprise 90-day rule (S.No. 3), foreign ship salaries (S.No. 4), cruise ship lease (S.No. 9), SPR crude oil (S.No. 10–13).**
- **Only action: update section references in opinions, Form 10F guidance, and software labels.**
- **DTAA benefits unaffected — treaty provisions operate independently of domestic section renumbering.**
