# Unit 10.7: Schedule VII — Specified Entities + Schedule VIII — Political Parties

## Overview

Schedule VII and Schedule VIII of the 2025 Act represent two distinct but related reorganisations of entity-level tax exemption provisions. **Schedule VII** consolidates 48 entries covering entities whose income is wholly or partially exempt from tax. **Schedule VIII** carves out the special regime for political parties and electoral trusts into a dedicated two-entry schedule.

Schedule VII contains a **genuine conceptual shift** introduced by the 2025 Act. Under the 1961 Act, Section 10 exempted specific *income* of specific entities — the drafting focus was on the income stream. Under the 2025 Act, Schedule VII exempts specified *persons* from tax — the entire entity is treated as not chargeable to tax if conditions are met. This is more than cosmetic reorganisation: the new approach treats the entity itself as the exempt unit, rather than exempting individual income streams.

Schedule VIII (political parties and electoral trusts) is a structural reorganisation without substantive change, moving the old Section 13A provisions into a standalone schedule with cross-references to Section 12.

---

## Part A: Schedule VII — Specified Entities (48 Entries)

### The Conceptual Shift

Under the 1961 Act, entity-level exemptions were drafted as: *"The following income shall not be included in the total income — [income of entity X]."* The focus was on identifying the income stream and exempting it.

Under the 2025 Act, Schedule VII is drafted as: *"The following persons shall not be chargeable to tax — [Entity X, subject to conditions]."* The focus shifts to the person (entity) itself. If the entity meets the prescribed conditions, it is not a chargeable person.

**Why this matters:**

1. **Scope clarity.** Under the old approach, disputes could arise about whether a particular income stream of an otherwise-exempt entity fell within the specific exemption clause. Under the new approach, if the entity qualifies, its entire income is exempt — there is no need to match individual income streams to specific clauses.
2. **Compliance simplicity.** The entity either qualifies or it does not. The question is about entity-level status, not income-stream-level classification.
3. **Anti-abuse alignment.** Conditions for exemption (registration, activity restrictions, fund utilisation norms) are tied to the entity's status. Breach of conditions results in loss of exempt status for the entity as a whole, rather than for individual income items.

> **Practical Caution:** This conceptual shift does not change the ground-level outcome for most entities in the short term — the same entities that were exempt under the 1961 Act continue to be exempt under Schedule VII, subject to the same conditions. The shift becomes relevant in interpretive disputes and in the framing of assessment proceedings.

### Selected Entries — Detail

#### S.No.1: Armed Forces Welfare Fund

Income of any fund established by the Central Government for the **welfare of members of the armed forces** (Army, Navy, Air Force) and **ex-servicemen** is exempt. The fund must be established and administered by the Central Government. This is a carryover of old clause 10(23AAA).

#### S.No.10-16: PM Funds and National Funds

Income of the **Prime Minister's National Relief Fund** (S.No.10), **PM CARES Fund** (S.No.11), **National Defence Fund** (S.No.12), **National Foundation for Communal Harmony** (S.No.13), **Swachh Bharat Kosh** (S.No.14), **Clean Ganga Fund** (S.No.15), and **National Sports Development Fund** (S.No.16) is exempt. These are government-administered trust funds, and the exemption has existed since each fund's inception. No conditions apply beyond the identity of the fund itself.

#### S.No.17-19: Government Educational and Medical Institutions

Income of a **university or educational institution** existing solely for educational purposes and not for the purposes of profit (S.No.17), income of a **hospital or medical institution** existing solely for philanthropic purposes and not for profit (S.No.18), and income of a **research association** approved by the prescribed authority (S.No.19) are exempt.

The "solely for educational / medical purposes and not for profit" condition continues to be the key qualifying criterion. The interpretation of "not for profit" remains governed by existing case law, including the Supreme Court's rulings on surplus generation by educational institutions.

#### S.No.20-21: Mutual Funds

Income of a **mutual fund registered under SEBI** (S.No.20) is exempt. This ensures that the mutual fund vehicle itself is not taxed — taxation occurs at the unit-holder level (or at the point of distribution, as applicable). The entity-level exemption for mutual funds is unchanged in substance.

#### S.No.22-24: PF / Gratuity / Superannuation Funds

Income of a **statutory provident fund** (S.No.22), **recognised provident fund** (S.No.23), and **approved gratuity fund / approved superannuation fund** (S.No.24) is exempt. These funds must meet the conditions for recognition or approval under the applicable provisions (Employees' Provident Funds Act, Income Tax Rules for recognised PF, etc.).

#### S.No.33-40: Commodity Boards

Income of the **Tea Board, Coffee Board, Rubber Board, Cardamom Board, Coir Board, Tobacco Board, Marine Products Export Development Authority, and APEDA** is exempt. These are statutory bodies established under specific Acts of Parliament. Their exemption has existed since inception and is carried forward without change.

#### S.No.41: NPS Trust

Income of the **National Pension System Trust** established under the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority Act, 2013, is exempt. The NPS Trust administers the National Pension System and its income (investment management fees, administrative receipts) is not taxable.

#### S.No.47-48: Infrastructure Institutions

Income of the **National Housing Bank** (S.No.47) and income of an **Infrastructure Debt Fund** registered with SEBI or RBI (S.No.48) are exempt. The Infrastructure Debt Fund exemption ensures that the IDF vehicle — used to channel long-term debt into infrastructure projects — is not taxed at the entity level.

## Part B: Schedule VIII — Political Parties & Electoral Trusts (2 Entries)

Schedule VIII contains two entries, governing the tax treatment of **registered political parties** and **electoral trusts**.

### Old to New Mapping

| Entry | Old Provision | New Provision | Subject | Change Type |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Section 13A | Section 12 + Schedule VIII, Entry 1 | Income of registered political parties — exempt subject to conditions | Reorganised |
| 2 | Section 13A proviso / Section 10(23C)(ivb) | Section 12 + Schedule VIII, Entry 2 | Income of electoral trusts — exempt subject to distribution condition | Reorganised |

### Entry 1: Political Parties — Conditions for Exemption

The income of a **political party registered under Section 29A** of the Representation of the People Act, 1951, is exempt from tax, subject to the following conditions:

1. **Books of account.** The political party must maintain books of account and other documents as would enable the Assessing Officer to properly deduce the party's income.
2. **Record of contributions exceeding Rs 20,000.** The party must maintain a record of each voluntary contribution in excess of **Rs 20,000**, including the name and address of the person making the contribution.
3. **Audit.** The accounts of the political party must be audited by an accountant (as defined in the Explanation to Section 288 of the 1961 Act / corresponding provision of the 2025 Act).
4. **Donations exceeding Rs 2,000 — banking channel only.** No donation of an amount exceeding **Rs 2,000** shall be received otherwise than by an account payee cheque, an account payee bank draft, the use of an electronic clearing system through a bank account, or electoral bonds. Cash donations above Rs 2,000 are not permitted.
5. **Return of income by due date.** The political party must furnish a return of income in accordance with the provisions of the Act, on or before the due date.

> **All five conditions are cumulative.** Failure to satisfy any one condition results in loss of exempt status for the relevant tax year. The conditions are a direct carryover from old Section 13A — no thresholds or requirements have been altered.

### Entry 2: Electoral Trusts

An **electoral trust** approved by the Central Board of Direct Taxes and established in accordance with the Electoral Trusts Scheme, 2013, is exempt from tax, subject to the condition that the trust distributes **at least 95% of the aggregate donations received** during the financial year, along with any surplus from the preceding year, to **registered political parties**.

The 95% distribution requirement is the sole operative condition. If the electoral trust retains more than 5% of total donations received, the exemption is lost.

| Old Form | New Form | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Form 10BC | Form 181 | Report of audit of accounts of electoral trust |

## Delta Summary: What Actually Changed

| Aspect | Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Genuine Change? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Conceptual approach — Schedule VII** | Exempts specific *income* of specific entities | Exempts specified *persons* from tax | **Yes — conceptual shift** |
| Entity conditions and thresholds | Various clauses of Section 10 | Schedule VII, S.No.1-48 | **No** — same conditions carried forward |
| Number of exempt entity categories | ~48 scattered across Section 10 | 48 entries in Schedule VII | **No** — same universe of entities |
| Political party conditions | Section 13A | Section 12 + Schedule VIII, Entry 1 | **No** — same five conditions |
| Electoral trust — 95% distribution | Section 13A / 10(23C)(ivb) | Schedule VIII, Entry 2 | **No** |
| Rs 20,000 contribution recording threshold | Section 13A | Schedule VIII, Entry 1 | **No** |
| Rs 2,000 banking-channel threshold | Section 13A | Schedule VIII, Entry 1 | **No** |
| Audit report form | Form 10BC | Form 181 | **Form number only** |

**Bottom line:** Schedule VII introduces a genuine conceptual reframing — the exemption attaches to the *person* (entity) rather than to specific income streams. While the practical outcome for most entities is identical in the short term, this shift has interpretive significance for future disputes. Schedule VIII is a pure reorganisation of the political party and electoral trust provisions, with no substantive changes. The immediate action item is updating form references (10BC to 181) and recognising the entity-level framing of Schedule VII in advisory and compliance work.
