# Unit 10.2: Schedule II — General Exemptions

## Overview

Schedule II contains 17 entries covering exemptions available to all assessee categories — not restricted by residential status or entity type. These are the bread-and-butter exemptions that CAs encounter daily: agricultural income, life insurance proceeds, PF accumulations, NPS, Sukanya Samriddhi, scholarships, and gallantry awards. The substantive rules are almost entirely carried forward from old Section 10. The one genuine addition is **S.No. 16 — Unified Pension Scheme (UPS) lump sum**, introduced following the January 2025 UPS notification.

> PF interest threshold (Rs 2.5 lakh/5 lakh cap on tax-free interest) — carried forward from Finance Act 2021. Life insurance Rs 5 lakh aggregate premium cap — carried forward from Finance Act 2023. Agniveer Corpus Fund exemption (S.No. 7) — carried forward from Finance Act 2023. Equalisation levy refund (S.No. 15) — transitional entry, as the levy itself was repealed by Finance Act 2025. No further change by the 2025 Act on any of these.

## Genuine 2025 Act Change: S.No. 16 — Unified Pension Scheme (UPS)

### What Changed

The Unified Pension Scheme was notified by the Central Government in January 2025 as an alternative to the National Pension System for government employees. **S.No. 16 of Schedule II** exempts the lump sum amount received on superannuation under the UPS. This entry has no counterpart in the 1961 Act — it is a fresh insertion by the 2025 Act to accommodate the new pension scheme.

The exemption applies to the lump sum component payable at superannuation. It does not cover the monthly pension (which is taxable as salary/pension) or any voluntary withdrawal before superannuation.

### Practical Impact

For government-employee clients opting into the UPS, the lump sum received on retirement is exempt under Schedule II, S.No. 16. The monthly pension remains taxable. This mirrors the treatment of NPS — partial withdrawal/commutation may be exempt, ongoing pension is taxable. Ensure tax computation software includes this new entry in the exemption dropdown.

## Key Entries — What Stayed the Same

### S.No. 1: Agricultural Income

Old Section 10(1) → Schedule II, S.No. 1. No change in scope or definition. Agricultural income remains defined under **Section 2(1)** of the 2025 Act (old Section 2(1A)). The integration with non-agricultural income for rate purposes under the First Schedule continues unchanged.

### S.No. 2: Life Insurance Proceeds

Old Section 10(10D) → Schedule II, S.No. 2. The exemption on maturity/death proceeds continues with the same conditions. The Rs 5 lakh aggregate annual premium cap (above which maturity proceeds are taxable) was introduced by Finance Act 2023 — carried forward, no further change.

### S.No. 3–4: Provident Fund

Old Sections 10(11) and 10(12) → Schedule II, S.No. 3 and 4 respectively. Statutory PF and recognised PF accumulations exempt on the same basis. The interest taxability threshold (Rs 2.5 lakh for private employees / Rs 5 lakh for government employees, above which PF interest is taxable) was introduced by Finance Act 2021 — carried forward, no further change.

### S.No. 5: Sukanya Samriddhi Account

Old Section 10(12A)/(12B) → Schedule II, S.No. 5. EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) status continues. Interest, contributions (within limits), and maturity proceeds all remain exempt.

### S.No. 6: NPS — Employer Contribution

Old Section 10(12C) → Schedule II, S.No. 6. Employer's contribution to NPS Tier I account remains exempt, subject to the 14%/10% of salary cap (Central Government employees get 14%; others get 10%). The Rs 7.5 lakh aggregate cap on employer PF + NPS + superannuation contributions (Finance Act 2020) continues.

### S.No. 9: Scholarships

Old Section 10(16) → Schedule II, S.No. 9. Scholarships granted to meet the cost of education remain fully exempt. No ceiling, no conditions beyond the educational purpose.

### S.No. 17: Death-cum-Retirement Gratuity

Old Section 10(10) → Schedule II, S.No. 17. Gratuity exemption continues — Rs 25 lakh for non-government employees (CBDT Notification 2024 limit), full exemption for government employees. The calculation formula (15 days' salary per completed year of service) is unchanged.

---

## S.No. 15: Equalisation Levy — Transitional Entry

The equalisation levy was repealed by Finance Act 2025. S.No. 15 exists as a transitional provision to exempt any refund of equalisation levy received by assessees. Once all pending refunds are processed, this entry becomes functionally dead. No action required unless a client has a pending equalisation levy refund claim.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Schedule II has 17 entries — general exemptions available to all assessees, regardless of status or entity type.**
- **Only one genuine 2025 Act addition: S.No. 16 — Unified Pension Scheme lump sum exemption (per UPS notification, January 2025).**
- **All other entries are renumbered from old Section 10 clauses with no substantive change.**
- **PF interest cap (FA 2021), life insurance Rs 5L cap (FA 2023), Agniveer (FA 2023) — all carried forward. One-line acknowledgment, nothing new.**
- **S.No. 15 (equalisation levy refund) is transitional — the levy itself is repealed.**
- **Citation format: old "Section 10(10D)" becomes "Section 11, Schedule II, S.No. 2" — update all templates.**
