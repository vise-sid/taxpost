# Unit 10.1: The New Architecture (Old Section 10 → Section 11 + Schedules)

## Overview

Section 10 of the 1961 Act was one of the longest provisions in Indian tax law — 140+ clauses with 134 provisos and 90 Explanations, accumulated over six decades. The 2025 Act dismantles this entirely and replaces it with a clean, schedule-based architecture: **Section 11** as a gateway provision (5 sub-sections) referencing **Schedules II through VII** for exempt income, and **Section 12** for political parties (Schedule VIII). This is not renumbering — it is a genuine structural overhaul. Over 300 redundant provisions (Sections 10A, 10B, 10BA, 10BB, 10C) have been eliminated.

---

## The Structural Shift at a Glance

| Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Section 10 (140+ clauses) | **Section 11** (5 sub-sections) | Gateway to exempt income |
| Section 10 clauses (individual exemptions) | **Schedules II–VI** | Specific exempt incomes, by category |
| Section 10(23C) + related provisos | **Schedule VII** (48 entries) | Entity-level exemptions |
| Section 13A | **Section 12 + Schedule VIII** (2 entries) | Political party income |
| Sections 10A, 10B, 10BA, 10BB, 10C | **Not carried forward** | SEZ/export units — sunset-expired, eliminated |

---

## The Schedule Architecture

The 2025 Act distributes what was one monolithic section into six purpose-specific schedules, each in tabular format with serial numbers. No provisos, no Explanations. Conditions and limits sit in dedicated columns within the table.

| Schedule | Subject | Entries | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Schedule II** | General exemptions | 17 | Available to all assessee categories |
| **Schedule III** | Individual/HUF exemptions | 39 | Allowances, perquisites, receipts specific to individuals and HUFs |
| **Schedule IV** | Non-resident exemptions | 14 | Income exempt only for non-residents |
| **Schedule V** | Government/sovereign exemptions | 8 | Income of specified sovereign/international bodies |
| **Schedule VI** | Miscellaneous exemptions | 12 | Residuary exemptions not fitting other schedules |
| **Schedule VII** | Exempt entities | 48 | Entire income of specified persons not chargeable to tax |
| **Schedule VIII** | Political parties | 2 | Income of registered political parties and electoral trusts |

### Total: 140 entries across 7 schedules, replacing 140+ clauses with 134 provisos and 90 Explanations.

---

## What Genuinely Changed (2025 Act)

### 1. Proviso-Free, Table-Based Format

The 1961 Act's Section 10 was notorious for nested provisos — some clauses had 5-6 provisos and multiple Explanations. The 2025 Act eliminates every proviso. Each schedule entry is a table row with structured columns: S.No., description of income, conditions/limits, and applicable person. Conditions that were buried in provisos now sit in a dedicated column.

**This is not cosmetic.** For the first time, a CA can scan a schedule and identify all conditions applicable to an exemption in a single row, without cross-referencing provisos scattered across pages.

### 2. Section 11 as Gateway (Not a List)

Under the 1961 Act, Section 10 was both the gateway ("In computing the total income... the following shall not be included") and the exhaustive list of exemptions. The 2025 Act splits these roles:

- **Section 11(1)** — general exempt incomes (Schedules II–VI)
- **Section 11(2)** — income of exempt entities (Schedule VII — not chargeable on total income)
- **Section 11(3)** — conditions for entities receiving exempt income
- **Section 11(4)** — withdrawal of exemption provisions
- **Section 11(5)** — power to prescribe rules

The distinction between Section 11(1) and Section 11(2) is conceptually significant. Schedule VII provides entity-level exemption — the entire income of specified persons is not chargeable to tax. This differs from the old approach where Section 10(23C) exempted specific categories of income of specific entities. The 2025 Act's formulation is cleaner: the entity itself is exempt, not individual streams of its income.

### 3. Section 12 Carved Out for Political Parties

Political party exemptions (old Section 13A) are no longer mixed with other exempt income provisions. Section 12 is a standalone provision with Schedule VIII containing just 2 entries: registered political parties and electoral trusts. This architectural separation reflects the distinct compliance framework for political entities.

### 4. Elimination of Dead Provisions

Over 300 provisions have been removed — not because the 2025 Act repeals them, but because they had already sunset-expired or become functionally dead:

| Removed Provision | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Section 10A** — Free trade zone units | Sunset expired |
| **Section 10B** — 100% EOU profits | Sunset expired |
| **Section 10BA** — Handicraft exports | Sunset expired |
| **Section 10BB** — NABARD refinance | Obsolete |
| **Section 10C** — Relocation to SEZ | Sunset expired |

These provisions had accumulated amendments, transitional rules, and Explanations that bloated the 1961 Act without serving any current purpose. The 2025 Act simply does not re-enact them.

---

## What Stayed the Same

The substantive exemptions are overwhelmingly carried forward. Agricultural income remains exempt. Life insurance proceeds, PF accumulations, NPS withdrawals, NRE interest, HRA, LTC — all continue with the same limits and conditions. The 2025 Act did not use this reorganization as an opportunity to revise exemption policy. The change is architectural, not substantive (with limited exceptions covered in Units 10.2 and 10.3).

> PF interest threshold (Rs 2.5 lakh/5 lakh) — carried forward from Finance Act 2021. Life insurance Rs 5 lakh premium cap — carried forward from Finance Act 2023. Agniveer Corpus Fund exemption — carried forward from Finance Act 2023. No further change by the 2025 Act.

## Practical Impact

### Citation Format Changes

Every reference to a Section 10 clause must be updated. The new citation format is:

> **Section 11, Schedule [X], S.No. [Y]**

For example, agricultural income (old Section 10(1)) is now cited as **Section 11, Schedule II, S.No. 1**. HRA exemption (old Section 10(13A)) is now **Section 11, Schedule III, S.No. 11**.

### Opinions and Advisory Letters

Any opinion letter or tax advisory citing Section 10 exemptions needs updated references. A blanket find-and-replace will not work — each clause maps to a different schedule based on its category (general, individual/HUF, non-resident, etc.).

### Software and Templates

Tax computation templates, ITR utilities, and practice management software must be updated to reference the new schedule-based structure. The old "Section 10(X)" dropdowns become "Schedule II-VI, S.No. Y" selectors.

### Watch Out

**Schedule VII is not the same as old Section 10(23C).** Under the 1961 Act, Section 10(23C) exempted income of universities, hospitals, educational institutions, etc. — but the exemption was framed as exempting specific income. Schedule VII frames it as the entity's total income not being chargeable. If a Schedule VII entity has income that falls outside its stated purpose, the treatment under the 2025 Act may differ from old Section 10(23C) practice. Monitor CBDT circulars on this.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Section 10 (140+ clauses, 134 provisos, 90 Explanations) is replaced by Section 11 (5 sub-sections) + 6 schedules (II–VII) in tabular format — zero provisos.**
- **Section 12 + Schedule VIII carved out separately for political parties (old Section 13A).**
- **Schedule VII introduces entity-level exemption (total income not chargeable) — a conceptual shift from old Section 10(23C)'s income-level exemption.**
- **Over 300 redundant provisions (10A, 10B, 10BA, 10BB, 10C) not carried forward — all sunset-expired or obsolete.**
- **Schedule entry counts: II (17), III (39), IV (14), V (8), VI (12), VII (48), VIII (2) — 140 total entries.**
- **Substantive exemptions overwhelmingly unchanged — this is reorganization, not reform, with limited exceptions in Schedules II and III.**
- **Citation format changes from "Section 10(X)" to "Section 11, Schedule [X], S.No. [Y]" — every template, opinion, and software reference needs updating.**
