# Unit 10.3: Schedule III — Individual/HUF Exemptions

## Overview

Schedule III is the largest schedule under Section 11, with **39 entries** covering exemptions available exclusively to individuals and HUFs. This is where allowances, perquisites, and specific receipts land — HRA, LTC, leave encashment, special allowances, and more. Most entries are straight renumbering from old Section 10 clauses. However, the **Income Tax Rules 2026** (notified alongside the 2025 Act) introduce three genuine changes that affect daily practice: HRA metro-city expansion, children's education allowance increase, and hostel allowance increase.

## Genuine Changes: IT Rules 2026

The 2025 Act itself does not change the HRA formula or allowance limits — these are set by Rules. The **Income Tax Rules 2026**, notified alongside the Act, introduce three changes that take effect from 1 April 2026.

### 1. HRA — Metro City List Expanded (Rule 279)

**Old position (Rule 2A):** HRA exemption calculated at 50% of salary only for employees residing in Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, or Chennai. All other cities: 40%.

**New position (Rule 279):** The 50% tier now covers **8 cities**:

| 50% Cities (Old — 4) | 50% Cities (New — 8) |
|---|---|
| Mumbai | Mumbai |
| Delhi | Delhi |
| Kolkata | Kolkata |
| Chennai | Chennai |
| — | **Bengaluru** |
| — | **Pune** |
| — | **Hyderabad** |
| — | **Ahmedabad** |

**Practical impact:** Any employee residing in Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, or Ahmedabad whose HRA was previously computed at 40% of salary will now get 50%. For an employee with basic salary of Rs 10 lakh, this increases the potential HRA exemption ceiling by Rs 1 lakh (from Rs 4 lakh to Rs 5 lakh). Payroll systems must be updated to reflect the expanded city list.

**Watch out:** The rule references the municipal corporation limits. Employees in satellite cities (e.g., Pimpri-Chinchwad for Pune, Navi Mumbai for Mumbai) — verify whether the address falls within the notified municipal limits. The 2026 Rules may clarify this; monitor CBDT notifications.

### 2. Children's Education Allowance — Rs 100 → Rs 3,000/month/child (Rules 2026)

**Old position (Rule 2BB):** Exemption of Rs 100 per month per child for children's education allowance, for up to 2 children. This limit was set decades ago and had not been revised.

**New position (Rules 2026):** **Rs 3,000 per month per child**, up to 2 children. Annual exempt amount increases from Rs 2,400 to **Rs 72,000** for two children.

This is a 30x increase — the old Rs 100 limit was widely regarded as obsolete. The annual impact for an employee with two school-going children: Rs 72,000 exempt (vs Rs 2,400 earlier).

### 3. Hostel Expenditure Allowance — Rs 300 → Rs 9,000/month/child (Rules 2026)

**Old position (Rule 2BB):** Exemption of Rs 300 per month per child for hostel expenditure allowance, up to 2 children.

**New position (Rules 2026):** **Rs 9,000 per month per child**, up to 2 children. Annual exempt amount increases from Rs 7,200 to **Rs 2,16,000** for two children.

A 30x increase, paralleling the education allowance revision. Combined with the education allowance, the total annual exemption for two children in boarding school rises from Rs 9,600 to **Rs 2,88,000**.

## Key Entries — What Stayed the Same

### S.No. 1: HUF Member's Share

Old Section 10(2) → Schedule III, S.No. 1. Share of income received by a member from the HUF remains fully exempt (since the HUF is taxed on the income). No change.

### S.No. 2: Partner's Share of Profit

Old Section 10(2A) → Schedule III, S.No. 2. Share of profit received by a partner from a firm continues to be exempt in the partner's hands (taxed at firm level). No change.

### S.No. 6 / S.No. 8: Leave Travel Concession

Old Section 10(5) and 10(6)(ii) → Schedule III, S.No. 6 and S.No. 8 respectively. LTC exemption continues — value of travel for employee and family, domestic travel only, subject to the block-of-4-years rule. No change in substance.

### S.No. 10: Commutation of Pension

Old Section 10(10A) → Schedule III, S.No. 10. One-third exempt for non-government employees receiving gratuity, one-half if not receiving gratuity. Full exemption for government employees. No change.

### S.No. 14: Leave Encashment

Old Section 10(10AA) → Schedule III, S.No. 14. Leave encashment on retirement exempt up to Rs 25 lakh for non-government employees (CBDT Notification 2023 limit). Full exemption for government employees. No change.

### S.No. 22–39: Entity-Level Entries

Several entries in Schedule III cover institutional income (local authorities, research associations, trade unions, mutual funds, etc.). These have been placed in Schedule III rather than Schedule VII. The distinction: these entities have income exempt under specific conditions, but they are not wholly exempt entities. Their other income remains taxable.

## Practical Impact

### Payroll Systems — Immediate Updates Required

Three changes hit payroll directly:
1. **HRA computation** — update city master to include Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad at 50%
2. **Children's education allowance** — update monthly exempt limit from Rs 100 to Rs 3,000 per child
3. **Hostel allowance** — update monthly exempt limit from Rs 300 to Rs 9,000 per child

These take effect from 1 April 2026. April salary processing must reflect the revised limits.

### Client Advisory

For employees in the four newly added metro cities, the HRA revision is the most impactful. A client earning Rs 15 lakh basic salary and paying Rs 5 lakh annual rent in Bengaluru — the HRA exemption ceiling jumps from Rs 6 lakh (40%) to Rs 7.5 lakh (50%). Depending on actual rent paid and salary composition, this could reduce taxable income by up to Rs 1.5 lakh.

For clients with children in boarding school, the combined education + hostel exemption increase (from Rs 9,600 to Rs 2,88,000 for two children) is substantial — particularly for armed forces and transferable-job employees who routinely claim these allowances.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **Schedule III has 39 entries — the largest schedule, covering Individual/HUF exemptions (allowances, perquisites, specific receipts).**
- **Three genuine changes via IT Rules 2026: HRA 50% cities expanded from 4 to 8; children's education allowance Rs 100→Rs 3,000/month/child; hostel allowance Rs 300→Rs 9,000/month/child.**
- **HRA metro expansion adds Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad, and Ahmedabad to the 50% tier (Rule 279).**
- **Combined education + hostel exemption for two children rises from Rs 9,600 to Rs 2,88,000 per year — a 30x increase.**
- **All other entries are renumbered from old Section 10 clauses with no substantive change.**
- **Old Section 10(13A) → Schedule III, S.No. 11; old Section 10(14) → Schedule III, S.No. 12–13.**
- **Payroll systems must be updated before April 2026 salary processing for the three Rules changes.**
