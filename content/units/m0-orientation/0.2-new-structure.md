# Unit 0.2: The New Structure at a Glance

## The Before-and-After Snapshot

The Income Tax Act, 2025 is fundamentally a **structural overhaul** of the same law. Think of it as the same building, completely renovated — same rooms, but reorganized hallways, clearer signage, and no more maze-like extensions.

### The Numbers

| Metric | Old Act (1961) | New Act (2025) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sections | 819 | 536 | -35% (283 fewer) |
| Words | ~5.12 lakh | ~2.6 lakh | -49% |
| Chapters | 47 | 23 | -51% |
| Schedules | 14 | 16 | +2 |
| Provisos | ~1,200 | 0 | -100% |
| Explanations | ~900 | 0 (converted) | Restructured |
| Tables | Few | 39 | +39 new |
| Pages | 823 | 622 | -24% |

---

## The New Numbering System

### Death of Alphabetical Suffixes

Under the 1961 Act, as Parliament added new provisions, sections got alphabetical extensions:

```
Section 80C, 80CC, 80CCA, 80CCB, 80CCC, 80CCD, 80CCE, 80CCF, 80CCG, 80CCH
```

This made the Act look like someone had run out of numbers and started playing Scrabble. The deductions section alone (Chapter VI-A) had over 60 sub-variants of Section 80.

### Sequential Numbering in the 2025 Act

The new Act uses **clean, sequential numbers from 1 to 536**. No alphabetical suffixes. No nested sub-sub-sections like `80CCD(1B)(ii)`.

| Old Reference | New Reference | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| Section 80C | Section 123 | LIC, PF, ELSS deductions |
| Section 80CCD | Section 124 | NPS contributions |
| Section 80D | Section 126 | Health insurance premium |
| Section 80E | Section 129 | Education loan interest |
| Section 80G | Section 133 | Donations |
| Section 80GGC | Section 137 | Political party donations |
| Section 80TTA/80TTB | Section 153 | Savings bank interest |
| Section 80U | Section 154 | Self disability |

**Key insight**: The new section numbers bear **no mathematical relationship** to the old ones. Section 80C didn't become Section 80 — it became Section 123. You need to learn the new map from scratch.

## Chapters: 47 → 23

The old Act had 47 chapters, many of which existed because provisions had been added incrementally over decades. The new Act consolidates these into 23 logically organized chapters.

### How the Chapters Map

| New Chapter | Coverage | Old Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| I | Preliminary | Chapter I |
| II | Basis of Charge | Chapters II, III |
| III | Income Not Included in Total Income | Part of Chapter III |
| IV | Salaries | Chapter IV (Sec 15-17) |
| V | House Property | Chapter IV (Sec 22-27) |
| VI | Business/Profession | Chapter IV (Sec 28-44) |
| VII | Capital Gains | Chapter IV (Sec 45-55A) |
| VIII | Other Sources | Chapter IV (Sec 56-59) |
| IX | Exempt Income (Schedules II-VII) | Part of Chapter III (Sec 10) |
| X | Clubbing & Set-Off | Chapters V, VI |
| XI | Deductions (Chapter VI-A) | Chapter VI-A |
| XIX | TDS/TCS | Chapter XVII-B, XVII-BB |
| XXIII | Miscellaneous & Transition | Various |

The most impactful reorganization: **income heads** now have their own dedicated chapters (IV through VIII) instead of being crammed into one mega-chapter.

## Schedules: 14 → 16

The old Act had 14 schedules. The new Act has 16 — an **increase**, not a decrease. This is intentional: complex provisions that were buried in section text are now pulled out into schedules for clarity.

### The New Schedules

| Schedule | Contents |
|---|---|
| I | Conditions for business connection |
| II | General Exempt Income (agricultural income, LIC payouts, PF, scholarships) |
| III | Exempt Income of Eligible Persons (HUF shares, partnership profits, travel allowances) |
| IV | Exempt Income of Non-Residents (NRE interest, diplomat remuneration) |
| V | Business Trust & Investment Fund Exemptions |
| VI | IFSC Exemptions |
| VII | Persons Exempt from Tax (political parties, educational institutions) |
| VIII | Political Party & Electoral Trust Income |
| IX-XVI | Various (tea/coffee development, site restoration, recognized funds, depreciation, etc.) |

**The big change**: Old Section 10, which had **140+ exemption clauses** jammed into a single section, has been distributed across **Schedules II through VI** for exempt income, with **Schedules VII-VIII** covering exempt entities. Section 11 serves as the gateway provision referencing these schedules.

## Provisos: ~1,200 → Zero

This is perhaps the most dramatic structural change.

### What Were Provisos?

A proviso is a clause beginning with "Provided that..." — it creates an exception to the main section. Under the 1961 Act, instead of rewriting sections cleanly when exceptions were needed, Parliament simply bolted on provisos.

**Example of the old mess**:
```
Section 43B: Certain deductions only on actual payment
  Provided that...  [Exception 1]
  Provided further that...  [Exception to Exception 1]
  Provided also that...  [Exception 3]
  Provided further that...  [Exception to Exception 3]
```

Some sections had **10+ nested provisos**, each modifying behavior differently. Reading them required mentally tracking which proviso overrode which.

### What Happened to Them?

The 2025 Act **eliminates all provisos**:
- **Necessary exceptions** → converted into standalone **sub-sections** with clear numbering
- **Redundant exceptions** → removed entirely
- **Overlapping provisos** → merged into single, clear sub-sections

**Result**: Every exception is now a numbered sub-section, readable without mentally tracking a chain of "provided that" clauses.

---

## Explanations: ~900 → Converted to Sub-sections

### The Problem with Explanations

Under the old Act, "Explanations" were appended to sections to clarify terms or define scope. They were not part of the section text — they were annotations.

```
Section 56(2)(x) — Income from other sources
  Explanation 1: For the purposes of this clause...
  Explanation 2: For the purposes of this clause...
  Explanation 3: ...
```

The new Act converts all ~900 explanations into **numbered sub-sections** that are part of the main section text. No more hunting for explanations buried after the operative provisions.

---

## 39 New Tables

One of the most practically useful changes. Complex provisions that were described in prose are now presented in **tabular format**.

### Key Tables

| Area | What's in the Table |
|---|---|
| TDS rates (Section 393) | Three tables: Resident, Non-Resident, Any Person |
| Perquisite valuation | Motor car, accommodation, other benefits — structured rows |
| Presumptive taxation (Section 58) | Turnover thresholds, rates by business type |
| Capital gains tax rates | Short-term, long-term, special rates — all in one place |
| Depreciation | Asset classes, rates, conditions |

**Why this matters**: Under the old Act, determining the TDS rate on a specific payment required reading through 30+ sections. Under the new Act, you look up one table in Section 393.

## The First 10 Sections: What Stayed the Same

An important anchor for CAs: **Sections 1 through 9 are almost identical in numbering** between the old and new Acts.

| Section | Old Act | New Act | Same? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Short title, extent, commencement | Short title, extent, commencement | ✓ |
| 2 | Definitions | Definitions | ✓ |
| 3 | Previous Year | Tax Year | ≈ (renamed) |
| 4 | Charge of income tax | Charge of income tax | ✓ |
| 5 | Scope of total income | Scope of total income | ✓ |
| 6 | Residence in India | Residence in India | ✓ |
| 7 | Income deemed received | Income deemed received | ✓ |

After Section 9, the numbering diverges significantly. But the foundation — Sections 1-7 — provides familiar footing.

## Key Takeaways

- **536 sections** (down from 819) — 35% reduction, clean sequential numbering
- **23 chapters** (down from 47) — logical reorganization by income head
- **16 schedules** (up from 14) — complex provisions moved to schedules for clarity
- **Zero provisos** — all 1,200 converted to sub-sections or removed
- **Zero explanations** — all 900 converted to numbered sub-sections
- **39 new tables** — prose replaced with structured, scannable formats
- **Sections 1-7** are nearly identical in both Acts — a familiar starting point
- **No mathematical mapping** between old and new section numbers — learn the new map fresh
