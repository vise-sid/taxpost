# Unit 0.4: What's Gone

## The Clean-Up Exercise

The Income Tax Act, 2025 didn't just reorganize — it cleaned house. Over 63 years, the 1961 Act accumulated dead provisions, expired incentives, references to abolished taxes, and formally "[Omitted]" sections that still cluttered the statute. The 2025 Act removes all of this, creating a clean statute without ghosts.

Understanding what's gone is essential for two reasons:
1. **You need to stop looking for provisions that no longer exist** in their old form
2. **You need to know what was actually removed vs. what was just renumbered** — the distinction matters for client communication

---

## Category 1: Dead Letter Provisions

These are sections that technically existed in the 1961 Act but served no practical purpose — their sunset had expired, the underlying scheme had been discontinued, or the provision had been superseded years ago.

### Section 10A — Free Trade Zone Units

**What it was**: Special tax provision for newly established industrial undertakings in free trade zones, electronic hardware technology parks, and software technology parks.

**Why it was dead**: The deduction became unavailable from **Assessment Year 2012-13 onwards** — its sunset clause expired. Despite being a dead letter for over **12 years**, the section remained in the statute book, appearing in search results and occasionally confusing junior professionals.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Removed entirely. No equivalent exists.

### Section 10B — 100% Export Oriented Units

**What it was**: Tax incentive for 100% Export Oriented Units.

**Why it was dead**: Sunset clause expired. No new claims possible.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Removed entirely.

### Section 10BA — Export of Certain Articles/Things

**What it was**: Incentive for export of specified articles.

**Why it was dead**: Sunset clause expired.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Removed entirely.

### Section 10C — NE Region Industrial Undertakings

**What it was**: Incentive for industrial undertakings in the North-Eastern Region.

**Why it was dead**: Policy changes rendered it redundant.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Removed entirely.

### Section 10BB — Computer Programmes Definition

**What it was**: Definition of computer programmes for certain cases.

**Why it was dead**: No longer relevant in the current context.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Removed entirely.

### The "[Omitted]" Sections

Under the old Act, when a section was repealed by a subsequent Finance Act, the section number remained in the statute with an "[Omitted]" marker. Over 63 years, dozens of such placeholders accumulated — section numbers that existed on paper but contained nothing. The 2025 Act eliminates all such placeholders, using fresh sequential numbering without gaps.

---

## Category 2: References to Abolished Taxes

The 1961 Act contained vestiges of taxes that had been abolished years — sometimes decades — ago. These references persisted because no comprehensive clean-up had been undertaken.

| Abolished Tax | Year Abolished | References in 1961 Act |
|---|---|---|
| **Gift Tax** | Abolished decades ago | Cross-references in multiple sections |
| **Wealth Tax** | Abolished in **2015** | References persisted for 10 years |
| **Fringe Benefit Tax (FBT)** | Abolished in **2009** | References survived 16 years |
| **Banking Cash Transaction Tax (BCTT)** | Abolished years ago | Vestiges remained in the Act |

**Status in 2025 Act**: All references to abolished taxes have been removed. The Act now only references taxes that are actually in force.

---

## Category 3: Specific Provisions Omitted (Not Just Renumbered)

These provisions were deliberately dropped in the 2025 Act — they have no equivalent in the new law.

### Sections 206AB and 206CCA — Higher TDS/TCS for Non-Filers

**What they were**: Introduced in 2021, these sections imposed higher TDS (206AB) and TCS (206CCA) rates on persons who had not filed income tax returns for the preceding two years.

**Status in 2025 Act**: **Omitted**. These provisions have no equivalent in the new Act.

**Why this matters**: If you were advising clients on the higher TDS/TCS rates for non-filers, this compliance requirement no longer exists under the new Act. However, monitor CBDT notifications — similar provisions could be reintroduced via rules or notifications.

### Section 194N — TDS on Cash Withdrawals (Partial Removal)

**What it was**: TDS at 2% on cash withdrawals exceeding Rs 20 lakh (5% above Rs 1 crore) for non-filers.

**Status in 2025 Act**: Specific sub-provisions related to enhanced 5% TDS on withdrawals exceeding Rs 1 crore for non-filers have been removed.

### Section 47 Exceptions — Industrially Sick Companies & Demutualisation

**What they were**: Two specific exceptions in Section 47 (transactions not regarded as transfer for capital gains purposes):
1. Transfer of land of an industrially sick company managed by a worker's cooperative
2. Transfer of membership rights in a recognized stock exchange in connection with demutualisation/corporatisation

**Status in 2025 Act**: Both exceptions have been removed. Transfers of this nature are no longer specifically excluded from capital gains computation.

**Why this matters**: These are substantive changes, not just renumbering. CAs handling capital gains for such entities need to be aware that these safe harbors no longer exist.

## Category 4: Structural Eliminations

These aren't individual provisions but entire structural elements that were eliminated as a drafting philosophy change.

### All ~1,200 Provisos → Eliminated

| What They Were | What Happened |
|---|---|
| "Provided that..." clauses creating exceptions | Converted to numbered sub-sections if substantive |
| Nested proviso chains (10+ deep in some sections) | Unwound into clear, independently readable sub-sections |
| Redundant provisos restating obvious rules | Removed entirely |

**Example — Bad Debts (Section 36)**: Under the old Act, bad debt provisions in Section 36(1)(vii), (viia) and 36(2) contained **10 clauses, 6 provisos, and 4 explanations**. In the 2025 Act, these are consolidated into a single clean section with all provisos and explanations converted to sub-sections.

### All ~900 Explanations → Converted to Sub-sections

Explanations were paragraphs appended to sections that defined terms or clarified scope. Their legal status was ambiguous — courts debated whether they were substantive law or merely clarificatory.

**Status in 2025 Act**: All 900 explanations are now **numbered sub-sections** within their respective sections. No ambiguity about their binding nature.

## Category 5: Concepts That Changed Form

These provisions weren't removed — they were fundamentally restructured.

### "Previous Year" and "Assessment Year" → "Tax Year"

| Old Concept | Definition | New Concept |
|---|---|---|
| **Previous Year** (Section 3) | Financial year in which income was earned (April 1 - March 31) | **Tax Year** (Section 3) |
| **Assessment Year** | Year following the previous year, in which returns were filed and income assessed | **Eliminated entirely** |

**What changed**: The new Act uses a single concept — **Tax Year** — defined in Section 3 as the 12-month period from April 1 to March 31. Income is charged to tax under Section 4 for each Tax Year.

**Why it mattered**: The dual terminology was one of the most confusing aspects of Indian tax law:
- "AY 2025-26" referred to income earned in "PY 2024-25" (FY April 2024 - March 2025)
- Clients, banks, and even some professionals regularly mixed up the two
- Forms asked for "Assessment Year" when most people thought in terms of financial years

**Transition**: For years prior to April 1, 2026, old terminology continues to apply. Section 536 preserves continuity for pending matters.

### Section 10 (140+ Clauses) → Section 11 + Schedules II-VIII

The exemption mega-section was distributed:
- **Section 11** serves as the gateway/umbrella provision
- **Schedules II-VI** list exempt incomes by category
- **Schedules VII-VIII** cover exempt entities and political parties

The old Section 10's word count dropped from approximately **30,000 words to approximately 13,500 words** — a 55% reduction — through consolidation and removal of expired exemptions.

### 60+ TDS Sections → 3 Sections

| Old Structure | New Structure |
|---|---|
| Section 192 (salary) | Section 392 (salary) |
| Sections 193-196D (30+ individual sections) | Section 393 (3 tables: Resident, Non-Resident, Any Person) |
| Section 206C and related (TCS) | Section 394 (TCS) |

The rates, thresholds, and compliance requirements remain the same — only the organizational structure changed.

### Alphabetical Section Numbering → Sequential

| Old System | New System |
|---|---|
| 80C, 80CC, 80CCA, 80CCB, 80CCC, 80CCD, 80CCE, 80CCF, 80CCG, 80CCH | 123, 124, 125, 126... (sequential) |
| 194A, 194B, 194BB, 194C, 194D, 194DA... | Part of Section 393 |
| Section references like 80CCD(1B)(ii) | Simple sub-sections: 124(2), 124(3) |

---

## Category 6: What Did NOT Change

For clarity on what the new Act **did not remove**:

| Still Exists | Old Section | New Section |
|---|---|---|
| Section 80C deductions (LIC, PPF, ELSS) | 80C | 123 |
| Health insurance deduction | 80D | 126 |
| NPS employer contribution | 80CCD(2) | 124 |
| Education loan interest | 80E | 129 |
| Donations deduction | 80G | 133 |
| Tax audit requirement | 44AB | 63 |
| Presumptive taxation | 44AD/ADA/AE | 58 |
| Capital gains structure | 45-55A | 67-91 |
| Return filing | 139 | 263 |
| All tax rates and slabs | Finance Act schedules | Schedule I |

**The substance of the law remains the same.** No deductions were abolished (except the non-filer TDS provisions). No exemptions were removed (except sunset-expired ones). No tax rates were changed.

## What This Means for Your Practice

### Immediate Actions

1. **Stop referencing dead provisions**: If you were citing Section 10A, 10B, or 10BA in any context — stop. They don't exist.

2. **Update non-filer TDS tracking**: If you tracked clients for Section 206AB/206CCA compliance, that tracking is no longer needed under the new Act.

3. **Re-examine capital gains**: If you have clients with transactions involving industrially sick companies or stock exchange demutualisation — the old Section 47 exceptions no longer apply.

4. **Retrain your terminology**: "Assessment Year" is gone. Use "Tax Year" in all communications from April 1, 2026.

5. **Clean your reference materials**: Remove references to Wealth Tax, Gift Tax, FBT, and BCTT from any cross-reference documents.

### What Stays the Same

- All deductions (80C→123, 80D→126, etc.) — **same rules, new numbers**
- All tax rates — **unchanged**
- All income heads — **same structure, new chapters**
- Capital gains computation — **same methodology**
- TDS rates and thresholds — **same rates, organized in tables**

---

## Key Takeaways

- Dead letter provisions (10A, 10B, 10BA, 10C, 10BB) have been removed — they'd been non-functional for years
- References to abolished taxes (Wealth Tax, Gift Tax, FBT, BCTT) are finally cleaned out
- **Sections 206AB and 206CCA** (higher TDS/TCS for non-filers) have been **omitted** — a substantive change
- **Section 47 exceptions** for industrially sick companies and demutualisation removed — another substantive change
- All 1,200 provisos eliminated; all 900 explanations converted to sub-sections
- "Previous Year" and "Assessment Year" replaced by single "Tax Year" concept
- Section 10's 140+ clauses restructured into Schedules II-VIII (word count cut by 55%)
- The substance of the law (rates, deductions, exemptions) is **unchanged** — this is a structural clean-up, not a policy change
