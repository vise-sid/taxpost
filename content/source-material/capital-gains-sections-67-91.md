# Capital Gains — Sections 67-91 of Income Tax Act 2025

## Section 67 — Charge of Capital Gains
Profits or gains arising from transfer of a capital asset during a tax year are chargeable as "Capital Gains" and deemed income of the tax year in which transfer takes place.
Subject to exemptions in Sections 82-89.

## Key Definitions (from Section 2)

### Capital Asset — Section 2(22)
Property of any kind held by an assessee.
Excludes: stock-in-trade, raw materials, consumable stores, personal effects (except jewelry/valuable articles exceeding Rs 10 lakh).

### Transfer — Section 2(109)
Includes: sale, exchange, relinquishment of rights, extinguishment of rights, compulsory acquisition, conversion to stock-in-trade, maturity of zero coupon bonds.

### Short-Term Capital Asset — Section 2(101)
- General assets: held for NOT more than 24 months
- Financial assets (listed shares, equity mutual funds, UTI units, zero-coupon bonds): 12 months

### Long-Term Capital Asset — Section 2(67)
Capital asset that is NOT short-term.

## Computation
Capital Gain = Sale Consideration - Cost of Acquisition - Cost of Improvement - Transfer Expenses

## Capital Gains Tax Rates (Finance Act 2026)

| Asset Type | STCG Rate | LTCG Rate | LTCG Exemption |
|---|---|---|---|
| Listed Shares / Equity Mutual Funds | 20% | 12.5% | Rs 1,25,000/year |
| Immovable Property | Slab rates | 12.5% | — |
| Debt Mutual Funds | Slab rates | 12.5% | — |
| Other assets | Slab rates | 12.5% | — |

## Key Changes from Old Act
1. Holding period for STCG/LTCG distinction simplified
2. Virtual Digital Assets formally included as capital assets
3. Indexation benefit removed for most assets (Finance Act 2024 change carried forward)
4. LTCG rate unified at 12.5% across asset classes
5. STCG on listed equity increased from 15% to 20%
6. Annual LTCG exemption increased from Rs 1L to Rs 1.25L for listed equity
